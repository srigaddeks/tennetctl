## Tips for Getting the Best Answers

1. **Talk first, write second.** Open a voice recorder on your phone and answer each question out loud. Then transcribe the highlights. Your speaking voice is more authentic than your writing voice.

2. **Be specific.** "I felt bad" is weak. "I was 28, sitting at my desk in [company], and I couldn't focus after lunch for the third day in a row" is powerful.

3. **Include the messy parts.** The biryani you ate last night. The days you skip your own juice. The doubt about whether this will work. Mess is relatable. Perfection is suspicious.

4. **Name real things.** Real restaurants, real streets, real brands, real people (with permission). Specificity builds trust.

5. **Don't write for an audience.** Write for yourself first. The editing comes later — and will be done with your approval.

6. **Come back and add.** If you think of something at 2 AM, open this doc and add it. The best answers often come after you stop trying.

7. **Read your answers aloud.** If it sounds like a LinkedIn post, rewrite it. If it sounds like you talking to a friend, keep it.
