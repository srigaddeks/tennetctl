> Hand this document to the photographer before the shoot.

## 1. Brand Overview (for Photographer Context)

**Brand:** Soma Delights — cold-pressed juice subscription, micro-wellness brand, Hyderabad.

**Product:** 5-6 cold-pressed juice SKUs in 250-300ml glass/PET bottles, delivered fresh daily. Accompanied by educational booklets and ingredient cards.

**Customer:** Health-curious IT professionals and young families in Kukatpally/Miyapur/Chandanagar, Hyderabad.

**Brand Personality:** Calm, clean, trustworthy, knowledgeable, personal, premium but accessible. Think: the smart friend who knows about wellness and shares naturally, not the loud fitness influencer.

## 2. Design System — Visual Rules

### 2.1 The Greyscale Rule

**This is the single most important creative direction:**

- **Everything is greyscale** — backgrounds, surfaces, linens, props, ceramics, hands, environment
- **Product colors are the ONLY color** — the juice itself (green, orange, red, yellow, purple) provides all color in every frame
- **This means:** the juice bottles should POP against a muted, tonal world
- **Do NOT add colored props** (no colorful fruit bowls, no bright napkins, no warm wood tones that compete with juice color)

### 2.2 Color Palette

| Element | Color | Hex Reference |
|---------|-------|---------------|
| Primary background | White | #FFFFFF |
| Secondary background | Off-white / warm grey | #F5F3F0 |
| Tertiary background | Light grey | #E5E3E0 |
| Dark accents (text, labels) | Charcoal | #2C2C2C |
| Surfaces | White, off-white, light grey, concrete | Neutral tones only |
| Linens/textiles | White, cream, light grey | No color |
| Ceramics/glassware | White, clear, light grey | No color |
| **Juice (the ONLY color)** | Natural juice colors | Varies by SKU |

### 2.3 Lighting

- **Natural light preferred** — shoot near large windows, north-facing if possible
- **Soft, diffused** — no harsh shadows, no dramatic lighting
- **Direction:** side light or back light (creates depth without harshness)
- **Avoid:** direct overhead (flat), flash (artificial look), colored gels
- **Time of day:** morning light (7-10 AM) preferred for warmth without intensity
- **Exception:** Category C (process shots) may use moody early-morning light (4-5 AM kitchen with warm overhead lighting)

### 2.4 Mood & Style References

**Primary References:**
- **Aesop** (skincare brand) — minimal, tonal, product-as-hero, editorial quality
- **Kinfolk magazine** — calm, natural, considered, unhurried
- **Cereal magazine** — clean, lots of white space, editorial stillness

**Secondary References:**
- **Blue Bottle Coffee** — product simplicity, clean backgrounds, craft feeling
- **Dieux Skin** — modern, sharp, product-first with personality
- **The New York Times Cooking** — ingredient photography (for Category B shots)

**Anti-References (what we do NOT want):**
- Overly saturated, Instagram-filter look
- Dark moody food photography (we are light and clean)
- Busy backgrounds or excessive styling
- Stock photo aesthetic (generic, impersonal)
- Fitness/gym aesthetic (too aggressive for our brand)
- Tropical smoothie bowl look (too casual, too colorful)

### 2.5 Composition Principles

- **Negative space:** leave breathing room — aim for 40-60% empty space in frame
- **Minimal props:** 2-3 props maximum per frame (less is more)
- **Clean lines:** align elements to grid, avoid chaotic arrangements
- **Depth:** slight blur in background elements (f/2.8-f/4 for lifestyle, f/8-f/11 for flat-lays)
- **Asymmetry:** off-center compositions preferred over centered (rule of thirds)
- **Human elements:** hands visible occasionally (greyscale/desaturated in edit), never faces unless founder portraits

## 3. Shot Categories

### Category A: Product Shots (PRIORITY 1 — Shoot First)

**Purpose:** Website hero, product pages, Instagram grid, ads, packaging reference, pitch deck.

**Per SKU (5-6 SKUs), shoot the following:**

#### A1. Hero Shot (per SKU)
- Single bottle, front-facing, label clearly readable
- White background (#FFFFFF)
- Slight natural shadow beneath bottle (not dropped shadow — real shadow)
- Bottle centered or slightly off-center
- Full bottle visible including cap
- Orientation: portrait primary, square secondary

#### A2. Angle Shot (per SKU)
- Single bottle, 45-degree angle showing bottle shape and liquid color
- Same white or off-white background
- Shows dimensional quality of bottle and liquid clarity
- Orientation: portrait primary, square secondary

#### A3. Pour Shot (per SKU)
- Juice being poured from bottle into a clear glass
- Capture the stream of juice — freeze it (fast shutter speed: 1/500+)
- Show the color and texture of the juice (some juices are opaque, some translucent)
- White or light grey surface beneath
- Orientation: portrait primary (vertical pour works better in portrait)

#### A4. Lifestyle Context Shot (per SKU)
- Bottle on a simple morning scene: white surface, linen napkin, maybe a book or newspaper edge
- Natural morning light (side light)
- Bottle is the color hero — everything else is tonal/greyscale
- Should feel like "someone's beautiful morning" not "a styled shoot"
- Orientation: landscape primary (for web headers), square secondary

#### A5. Group Shot (all SKUs together)
- All bottles arranged together showing the full color spectrum
- Options: in a row (gradient from lightest to darkest), in a loose cluster, in a grid
- Shoot multiple arrangements — client will choose
- White background
- This shot should make the viewer say "those colors are beautiful"
- Orientation: landscape primary, square secondary

#### A6. Flat-Lay Product Grid
- Top-down view of 3-4 bottles arranged geometrically
- Each bottle is a different SKU/color
- Clean white surface
- Minimal props: maybe a linen corner, a glass rim
- Orientation: square (Instagram) primary

**Total Category A: approximately 30-35 shots (5-6 hero + 5-6 angle + 5-6 pour + 5-6 lifestyle + 3-4 group + 2-3 flat-lay)**

### Category B: Ingredient Shots (PRIORITY 1 — Shoot with Category A)

**Purpose:** Ingredient cards (print), website ingredient pages, Instagram educational posts, booklet illustrations.

**Core Ingredients (shoot all):**
1. Beetroot
2. Carrot
3. Ginger
4. Turmeric (raw root)
5. Spinach
6. Kale / leafy greens
7. Apple (green)
8. Lemon / lime
9. Cucumber
10. Amla (Indian gooseberry)
11. Celery
12. Pineapple
13. Pomegranate (whole + seeds)
14. Mint (fresh bunch)
15. Black pepper (whole peppercorns)

**Per Ingredient, shoot:**

#### B1. Whole Ingredient
- Single ingredient, uncut, on white/grey surface
- Natural form: beetroot with greens attached, ginger with its knobby shape, apple with stem
- Clean, editorial style — no clutter
- Orientation: square primary

#### B2. Cut / Prepared
- Ingredient sliced or broken to show interior color and texture
- Examples: beetroot halved (showing rings), pomegranate opened (showing seeds), ginger sliced (showing fibers)
- On white surface or light grey cutting board
- Orientation: square primary

#### B3. Close-Up / Macro
- Extreme close-up showing texture detail
- Examples: ginger root texture, spinach leaf veins, pomegranate seed moisture, turmeric's intense orange interior, peppercorn surface
- Shallow depth of field (f/2.8 or wider)
- These shots are for booklet and card illustrations — they need to be visually stunning at close range
- Orientation: square primary, portrait secondary

#### B4. Ingredient + Bottle Pairing
- One ingredient placed next to or behind its corresponding bottle
- Shows the connection: "this beetroot becomes this juice"
- Keep it simple — one ingredient, one bottle, white surface
- Orientation: square primary, landscape secondary

#### B5. Flat-Lay Ingredient Spread
- Multiple ingredients for ONE recipe arranged in a flat-lay
- Example: for a green juice — kale, cucumber, apple, lemon, ginger, mint arranged artfully from above
- White surface, minimal props (maybe a knife edge, a cutting board corner)
- This tells the story of "what goes into your bottle"
- One flat-lay per SKU
- Orientation: square primary

**Total Category B: approximately 50-60 shots (15 whole + 15 cut + 10 macro + 6 pairings + 6 flat-lays)**

### Category C: Process / Kitchen Shots (PRIORITY 2)

**Purpose:** Behind-the-scenes content, "how it's made" page, Instagram stories, trust-building (customers see the real process).

**Mood shift:** These shots can have slightly warmer, moodier lighting than product shots — the 4 AM kitchen has a specific atmosphere. Still clean, but allow the warmth of early morning. Think: documentary feel, not advertising.

#### C1. Ingredient Preparation
1. **Washing station** — ingredients being washed under running water in a clean sink. Hands visible, greyscale gloves if used.
2. **Chopping/prep** — action shot of knife cutting through a beetroot or apple on a white cutting board. Motion blur on knife hand acceptable.
3. **Prepped ingredients** — bowls of prepped ingredients ready for the juicer. Clean stainless steel or white bowls. Multiple colors of ingredients visible.

#### C2. Juicing Process
4. **Juicer in action** — juice flowing from the cold-press juicer. Capture the stream of juice. The juicer itself should be clean and photogenic (clean the juicer before the shoot).
5. **Juice collection** — fresh juice being collected in a glass or stainless steel container. Focus on the vibrant color against the metal/glass.

#### C3. Bottling and Labeling
6. **Bottling** — juice being poured into bottles through a funnel or directly. Clean pour, no spills.
7. **Labeling** — hands applying labels to bottles. Close-up of the label being smoothed onto the bottle.
8. **Quality check** — thermometer reading (cold-chain verification), or the moment of tasting/checking color against a reference.

#### C4. Packing and Dispatch
9. **Route packing** — bottles being placed into insulated delivery bags. Show the organization: each bag labeled for a delivery zone.
10. **Loaded bags** — delivery bags lined up, ready to go. The "ready for the day" shot.

#### C5. Early Morning Atmosphere
11. **4 AM kitchen scene** — the kitchen at the start of production. Clock showing early time (if available), overhead light on, darkness outside windows. Moody, cinematic.
12. **First light** — the moment morning light starts coming in through the kitchen window while production is underway. Transition from artificial to natural light.

#### C6. Cleanliness / Hygiene
13. **Clean workspace** — the kitchen BEFORE production starts. Spotless surfaces, organized tools. Communicates hygiene and care.
14. **Handwashing / prep routine** — brief shot of hygiene practices (gloves on, surfaces wiped).

**Total Category C: approximately 14-16 shots**

**Photographer Note:** These shots require access to the actual production kitchen during actual production (or a realistic recreation). Schedule these for a real 4 AM production session if possible — authentic lighting and atmosphere will be much better than recreation.

### Category D: Delivery & Experience Shots (PRIORITY 2)

**Purpose:** Customer-facing content showing what the experience feels like. Website "How It Works" section, Instagram content, pitch deck.

#### D1. Delivery Moment
1. **At the door** — insulated delivery bag or juice bottles placed neatly at an apartment door. Door handle/number visible (use a photogenic door). Morning light in the hallway.
2. **Delivery in progress** — shot from behind: delivery person (or founder) walking through an apartment complex corridor with delivery bag. Silhouette or partial figure. Not a posed portrait.
3. **Handoff** — moment of handing the bottle or bag to someone at the door. Two pairs of hands, no faces needed. Greyscale hands, colored juice bottle.

#### D2. Starter Kit Unboxing
4. **Closed box** — the starter kit box on a table/doorstep. Clean, minimal, inviting.
5. **Opening** — hands lifting the lid, first glimpse inside.
6. **Contents revealed** — box open, all contents visible: bottles, booklet, ingredient cards, welcome note. Arranged attractively.
7. **Booklet in hand** — someone holding the A5 booklet, partially open, reading.

#### D3. Customer Experience
8. **Morning drinking** — glass of juice held by hands (or on a table) with morning light. Person in frame but face not required — crop at neck or show from behind.
9. **Ingredient card collection** — several ingredient cards spread on a surface, as if someone is looking through their collection.
10. **Fridge shelf** — Soma bottles on a clean fridge shelf. Organized, appealing.

**Total Category D: approximately 10-12 shots**

### Category E: Founder Portraits (PRIORITY 2)

**Purpose:** Website "About" page, booklet founder note, social media, press/media kit, pitch deck.

**Note to photographer:** The founder is not a model. The goal is to capture genuine warmth, approachability, and passion — not a polished corporate headshot. Candid is better than posed. Action is better than standing still.

#### E1. In the Kitchen (Working)
1. **Production candid** — founder at the juicer or prep station, focused on the work. Not looking at camera. Captured mid-action.
2. **Ingredient inspection** — founder examining a beetroot or smelling fresh mint. Close enough to see expression. Natural, not staged.

#### E2. Professional / Clean
3. **Headshot** — clean background (white or light grey), natural light, relaxed expression. Looking at camera but not forced smile. This is the "official" photo for press and formal use.

#### E3. With Products
4. **Holding a bottle** — founder holding a juice bottle naturally (not product-placement style). Maybe mid-sentence, as if explaining something.

#### E4. Sourcing
5. **At the market** — founder at a vegetable market or with a supplier, examining produce. Authentic environment, selective focus on founder.

**Total Category E: approximately 5-7 shots**

**Wardrobe:** Plain t-shirt or shirt in white, black, or grey. No logos, no patterns, no bright colors. The person should be in the greyscale world — the juice is the only color.

---

### Category F: Lifestyle / Contextual (PRIORITY 3)

**Purpose:** Website headers, Instagram mood posts, seasonal content, pitch deck backgrounds.

#### F1. Morning Scenes
1. **Morning table** — juice bottle + glass on a breakfast table with minimal props: white plate, newspaper edge, maybe a phone face-down. Morning light through window. The feeling: "this is a beautiful, intentional morning."
2. **Reading with juice** — booklet or book open on a table, juice glass beside it. Focus on the juice and reading material. No person needed.

#### F2. Work Context
3. **Office desk** — juice bottle on a clean desk next to a laptop. The "this is part of my workday" shot. Neutral desk, minimal items.
4. **Meeting room table** — juice bottle on a conference table. Suggests wellness is for professionals too.

#### F3. Active Context
5. **Post-workout** — juice bottle next to a gym bag or yoga mat. Not a gym bro shot — clean, minimal, suggesting that juice is part of an active routine.

#### F4. Home Context
6. **Family morning** — two or three juice bottles on a kitchen counter. Suggestion of family without showing faces (hands, silhouettes).
7. **Weekend relaxed** — juice + booklet on a balcony or sofa. Relaxed, weekend morning feeling.

#### F5. Seasonal / Textural
8. **Ingredient texture board** — a mood board style arrangement of 3-4 ingredients with their corresponding bottle. More artistic/editorial than the Category B shots.

**Total Category F: approximately 8-10 shots**

**Note:** Category F shots can be done by the founder using a good smartphone (iPhone 14+, Pixel 7+) if budget is tight. The key is: good natural light, clean/minimal setup, greyscale everything except juice. The photographer should provide guidelines for phone photography if this category is deferred.

## 4. Technical Specifications

### 4.1 Camera Requirements

- Full-frame or APS-C mirrorless camera (Sony A7 series, Canon R series, Nikon Z series, Fuji X-T series)
- Primary lens: 50mm f/1.8 or 35mm f/1.4 (lifestyle and product)
- Secondary lens: 85mm or 90mm macro (close-ups and ingredient detail)
- Wide lens: 24mm or 28mm (kitchen environment shots)
- Tripod for flat-lays and product shots (consistency)

### 4.2 File Delivery

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|--------------|
| Primary format | RAW (.ARW, .CR3, .NEF) + edited high-res JPEG |
| JPEG quality | Maximum quality, no compression artifacts |
| Resolution | Minimum 4000px on longest edge (for print and web crop flexibility) |
| Color space | sRGB for web delivery, Adobe RGB for print delivery |
| File naming | Category_SKU_ShotType_Orientation_Number (e.g., A_Beetroot_Hero_Portrait_01.jpg) |
| Delivery method | Google Drive shared folder, organized by category |

### 4.3 Orientation Requirements

Every shot should be captured in multiple orientations where composition allows:

| Orientation | Aspect Ratio | Primary Use |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Landscape | 16:9 or 3:2 | Website hero banners, blog headers |
| Square | 1:1 | Instagram grid posts |
| Portrait | 4:5 or 9:16 | Instagram stories, mobile web, print |

**Priority:** Square and portrait are highest priority (mobile-first audience). Landscape for web headers.

### 4.4 Post-Processing Guidelines

| Guideline | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Overall tone | Clean, slightly cool, not warm or yellow |
| Saturation | **Desaturate backgrounds** (everything except juice). Product colors should be accurate to life — not over-saturated, not muted |
| White balance | Consistent across all shots. Calibrate at start of shoot. Slightly cool (5500-6000K) |
| Skin tones | If hands or founder visible: natural, not orange or grey |
| Shadows | Lift shadows slightly (not crushed). Clean shadow detail |
| Highlights | Preserve highlight detail (no blown whites on products) |
| Retouching | Remove dust, scratches on surfaces. Do NOT retouch food/ingredients to look unrealistic |
| Cropping | Deliver both original composition and suggested crop for key orientations |
| Filters | NO Instagram-style filters, NO presets that alter product colors |

### 4.5 Consistency Requirements

- All Category A product shots must have identical lighting, background, and color treatment (so they look like a set on the website)
- All Category B ingredient shots must have consistent surface and lighting (so they work as a series for ingredient cards)
- White balance and exposure should be matched across the full shoot
- If shooting across two days, use a color checker card for consistency

## 7. Finding the Right Photographer in Hyderabad

### 7.1 Search Strategy

| Method | Where to Look | What to Search |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| Instagram | Search and explore | #hyderabadfoodphotographer, #hyderabadproductphotography, #foodphotographerhyderabad |
| UrbanCompany | App/website | "Product Photography" or "Food Photography" in Hyderabad |
| Google | Search | "food photographer Hyderabad portfolio" |
| Food blogger referrals | Instagram DM | Ask Hyderabad food bloggers who shoots their content |
| Photography communities | Facebook groups | "Hyderabad Photographers", "India Food Photography" |
| Canvera | Website | Search Hyderabad photographers with food/product portfolio |

### 7.2 Portfolio Evaluation Checklist

When reviewing a photographer's portfolio, check:

- [ ] **Food/product photography specifically** — wedding photographers and food photographers are completely different specialties
- [ ] **Clean, minimal styling** — not busy, not dark, not over-styled
- [ ] **Color accuracy** — do the food colors look natural or over-processed?
- [ ] **Lighting quality** — soft, natural, well-controlled?
- [ ] **Consistency** — do shots in a series look like they belong together?
- [ ] **Ingredient/produce shots** — have they shot raw ingredients before? (Not just plated food)
- [ ] **Bottle/product shots** — have they shot beverages or bottled products? (Glass/liquid photography has its own challenges)
- [ ] **Editorial style capability** — can they do clean/minimal, not just restaurant/menu style?

### 7.3 Questions to Ask Before Booking

1. Can I see 3-4 examples of minimal/clean food product photography you've done?
2. Have you shot beverages or bottled products before? (Reflections and liquid clarity are tricky)
3. Do you have access to a natural-light studio, or do we need to arrange one?
4. What is your turnaround time for edited photos?
5. Do you deliver RAW files? (Some photographers only deliver edited JPEGs — we want both)
6. Can you shoot in multiple orientations (portrait, landscape, square) for the same setup?
7. What is your rate for a half-day (4-5 hours)? Full-day? Does post-processing cost extra?
8. Can we do a 30-minute test shoot with 2-3 products before committing to a full shoot? (Worth paying Rs 2,000-3,000 for this — confirms compatibility)
9. Are you comfortable with a detailed brief and specific creative direction? (Some photographers want full creative freedom — we need someone who can execute a specific vision)

### 7.4 Red Flags

- Portfolio is all weddings and events, no food/product work
- Heavily filtered, oversaturated editing style
- Cannot provide RAW files
- Has not shot beverages before (liquid and glass are technically demanding)
- Pushes back on detailed creative direction (we need execution, not reinvention)
- No examples of clean/minimal/editorial style
- Turnaround longer than 10 days

## 9. Smartphone Photography Guide (for Ongoing Content)

Between professional shoots, the founder will need to create content daily. These guidelines ensure phone photos stay on-brand.

### 9.1 Phone Settings

- Shoot in the highest resolution available
- Turn OFF HDR (it over-processes and adds unrealistic saturation)
- Turn OFF portrait mode for product shots (the fake blur looks artificial)
- Use the 1x lens (not ultra-wide, not zoomed)
- Grid overlay ON (for composition alignment)

### 9.2 The 5-Second Setup

Before EVERY photo:
1. **Clean the lens** (fingerprints ruin clarity)
2. **Find natural light** (face the window, product between you and window)
3. **Clear the background** (remove clutter, use a white surface)
4. **Tap to focus** on the product (ensure it's sharp)
5. **Hold steady** and take 3-4 shots (pick the best later)

### 9.3 Phone Editing (Quick Workflow)

App: Snapseed (free, powerful, available on Android and iOS)

Steps:
1. Straighten and crop (square for Instagram, 4:5 for stories)
2. Reduce saturation of background colors by 20-30% (selective tool)
3. Slightly increase saturation of juice color by 5-10% (selective tool)
4. Increase brightness slightly if needed
5. Do NOT use filters
