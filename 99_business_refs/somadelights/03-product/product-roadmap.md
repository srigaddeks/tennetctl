# Soma Delights — Product Roadmap

## Roadmap Philosophy

Every stage in this roadmap follows three rules:

1. **Triggered by customer count, not calendar.** We expand when we've earned the right to, not when we feel like it.
2. **Each stage reuses existing infrastructure.** Same delivery routes, same customer relationships, same brand.
3. **Each stage deepens the wellness habit, not just the product catalog.** More products is not better. Better daily wellness is better.

## Stage 1: Cold-Pressed Drinks (Launch)

### Why This Is the Right Entry Point

| Reason | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Customer familiarity** | Cold-pressed juice is understood in urban India. No education needed on the concept — only on the habit. |
| **Home kitchen feasible** | A cold press machine + refrigerator + prep station fits in a home kitchen. No commercial kitchen needed until 100+ daily orders. |
| **Daily consumption natural** | "A fresh juice every morning" feels intuitive. Not a hard sell. |
| **Subscription-compatible** | Daily delivery of a perishable product creates natural subscription behavior. |
| **Short shelf life = trust signal** | 24–48 hour shelf life proves freshness. Customers can taste the difference from packaged alternatives. |
| **Clear unit economics** | Ingredients are affordable. Margins are healthy (50–60%). Revenue per customer is predictable. |
| **Habit formation vehicle** | The daily delivery creates the ritual. The product is the medium for building the habit. |

### Role in the Brand

Cold-pressed drinks are NOT the brand. They are the **entry point** to the brand. Their role:
- Establish the daily delivery habit
- Build trust through consistency and transparency
- Create the education relationship (daily tips, ingredient cards)
- Generate the customer base that unlocks later stages
- Prove unit economics and operational capability

### Best Subcategories for Launch

| Subcategory | Examples | Habit Potential | Operational Feasibility | Recommendation |
|-------------|---------|----------------|------------------------|----------------|
| **Green/detox blends** | Spinach-apple-ginger-lemon, kale-cucumber-mint | Very high (daily staple) | High (ingredients available year-round) | **Launch core** |
| **Root vegetable blends** | ABC (apple-beet-carrot), beetroot-amla | Very high (daily staple) | High (stable supply, year-round) | **Launch core** |
| **Anti-inflammatory** | Turmeric-ginger-lemon-pepper | High (daily ritual) | High (shelf-stable ingredients) | **Launch core** |
| **Fruit-forward** | Mango-carrot-orange, pineapple-mint-coconut | Medium (taste-driven, less habit-sticky) | Medium (seasonal fruit, higher cost) | **1–2 SKUs for trial/family** |
| **Hydration** | Watermelon-cucumber-lime-mint | Medium (seasonal) | Medium (watermelon seasonal, summer only) | **Seasonal addition** |
| **Nut milks** | Almond milk, cashew milk blends | Medium | Low (soaking time, different equipment) | **Defer to Stage 4** |
| **Smoothies** | Banana-based, protein-added | Low (meal replacement, not micro-wellness) | Medium (banana oxidation, thicker texture) | **Defer** |

### Habit Formation Mechanics

| Mechanism | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Trigger** | Morning delivery notification (WhatsApp message: "Your wellness is at the door") |
| **Routine** | Open door → pick up bottle → drink within 30 seconds → read ingredient card |
| **Immediate reward** | Fresh taste, energy, hydration |
| **Variable reward** | Different ingredient card each day (collectible), daily wellness tip varies |
| **Investment** | Subscription commitment, knowledge accumulation, referral credits building |
| **Social reinforcement** | Neighbor sees delivery, family sees ritual, colleague sees bottle at work |

### Operational Feasibility at Home Kitchen Scale

| Operation | Requirement | Home Kitchen Solution | Daily Capacity |
|-----------|------------|----------------------|----------------|
| **Cold pressing** | Commercial cold press machine | Kuvings or Hurom commercial (Rs 25,000–40,000) | 50–80 bottles/day |
| **Ingredient storage** | Refrigeration for 2–3 days of produce | Dedicated refrigerator (Rs 15,000–20,000) | 3 days of ingredients |
| **Prep station** | Washing, cutting, portioning | Kitchen counter + cutting boards + colander | 50–80 bottles prep in 2–3 hours |
| **Bottling** | Food-grade bottles, filling, capping, labeling | Manual filling station, pre-labeled bottles | 50–80 bottles in 1 hour |
| **Quality control** | Taste, color, consistency check | Founder checks every batch | Every batch |
| **Storage (finished)** | Refrigerated storage overnight | Same refrigerator, separate shelf | 80 bottles |
| **Delivery staging** | Insulated bags, ice packs, route sheets | Dining table staging area, printed route list | 60 minutes to stage 50 deliveries |

**Capacity ceiling for home kitchen: 80 bottles/day.** At 80 daily subscribers, transition to dedicated kitchen space (not commercial kitchen — a rented 1BHK used exclusively for production). Cost: Rs 10,000–15,000/month rent.

### Shelf Life Management

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Target shelf life** | 48 hours from pressing |
| **Optimal consumption** | Within 24 hours |
| **Storage temp** | 4–8 C continuously |
| **Delivery cold chain** | Insulated bag + gel ice pack = 3–4 hours at ambient temp |
| **Customer instruction** | "Refrigerate immediately. Best before [date/time]." on label |
| **Batch timing** | Press at 3–5 AM, bottle by 5 AM, deliver by 7:30 AM = customer receives 2–4 hour old product |
| **Waste risk** | If delivery fails (customer not home), product is wasted. Mitigation: confirm delivery via WhatsApp night before. |

### Unit Economics (Stage 1)

**Per bottle (300ml):**

| Line Item | Cost (INR) | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Ingredients | 25–35 | Varies by recipe. Green blends cheapest, fruit-forward most expensive. |
| Bottle + cap | 7–9 | PET food-grade, 300ml |
| Label | 3–4 | Waterproof, custom print |
| Ingredient card | 1–2 | Offset printed, batch of 1,000 |
| Ice pack (amortized) | 2–3 | Reusable gel packs, replaced monthly |
| **Total COGS** | **38–53** | |
| **Selling price** | **120–180** | Depends on recipe/size |
| **Gross margin** | **67–142** | **56–79%** |

**Per subscriber (daily, monthly):**

| Metric | Conservative | Optimistic |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Daily revenue | Rs 130 | Rs 170 |
| Daily COGS | Rs 50 | Rs 45 |
| Daily delivery cost (amortized) | Rs 18 | Rs 12 |
| Daily gross profit | Rs 62 | Rs 113 |
| Monthly revenue | Rs 3,900 | Rs 5,100 |
| Monthly gross profit | Rs 1,860 | Rs 3,390 |
| Gross margin | 48% | 66% |

### Margin Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Ingredient price spikes (monsoon, summer) | Medium | Medium | Maintain 2–3 recipe options. Rotate to cost-efficient recipes when ingredient prices spike. |
| Delivery cost increase (fuel, labor) | Medium | Medium | Density focus. As subscribers per route increase, per-unit delivery cost decreases. |
| Spoilage/waste | Medium | Low-Medium | Confirm orders night before. No over-production. Waste goes to pulp products (Stage 2). |
| Customer requests larger bottles (lower margin) | Low | Low | Offer 300ml standard. Premium 500ml at proportionally higher price. |
| Bottle/packaging cost increase | Low | Low | Bulk ordering. Lock in prices with supplier for 3 months. |

### Quality Control Requirements

| Check | Frequency | Method | Fail Action |
|-------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Ingredient freshness | Every procurement | Visual + smell check | Reject batch, source backup |
| Wash and sanitize | Every batch | 3-step wash (water → vinegar → water) | Re-wash or discard |
| Press cleanliness | Every batch | Disassemble and clean cold press between batches | Do not press until clean |
| Taste test | Every batch | Founder tastes every recipe batch | Adjust or discard |
| Temperature | Every batch | Thermometer check post-bottling (must be <8 C) | Re-chill before delivery |
| Label accuracy | Every batch | Visual check that label matches content | Re-label |
| Bottle seal | Every batch | Check tamper-evident cap is properly sealed | Re-cap or discard |
| Delivery temperature | Random check weekly | Temp check at delivery point | Adjust cold chain (more ice packs, earlier delivery) |

## Stage 2: Dehydrated Pulp Products (Month 3–4)

### The Strategic Insight

Cold pressing produces juice AND pulp. The juice is the product. The pulp is currently waste. But this "waste" is nutrient-dense fiber — exactly what health-conscious consumers want.

**Converting waste into product creates:**
- A secondary revenue stream from the same raw material cost
- A zero-waste brand narrative that deepens trust
- A product category with higher margins (dried goods, longer shelf life)
- Customer upsell opportunities without new delivery infrastructure

### How Juice Pulp Converts to Products

```
Cold Press Process:
Ingredients → Cold Press → JUICE (primary product) + PULP (by-product)
                                                        │
                                              ┌─────────┴──────────┐
                                              │    DEHYDRATION     │
                                              │  (oven or machine) │
                                              └─────────┬──────────┘
                                                        │
                                    ┌───────────────────┼───────────────────┐
                                    │                   │                   │
                            Smoothie Powders    Cooking Boosters    Fiber Supplements
                            (add to smoothie,   (add to dal,       (mix with water
                             yogurt, oatmeal)    roti atta,         or curd)
                                                 rice dishes)
```

### Product Forms

| Product | Ingredients (from pulp) | Use Case | Packaging | Price | Margin |
|---------|----------------------|----------|-----------|-------|--------|
| **Green Booster Powder** | Spinach + kale + celery pulp, dehydrated | Add to morning smoothie, mix in curd | 100g pouch | Rs 250 | 75%+ (raw material is free) |
| **Beetroot-Carrot Fiber** | Beet + carrot pulp, dehydrated | Mix in chapati atta, add to dal | 100g pouch | Rs 200 | 75%+ |
| **Fruit Fiber Blend** | Apple + pineapple + orange pulp, dehydrated | Sprinkle on oatmeal, mix in yogurt | 100g pouch | Rs 300 | 70%+ |
| **Immunity Booster** | Ginger + turmeric + amla pulp, dehydrated + powdered | Mix in warm water, add to tea | 50g jar | Rs 350 | 80%+ |
| **Gut Health Fiber** | Mixed vegetable pulp + psyllium husk | Mix in water, take before meals | 150g pouch | Rs 250 | 70%+ |

### Customer Use Cases

| Use Case | Product | How Customer Uses It | Who Buys It |
|----------|---------|---------------------|-------------|
| Morning smoothie booster | Green Booster | 1 tsp in their blender smoothie | Persona 2 (Health-Conscious), Persona 3 (Gym) |
| Cooking nutrition boost | Beetroot-Carrot Fiber | Mix 2 tbsp into chapati atta for colored, nutritious rotis | Persona 4 (Women), Persona 5 (Family) |
| Breakfast topping | Fruit Fiber Blend | Sprinkle on oatmeal, cornflakes, or curd | Persona 1 (Tech), Persona 5 (Family) |
| Quick immunity drink | Immunity Booster | 1 tsp in warm water when feeling unwell | Persona 8 (Senior), Persona 7 (Gut Health) |
| Digestive support | Gut Health Fiber | 1 tbsp in water, 30 min before dinner | Persona 7 (Gut Health), Persona 8 (Senior) |

### Margin Upside

This is the most attractive margin profile in the entire roadmap:

| Metric | Cold-Pressed Juice | Pulp Products |
|--------|-------------------|---------------|
| Raw material cost | Rs 25–35/bottle | ~Rs 0 (waste from juice production) |
| Processing cost | Included in juice COGS | Rs 10–20 (dehydration electricity + labor) |
| Packaging | Rs 10–15 | Rs 15–25 (pouch + label) |
| Total COGS | Rs 45–53 | Rs 25–45 |
| Selling price | Rs 120–180 | Rs 200–350 |
| Gross margin | 56–79% | 75–87% |

### Operational Complexity

| Aspect | Complexity | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Dehydration equipment | Low | Food dehydrator: Rs 5,000–15,000. Or oven on low temp. |
| Processing time | Medium | 6–12 hours dehydration per batch. Overnight process. |
| Grinding/powdering | Low | Mixer grinder. Already in kitchen. |
| Quality consistency | Medium | Moisture content must be consistent. Too wet = mold. Too dry = dustite. Target 5–8% moisture. |
| Shelf life | Low complexity (advantage) | Dried products last 2–6 months in airtight packaging. No cold chain needed. |
| Food safety | Medium | FSSAI guidelines for dried food products. Moisture testing. Mold prevention. |
| Delivery | Low | Add to existing cold-pressed delivery. Or ship separately (no cold chain). |

### Branding Fit

"Even our by-products nourish you."

The zero-waste angle deeply reinforces brand trust:
- Environmentally conscious (no waste)
- Resourceful (same ingredients, double the products)
- Transparent (customers know where the powder comes from — the same ingredients in their morning juice)
- Innovation signal (we're constantly finding ways to add value)

### Recommendation

**Launch pulp products in Month 3–4, after:**
- Daily juice production is consistent (50+ bottles/day for 4+ weeks)
- Pulp volume is sufficient (50 bottles = ~5–7 kg pulp/day = enough for weekly powder batches)
- Customer base is 50+ subscribers (existing audience for upsell)
- Operational rhythm is established (adding a product line to a chaotic operation makes chaos worse)

**Start with 2 products only:** Green Booster Powder + Beetroot-Carrot Fiber. These use the most common juice pulps and have the clearest use cases.

## Stage 3: Fermented Shots (Trigger: 200 Customers)

### The Gut Health Angle

Gut health is the fastest-growing consumer wellness category in India. Probiotic supplements (Yakult, probiotic capsules) are mainstream but expensive and chemically produced. Natural fermented products are premium and differentiated.

### Why 200 Customers Is the Right Trigger

| Reason | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Operational maturity | At 200 customers, production and delivery ops are proven. Adding complexity is manageable. |
| Customer trust earned | 200 subscribers means many have been with you 2–4 months. Trust level supports premium product introduction. |
| Revenue supports R&D | Monthly revenue at Rs 6–8 lakhs. Can afford fermentation equipment (Rs 20,000–30,000) and testing. |
| Demand signal | By 200 customers, you've received enough gut health questions and interest to know demand is real. |
| Education foundation | 2–4 months of daily wellness education has primed customers for the gut health conversation. |

### Product Concepts

| Product | Composition | Volume | Price | Frequency |
|---------|------------|--------|-------|-----------|
| **Daily Gut Shot** | Water kefir + ginger + lemon + honey | 30ml | Rs 80 | Daily (morning, before food) |
| **Turmeric Fire Shot** | Apple cider vinegar + turmeric + black pepper + cayenne | 30ml | Rs 90 | Daily or alternate days |
| **Immunity Ferment** | Fermented amla + tulsi + ginger + honey | 30ml | Rs 100 | Daily (monsoon season focus) |
| **Digestive Bitters** | Fermented bitter gourd + ginger + cumin + lemon | 30ml | Rs 80 | Before meals (2x/day) |

### Differentiation

| Factor | Soma Delights Shots | Yakult | Probiotic Supplements |
|--------|-------------------|-------|----------------------|
| Ingredients | Natural, identifiable, whole food | Manufactured, cultured skim milk | Capsules, manufactured |
| Taste | Strong, intentional (not meant to taste like candy) | Sweet, dairy-based | No taste (pill) |
| Temperature | Cold, fresh | Cold | Room temp |
| Shelf life | 5–7 days (fermented, alive) | 30–40 days | 6–12 months |
| Price per serving | Rs 80–100 | Rs 12–15 | Rs 15–30 |
| Premium perception | High (craft, artisanal) | Low (mass market) | Medium (medical) |
| Education included | Yes (why each strain/ingredient matters) | No | No |
| Subscription fit | Natural add-on to daily delivery | Standalone, grocery purchase | Standalone, pharmacy purchase |

### Production Complexity

| Aspect | Challenge Level | Detail |
|--------|----------------|--------|
| **Fermentation time** | High | Water kefir: 24–48 hours. ACV-based: ready-mix (no fermentation needed). |
| **Consistency** | High | Fermentation is biological — batches vary. Taste, acidity, and probiotic count must be consistent. |
| **Equipment** | Medium | Fermentation vessels (Rs 5,000–10,000), pH meter (Rs 2,000), temperature control |
| **Food safety** | High | Fermented products require careful pathogen control. pH must stay below 4.6. |
| **Expertise** | High | Fermentation is a skill. Requires learning or hiring someone with fermentation experience. |
| **Scale** | Medium | Batch sizes limited by fermentation vessel capacity. Scaling requires more vessels. |

### Storage and Delivery

| Aspect | Requirement |
|--------|------------|
| Storage temperature | 2–6 C (must be cold to slow continued fermentation) |
| Shelf life | 5–7 days (living product) |
| Packaging | 30ml glass bottles with tamper-evident caps. Glass preferred for fermented products (no chemical leaching). |
| Delivery | Add to existing morning delivery. Small bottle, fits alongside juice bottle. |
| Labeling | Must include: fermentation culture type, best-before date, "contains live cultures," storage instructions |

### Subscription Fit

Fermented shots are the ideal subscription add-on:
- Small (30ml), easy to add to existing delivery
- Daily consumption needed for gut health benefit
- Premium pricing (Rs 80–100) increases AOV significantly
- Education-intensive (perfect for the Soma Delights model)
- Customers who subscribe to shots have the lowest churn rate (health need, not taste preference)

**AOV impact:** A customer who adds a daily gut shot to their juice subscription goes from Rs 4,500/month to Rs 7,000/month. A 55% AOV increase from one add-on.

## Stage 4: Wellness Plans (Trigger: 300 Customers)

### What Are Wellness Plans?

Wellness plans are curated combinations of products from Stages 1–3, bundled into goal-oriented daily regimens.

Instead of "pick your juice," the customer says "I want the Energy Plan" and receives a curated daily set: green juice + immunity shot + booster powder.

### Plan Architecture

| Plan | Contents | Daily Cost | Monthly | Target Persona |
|------|----------|-----------|---------|----------------|
| **Morning Energy** | Green Detox juice + Turmeric Fire shot | Rs 230 | Rs 6,900 | Persona 1 (Tech), Persona 6 (Premium) |
| **Gut Health Daily** | Ginger-Aloe juice + Daily Gut Shot + Gut Fiber powder (weekly) | Rs 260 | Rs 7,800 | Persona 7 (Gut Health) |
| **Women's Wellness** | Amla-Aloe juice + Immunity Ferment shot + Green Booster powder (weekly) | Rs 250 | Rs 7,500 | Persona 4 (Women) |
| **Family Starter** | 2 adult juices + 1 kid juice + 1 booster powder (weekly) | Rs 380 | Rs 11,400 | Persona 5 (Family) |
| **Senior Vitality** | Beetroot-Amla juice + Turmeric Fire shot | Rs 220 | Rs 6,600 | Persona 8 (Senior) |
| **Performance** | ABC juice + Turmeric-Ginger juice (alt days) + Recovery shot | Rs 280 | Rs 8,400 | Persona 3 (Gym) |
| **Complete Wellness** | Daily juice (rotating) + daily shot + weekly powder + monthly booklet | Rs 350 | Rs 10,500 | Persona 6 (Premium) |

### Fixed vs. Personalized

| Approach | Pros | Cons | Recommendation |
|----------|------|------|----------------|
| **Fixed plans** (predefined combos) | Simple to operate, predictable production, easy to explain | Less personal, may not fit everyone | **Start here.** 5–6 fixed plans cover 80% of needs. |
| **Personalized plans** (customer-configured) | Feels premium, addresses specific needs | Complex to produce, inventory management nightmare | **Defer until tech platform exists** (Year 2). |
| **Hybrid** | Fixed plans with 1–2 swap options | Good balance | **Phase 2 of Stage 4.** "Choose your plan, swap one product." |

### Operational Overhead

| Overhead | Impact | Mitigation |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Multiple products per delivery | More complex packing. 3 items vs. 1. | Pre-pack plan boxes night before. Standardize per plan. |
| Inventory for multiple products | Must produce juice + shots + powders simultaneously | Production schedule: powders (weekly), shots (every 2 days), juice (daily) |
| Plan customization requests | "Can I swap Tuesday's juice?" | Limited swaps. "You can change 1 product per week." |
| Plan changes | "I want to switch from Energy to Gut Health" | Allow plan changes with 3-day notice. |

### Retention Benefits

Plans dramatically improve retention:

| Metric | Individual Subscription | Wellness Plan |
|--------|------------------------|---------------|
| Monthly churn | 12–15% | 5–8% |
| Average tenure | 4–6 months | 10–14 months |
| AOV | Rs 3,500–5,000 | Rs 6,500–10,500 |
| Referral rate | 15–20% | 25–35% (more invested customers refer more) |
| Upsell potential | Low (already getting one product) | Medium (can upgrade plan tier) |

### Wallet / Credit System

Plans introduce the wallet system for referral credits:

| Feature | Design |
|---------|--------|
| Credit accumulation | 10% of each referred customer's monthly spend added to referrer's wallet |
| Minimum cashable amount | Rs 500 |
| Cash-out method | Applied as discount to next month's plan, or UPI transfer |
| Expiry | Credits expire after 12 months of inactivity |
| Display | WhatsApp message on the 1st of each month: "Your wallet balance: Rs [X]" |
| Plan discount application | Can use wallet balance to pay up to 50% of monthly plan cost |

## Stage 5: Meal Plans (Trigger: 500 Customers)

### Why 500 Customers

| Reason | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Operational scale required | Meal preparation requires significantly more kitchen capacity, ingredient variety, and labor |
| Revenue supports investment | At 500 subscribers, monthly revenue is Rs 15–20 lakhs. Can invest in dedicated meal kitchen. |
| Trust level maximized | Customers who've been with you 4–8 months trust your food quality implicitly. Meals feel natural. |
| Demand validation | By 500 customers, you've received enough "do you do meals?" requests to validate demand. |
| Delivery infrastructure mature | 3+ delivery routes, established timing, rider team in place. Adding meals to existing routes is feasible. |

### What Type First: Breakfast

Breakfast is the right entry point for meal plans. Not lunch. Not dinner. Breakfast.

| Why Breakfast | Detail |
|--------------|--------|
| **Same delivery window** | Already delivering at 6:30–7:30 AM. Add breakfast to the same delivery. Zero incremental delivery cost. |
| **Weakest meal in Indian homes** | Lunch and dinner are covered (domestic help, Swiggy, or home cooking). Breakfast is the most neglected, most inconsistent meal. |
| **Complements existing products** | Juice + breakfast = complete morning wellness. Natural upsell from existing subscription. |
| **Simplest to execute** | Breakfast items are smaller, more standardized, easier to prepare at scale than lunch/dinner. |
| **Highest habit potential** | Morning routine is the most habitable part of the day. Adding breakfast to the juice delivery deepens the morning ritual. |

### Breakfast Product Concepts

| Item | Description | Price | Shelf Stability | Complexity |
|------|------------|-------|----------------|------------|
| **Overnight oats jar** | Oats + almond milk + chia + fruit + honey, prepared night before | Rs 120 | 24 hours (cold) | Low |
| **Wellness muesli bowl** | House-made granola + dried fruit + seeds + yogurt | Rs 150 | Granola: 2 weeks. Yogurt: 2 days. | Medium |
| **Savory wellness wrap** | Whole wheat wrap + paneer/egg + vegetables + chutney | Rs 130 | 8–12 hours | Medium |
| **Smoothie bowl (premix)** | Frozen smoothie base + toppings (granola, seeds, fruit) | Rs 140 | Frozen base: 1 week. | Medium |
| **Idli/dosa wellness pack** | Millet-based idli or ragi dosa batter + wellness chutney | Rs 100 | Batter: 2 days. | Medium-High |
| **Sprouts & seeds jar** | Mixed sprouts + seeds + lemon + spice mix | Rs 90 | 24 hours | Low |

### Complexity vs. Upside

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| **Kitchen requirements** | Separate prep area needed. Meals can't be made in the same space as cold-pressed products (cross-contamination risk). |
| **Labor** | Need a cook (Rs 12,000–18,000/month) dedicated to breakfast prep. |
| **Ingredients** | Broader ingredient set (grains, dairy, eggs, wraps). More sourcing relationships. |
| **Quality consistency** | Meals are harder to standardize than juices. Taste variation is more noticeable. |
| **Customer expectations** | Higher. A Rs 120 juice that's slightly different is fine. A Rs 130 breakfast that's inconsistent is a complaint. |
| **Regulatory** | FSSAI requirements for prepared foods are stricter than beverages. |
| **Margins** | Lower than juice (40–50% vs. 55–65%). Higher ingredient costs, more labor. |
| **Revenue potential** | Very high. Breakfast plan + juice plan = Rs 8,000–12,000/month per family. |
| **Retention impact** | Very high. Customers who receive breakfast + juice are essentially locked in. Switching means rebuilding two habits. |

### Same Kitchen or Separate?

**Separate.** Non-negotiable.

| Reason | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Food safety | Cold-pressed juice production must be separate from cooked food preparation. Cross-contamination risk. |
| Equipment | Meals need stoves, ovens, storage that juice doesn't. Different kitchen entirely. |
| Timing | Juice production: 3–5 AM. Meal prep: 4–6 AM. Simultaneous but different workflows in same space = chaos. |
| FSSAI compliance | Separate food categories may require separate licenses or kitchen inspections. |

**Recommendation:** Rent a 2BHK. One room = juice production. One room = meal preparation. Shared refrigeration. Budget: Rs 15,000–25,000/month for the space.

## Stage Gate Checklist

Before advancing to any new stage, ALL criteria must be met:

### Gate 1 → Gate 2 (Juice → Pulp)

- [ ] 50+ active daily subscribers
- [ ] 4+ consecutive weeks of consistent daily production (zero missed deliveries)
- [ ] Unit economics positive (gross margin >50%)
- [ ] Customer retention >70% month-over-month
- [ ] Pulp volume sufficient (5+ kg/day)
- [ ] Dehydration test batch completed and taste-approved

### Gate 2 → Gate 3 (Pulp → Fermented Shots)

- [ ] 200+ active subscribers (juice + pulp combined)
- [ ] Pulp products generating Rs 15,000+/month revenue
- [ ] At least 2 fermentation test batches completed with consistent results
- [ ] pH testing equipment available and operator trained
- [ ] Food safety protocol documented for fermented products
- [ ] 3+ customers have explicitly asked about gut health products

### Gate 3 → Gate 4 (Shots → Wellness Plans)

- [ ] 300+ active subscribers
- [ ] All three product lines (juice, pulp, shots) operational simultaneously for 4+ weeks
- [ ] Customer data sufficient to define 5+ meaningful plan bundles
- [ ] Wallet/credit system designed and tested
- [ ] Multi-product packing process documented and timed (<5 min per plan box)

### Gate 4 → Gate 5 (Plans → Meal Plans)

- [ ] 500+ active subscribers
- [ ] Monthly revenue >Rs 15 lakhs
- [ ] Separate kitchen space secured and equipped
- [ ] Cook hired and trained on 3+ breakfast recipes
- [ ] 10+ pilot customers confirmed for meal plan beta test
- [ ] FSSAI license updated for prepared food category
- [ ] Supply chain for meal ingredients established (2+ suppliers per critical ingredient)

---

*This roadmap is not a timeline — it's a sequence. Execute each stage fully before advancing. The most common startup death is doing too many things poorly instead of one thing excellently.*
