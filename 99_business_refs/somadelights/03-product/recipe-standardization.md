# Recipe Standardization — Soma Delights

> The operational backbone of production. Every bottle must taste the same, every time.
> All measurements are by weight (grams) unless noted. No eyeballing. Ever.

---

## Production Principles

1. **Weigh everything.** Every ingredient, every batch. The digital scale is your most important tool after the juicer.
2. **Same order, every time.** Juicing sequence affects yield and taste consistency.
3. **Taste every batch.** Founder approval on every production run until a trained assistant is in place.
4. **Log everything.** Date, batch number, yield, taste notes, any deviations.
5. **Temperature discipline.** Juice must be below 8 degrees C within 15 minutes of juicing. Bottled and refrigerated immediately.

## Launch SKUs — Complete Recipes

### SKU 1: Green Morning (Green Vitality)

**Profile:** Fresh, bright, slightly sweet. The flagship daily green juice.
**Target volume:** 250ml per bottle
**Color:** Vibrant green (like fresh spinach water)

#### Ingredients (per bottle)

| Ingredient | Quantity | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Spinach (palak) | 80g | Fresh, dark green leaves only. Discard thick stems |
| Cucumber | 120g | English or desi. Wash, no need to peel if skin is thin |
| Green apple | 80g | Granny Smith preferred. Remove seeds and core. Keep skin on |
| Celery | 40g | Use stalks and leaves both. Wash thoroughly |
| Lemon juice | 15ml | Freshly squeezed. Strain seeds. (~half a medium lemon) |
| Ginger (fresh) | 5g | Peeled. Small thumb-nail sized piece |
| Water | 0ml | Pure cold-pressed — no dilution |

**Total raw input:** ~340g
**Expected yield:** 250-270ml (varies with produce freshness and juicer)
**If yield is below 220ml:** Produce is likely dehydrated or juicer needs cleaning. Do not add water to compensate.

#### Prep Steps

1. Wash all produce twice — first rinse under running water, second soak in clean water for 2 minutes, drain
2. Remove apple seeds and core. Cut into quarters. Keep skin on (nutrition + color)
3. Peel ginger. Cut into small pieces for easier juicing
4. Cut cucumber into 3-4 pieces (to fit juicer feed)
5. Separate spinach leaves from thick stems (thin stems are fine to include)
6. Trim celery base. Cut stalks into 8-10cm lengths
7. Juice lemon separately. Strain to remove seeds and pulp

#### Juicing Order (FOLLOW EXACTLY)

1. **Spinach** — goes first because it's hardest to juice. Follow immediately with...
2. **Cucumber** — high water content pushes spinach through the juicer
3. **Green apple** — adds sweetness and body
4. **Celery** — juices well after apple has primed the strainer
5. **Ginger** — small amount, push through with remaining cucumber if needed
6. **Lemon juice** — add AFTER juicing, stir in. Do not run through juicer (acids can affect strainer)

#### Post-Juice

1. Stir gently with a clean spoon (3-4 rotations — do not whisk/blend)
2. Pour through fine mesh strainer if any fibrous bits visible
3. Fill bottle immediately using funnel
4. Cap and refrigerate within 5 minutes of juicing
5. Temperature check: must be below 10 degrees C before going into delivery bag

#### Quality Gates

- **Taste:** Fresh, clean, slightly sweet from apple, mild ginger warmth on finish. Not bitter. Not grassy/swampy
- **Color:** Vibrant bright green. If brownish or olive-toned = oxidation = batch is compromised. Do not deliver
- **Smell:** Fresh green, clean. No fermented or off odors
- **Texture:** Smooth, slight body. Not watery, not thick/pulpy

**Profile:** Earthy, sweet, vibrant. The stamina and blood-health juice.

- **Color:** Deep magenta to crimson. If dull brown = old beet or oxidation
- **Special note:** Beet juice stains everything. Use dedicated containers if possible. Warn customers that red urine/stool after beet juice is normal (beeturia) — include on ingredient card

### SKU 3: Golden Shield (Turmeric Ginger Immunity)

**Profile:** Warm, spicy, golden. The daily immunity and anti-inflammation shot in juice form.
**Target volume:** 250ml per bottle
**Color:** Deep golden-orange

#### Ingredients (per bottle)

| Ingredient | Quantity | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Carrot | 150g | Peeled. Forms the juice base |
| Fresh turmeric | 15g | Peeled. Use fresh when available (Oct-Mar). Dry turmeric is NOT a substitute for juicing |
| Ginger (fresh) | 12g | Peeled. This is a warming, spicy juice |
| Apple | 60g | Sweetness to balance spice. Remove seeds |
| Lemon juice | 10ml | Freshly squeezed |
| Black pepper | 1 pinch (~0.3g) | Freshly ground. Activates curcumin absorption by 2000% |
| Water | 0ml | No dilution |

**Total raw input:** ~248g (lower volume because turmeric/ginger yield less juice)
**Expected yield:** 230-260ml

#### Prep Steps

1. Wash all produce twice
2. Peel carrots, cut into pieces
3. Peel fresh turmeric (wear gloves — turmeric stains worse than beet). Slice thin
4. Peel ginger, slice
5. Core and quarter apple
6. Juice lemon separately
7. Grind black pepper fresh (pre-ground loses potency quickly)

#### Juicing Order

1. **Carrot** — base juice, goes first for volume
2. **Fresh turmeric** — firm, juice right after carrot
3. **Ginger** — follows turmeric
4. **Apple** — flushes remaining turmeric/ginger through
5. **Lemon juice** — stir in after
6. **Black pepper** — add AFTER juicing, stir in thoroughly

#### Quality Gates

- **Taste:** Warm, golden, spicy. Ginger heat should be present but not overwhelming. Carrot sweetness balances. Slight peppery finish
- **Color:** Deep golden-orange. If pale = not enough turmeric. If brownish = oxidation
- **Special note:** Turmeric stains are nearly impossible to remove. Dedicate specific containers and cloths to Golden Shield production. Juice this AFTER lighter-colored juices to avoid cross-contamination of color

- **Color:** Bright orange-yellow. Should look like sunshine in a bottle

**Profile:** Intense, fiery, concentrated. The daily immunity ritual. Not sipped — taken as a shot.
**Target volume:** 60ml per bottle
**Color:** Pale golden-yellow with cloudy texture

- **Taste:** Intensely gingery. Burns in a good way. Lemon brightness cuts through. Should make your eyes water slightly. If it doesn't, add more ginger
- **Color:** Pale golden-yellow, slightly cloudy
- **Note:** This is a shot, not a sip. 60ml taken in 1-2 gulps. Educate customers: "Take it first thing in the morning on empty stomach. Follow with a glass of warm water. The burn lasts 30 seconds."

## Seasonal Recipe Adjustments

### Summer (March - June, Hyderabad: 35-45 degrees C)

- **Green Morning:** increase cucumber to 140g, reduce spinach to 70g (lighter, more hydrating)
- **All SKUs:** add 5ml aloe vera juice (cooling) — optional seasonal twist
- **New seasonal:** Watermelon Mint Cooler (watermelon 180g + mint 10g + lemon 15ml + pinch of black salt)
- **Ginger shots:** reduce ginger slightly (heat + ginger = too much for some)

### Monsoon (July - September)

- **Golden Shield:** increase ginger to 15g, add tulsi leaves 5g (immunity boost)
- **Citrus Immunity:** add crushed black pepper to 0.5g (anti-bacterial)
- **All SKUs:** extra emphasis on hygiene — produce is dirtier in monsoon, wash 3x instead of 2x
- **Ingredient caution:** leafy greens may have more insects/dirt in monsoon. Inspect carefully

### Winter (November - February)

- **Golden Shield:** increase turmeric to 20g (peak fresh turmeric season, use it)
- **Ginger shots:** maximum intensity — fresh ginger is at its best and cheapest
- **Citrus Immunity:** amla at its peak — increase to 40g for maximum benefit
- **New seasonal:** Amla-Ginger Immunity Bomb (amla 50g + ginger 30g + honey 10ml + lemon 15ml + pepper) as a limited-run shot

## Quality Control Checklist — Per Batch

Print this and keep at the production station. Check every box for every batch.

### Pre-Production
- [ ] Hands washed, gloves on, hair net on, apron on
- [ ] Workspace sanitized (counters, juicer, containers)
- [ ] All ingredients washed (minimum 2x)
- [ ] Digital scale zeroed and calibrated
- [ ] Bottles and caps clean (pre-rinsed, air-dried)
- [ ] Ice packs in freezer (ready for post-production packing)
- [ ] Production log open (paper or phone)

### During Production
- [ ] All ingredients weighed per recipe (no eyeballing)
- [ ] Juicing order followed exactly
- [ ] Lemon juice / pepper / honey added AFTER juicing, stirred in
- [ ] Juice poured through fine strainer (if needed for SKU)
- [ ] Taste test: flavor matches expected profile
- [ ] Color check: matches expected color (photograph if first batch of the day)
- [ ] Temperature check: below 10 degrees C at bottling

### Post-Production
- [ ] Bottles filled, capped immediately
- [ ] Labels applied (correct SKU label on correct bottle — double check)
- [ ] Manufacturing date written/stamped on label
- [ ] Bottles into fridge within 5 minutes of capping
- [ ] Actual yield recorded (total ml produced vs expected)
- [ ] Any deviations noted (ingredient substitutions, yield anomalies, taste differences)
- [ ] Juicer disassembled and cleaned within 10 minutes
- [ ] Workspace cleaned
- [ ] Pulp collected (compost or dehydrator)

## Pulp Management

Your juicer produces roughly 30-50% pulp by weight. For 120 bottles/week, that is 8-12 kg of pulp. Do not waste it.

### Immediate Uses
- **Compost:** best option if you have space. Excellent fertilizer
- **Dehydrated vegetable powder:** dehydrate at 55-60 degrees C for 8-12 hours, grind to powder. Sell as secondary product (Rs 200-400 per 100g jar)
- **Give to neighbors/community:** fresh pulp for cooking (beet pulp in parathas, carrot pulp in halwa)

### Pulp by SKU
- **Green Morning pulp:** mild, good for smoothies or composting
- **Beet Vitality pulp:** excellent for beet parathas, beet powder after dehydration
- **ABC Classic pulp:** sweet, good for baking (muffins, energy bars)
- **Ginger/turmeric pulp:** strong flavor, dehydrate for spice powder
