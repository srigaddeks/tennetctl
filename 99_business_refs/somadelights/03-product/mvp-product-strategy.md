# Soma Delights — MVP Product Strategy

## MVP Philosophy

The MVP product line must satisfy exactly three criteria:

1. **Every SKU serves the habit-building mission.** If a product is delicious but doesn't encourage daily consumption, it doesn't belong in the MVP.
2. **Every SKU is operationally feasible in a home kitchen.** If it requires specialized equipment, exotic ingredients, or complex timing, it doesn't belong in the MVP.
3. **The full lineup tells a coherent story.** A first-time customer looking at 4–6 options should immediately understand: "This is a daily wellness brand, and these are my options for starting my habit."

## Category Evaluation

### Green/Detox Juices

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| **Habit potential** | Very high. Green juice is the quintessential "daily health ritual" product. Customers who drink green juice daily tend to keep doing it. |
| **Operational feasibility** | High. Spinach, kale, cucumber, celery, apple, ginger, lemon — all available year-round at Bowenpally market. Simple cold-press process. |
| **Taste accessibility** | Medium. Pure green juice can be intense for first-timers. Apple and lemon balance it, but some customers need a gentler introduction. |
| **Margin** | Excellent. Greens are the cheapest category at Rs 30–80/kg. COGS per bottle: Rs 25–30. |
| **Brand signal** | Perfect. A green juice in someone's hand immediately signals "wellness." It's the visual identity of the brand. |
| **MVP inclusion** | **YES — 2 SKUs.** One strong (for experienced drinkers), one mild (for beginners). |

### Root Vegetable/Classic Blends (ABC, Beetroot)

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| **Habit potential** | Very high. ABC (apple-beet-carrot) is familiar, approachable, and has clear health associations (beetroot = BP, carrot = eyes/skin). |
| **Operational feasibility** | High. Root vegetables are stable, affordable, and year-round. Cold press handles them well. Minor equipment wear from dense roots. |
| **Taste accessibility** | High. Naturally sweet from beet and carrot. Apple adds familiarity. Most people enjoy this on first try. |
| **Margin** | Good. COGS per bottle: Rs 28–35. Slightly higher than greens due to apple cost. |
| **Brand signal** | Good. Beetroot's deep red color is visually striking and health-coded. |
| **MVP inclusion** | **YES — 1 SKU.** The ABC blend is the best trial product. Accessible, recognizable, health-credible. |

### Anti-Inflammatory Blends (Turmeric-Ginger)

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| **Habit potential** | Very high. Turmeric and ginger are already embedded in Indian health culture. A cold-pressed version of something their grandmother told them about. |
| **Operational feasibility** | High. Turmeric root, ginger root, lemon, black pepper — simple ingredients. Small volume per bottle (these are potent, 200–300ml). |
| **Taste accessibility** | Medium. Strong, spicy, warming. Not for everyone. But those who like it become daily devotees. |
| **Margin** | Excellent. COGS per bottle: Rs 20–28. Small ingredient quantities per bottle. Turmeric and ginger are inexpensive in bulk. |
| **Brand signal** | Excellent. This is where "daily wellness" and "ancient Indian wisdom" intersect. Differentiates from Western cold-pressed brands. |
| **MVP inclusion** | **YES — 1 SKU.** The turmeric-ginger-lemon-pepper blend. Unique to Soma Delights. Traditional + modern. |

### Fruit-Forward Blends

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| **Habit potential** | Medium. Fruit juices taste great but feel like treats, not rituals. Customers may not attribute health benefits the same way as green/root juices. |
| **Operational feasibility** | Medium. Depends on seasonal fruit availability. Mango (Apr–Jun only), pineapple (variable quality), orange (seasonal pricing). |
| **Taste accessibility** | Very high. These are the most immediately enjoyable products. Great for trial, family, and kids. |
| **Margin** | Lower. Fruit costs fluctuate significantly. Mango season: Rs 60–80/kg. Off-season tropical: Rs 100–150/kg. COGS: Rs 35–45. |
| **Brand signal** | Moderate. Pure fruit juice can feel like "just juice" rather than "wellness." Must be paired with a health angle. |
| **MVP inclusion** | **YES — 1 SKU.** One fruit-forward blend for accessibility, family/kid appeal, and trial conversion. NOT the lead product. |

### Wellness Shots (Concentrated, Small Volume)

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| **Habit potential** | Very high. 30ml shots are the ultimate micro-wellness product — tiny commitment, clear daily ritual, specific health purpose. |
| **Operational feasibility** | Medium-High. Small volumes, but require precision in ingredient ratios. Different bottle format (30–60ml vs. 300ml). |
| **Taste accessibility** | Low-Medium. Shots are meant to be functional, not delicious. Ginger-lemon-cayenne is intense. Customers must understand the purpose. |
| **Margin** | Excellent. COGS per shot: Rs 10–15. Selling price: Rs 60–80. Margin: 75%+. |
| **Brand signal** | Strong. Shots scream "serious about wellness." They elevate the brand from "juice delivery" to "wellness protocol." |
| **MVP inclusion** | **YES — 1 SKU.** One wellness shot to differentiate from juice-only brands and serve the most health-intentional customers. |

### Nut Milks

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| **Habit potential** | Medium. Good product, but different use case (coffee additive, cereal, cooking). |
| **Operational feasibility** | Low-Medium. Requires overnight soaking, straining equipment, different process flow. |
| **Taste accessibility** | Medium. Many people expect dairy-like taste and are disappointed. |
| **Margin** | Low-Medium. Nuts are expensive (almonds: Rs 800–1,200/kg, cashews: Rs 600–900/kg). |
| **Brand signal** | Weak for MVP. Adds confusion: "Are they a juice brand or a milk brand?" |
| **MVP inclusion** | **NO. Defer to Year 2.** |

### Smoothies

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| **Habit potential** | Medium. Smoothies feel like meals/snacks, not daily wellness rituals. |
| **Operational feasibility** | Low. Banana oxidation, thickness issues in delivery, separation during transport. |
| **Taste accessibility** | High. But "tastes good" is not the MVP criteria — "builds daily habit" is. |
| **Margin** | Low-Medium. Banana, protein additions, and thicker base ingredients increase COGS. |
| **Brand signal** | Weak. Smoothies position us closer to "food delivery" than "wellness." |
| **MVP inclusion** | **NO. May never launch under this brand.** |

## MVP SKU Recommendation: 5 Products

After evaluating all categories, the MVP launches with exactly **5 SKUs**. Not 4 (too limited for variety), not 6 (unnecessary complexity). Five.

### Why 5 SKUs

| Reason | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Variety without overwhelm** | 5 options is manageable for a first-time customer to evaluate. 10 options = decision paralysis. |
| **Production manageable** | 5 recipes to master, 5 ingredient sets to source, 5 labels to print. Doable for one person. |
| **Covers all persona entry points** | Each of the primary personas (1–5) has at least one ideal entry product. |
| **Allows daily rotation** | A daily subscriber can rotate through 5 products across the week without repetition (M-F rotation). |
| **Tests breadth** | 5 products across different categories generates data on what customers prefer. Drop losers, double down on winners. |

## What to EXCLUDE from MVP and Why

| Excluded Product | Why Excluded | When to Reconsider |
|-----------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Pure celery juice** | Trendy but divisive taste. Celery supply in Hyderabad is inconsistent and expensive. Medical claims around celery juice are overhyped. | Maybe never. Include celery AS AN INGREDIENT, not as a solo product. |
| **Wheatgrass shot** | Growing wheatgrass requires dedicated setup (trays, grow lights, 7–10 day cycles). Too complex for MVP. | Stage 3, alongside fermented shots. |
| **Aloe vera standalone** | Pure aloe vera juice tastes awful. Works as an ingredient (5–10% in blends) but not as a product. | Never as standalone. Always blended. |
| **Protein-added juices** | Adding protein powder (whey, plant-based) to cold-pressed juice changes the texture and creates settling issues. Conflicts with "no additives" brand. | Never for cold-pressed line. Possible in meal plan stage. |
| **Coconut water** | Available everywhere for Rs 30–40. Competing with commodity product is a losing strategy. | Never. Use coconut water as an ingredient base if needed, not as a product. |
| **Acai bowls / smoothie bowls** | Acai is imported, expensive, and inconsistent supply. Bowls require different packaging, different consumption occasion (sit-down, not grab-and-drink). | Stage 5 (meal plans), maybe. |
| **Detox packages** (3-day, 5-day, 7-day) | "Detox" is anti-habit. It positions the product as temporary. We are building daily habits, not cleanses. | Never under Soma Delights. Conflicts with core philosophy. |
| **Sugar-added drinks** | Any added sugar (even "natural" like jaggery beyond minimal amounts) compromises the wellness positioning. Fruits provide sufficient sweetness. | Never. Honey in small quantities (Ginger Fire Shot, Golden Shield) is acceptable because it's functional (coating the throat, antimicrobial properties). |

## Pricing Structure for MVP

### Pricing Philosophy

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Mid-premium, not luxury** | Rs 120–180 per full bottle. Accessible for daily use. Not cheap enough to seem low-quality. Not expensive enough to cause daily guilt. |
| **Daily math, not bottle math** | Frame pricing as "Rs 150/day for your wellness" not "Rs 150 per bottle." Daily framing normalizes the spend. |
| **Subscription discount** | Subscribers pay 10–15% less than one-time buyers. Rewards commitment. |
| **Trial pricing** | 3-day trial at a slight discount to reduce risk. Not a deep discount — just enough to say "low-risk first step." |
| **No discounting culture** | Never run "50% off" sales. No discount codes. No flash sales. Discounts train customers to wait for deals. |

## The Habit-Building Requirement: Why Every Product Must Earn Its Place

This is the most important section in this document.

Every product in the Soma Delights lineup must pass the **Daily Habit Test:**

| Test Question | Required Answer |
|---------------|-----------------|
| "Would someone drink this every single morning?" | Yes — either as a staple or as part of a rotation. |
| "Does this product feel like a ritual or a treat?" | A ritual. Treats are occasional. Rituals are daily. |
| "If a customer skipped this for 3 days, would they miss it?" | Yes — either the taste, the routine, or the feeling. |
| "Does this product educate?" | Yes — every ingredient has a reason, and the customer learns that reason. |
| "Does this product look like wellness?" | Yes — visually, packaging, naming, and messaging all signal daily wellness, not occasional indulgence. |
| "Can this product be part of a 6 AM delivery?" | Yes — it fits the morning delivery window and morning consumption ritual. |

Products that fail this test — no matter how delicious, popular, or profitable — do not belong in the Soma Delights lineup.

### What This Means in Practice

- **No dessert-like smoothies.** They taste great but feel like treats, not rituals.
- **No "cleanse" packages.** They're temporary. We build permanent habits.
- **No trendy superfoods without functional purpose.** No activated charcoal (does nothing). No spirulina unless we can explain why in one sentence on an ingredient card.
- **No products that require explanation.** If a customer can't understand the product's purpose in 10 seconds, it's too complicated for MVP.
- **No products that taste good but don't do anything.** Taste is necessary but not sufficient. Every bottle must contribute to a daily health outcome.

## MVP Launch Checklist

Before launching the first delivery:

### Product Readiness
- [ ] All 5 recipes finalized and tested (minimum 3 batches each)
- [ ] Shelf life tested: each product stable for 48 hours at 4–8 C
- [ ] Taste tested with 10+ people outside family/friends (honest feedback)
- [ ] Ingredient sourcing confirmed for all products (primary + backup suppliers)
- [ ] COGS calculated and confirmed per SKU (must be profitable at subscription price)

### Packaging Readiness
- [ ] 300ml PET bottles ordered (minimum 500 units)
- [ ] 60ml glass bottles ordered for shots (minimum 200 units)
- [ ] Labels printed (5 designs, waterproof, QR codes functional)
- [ ] Ingredient cards designed and printed (5 cards, 200 copies each)
- [ ] Insulated delivery bags procured (5–10 bags)
- [ ] Gel ice packs procured (20+ reusable packs)

### Operational Readiness
- [ ] Home kitchen optimized (cold press, refrigerator, prep station, bottling area)
- [ ] Production schedule documented (3 AM–5 AM pressing, 5 AM bottling, 5:30 AM staging)
- [ ] Delivery route mapped (first 10–20 delivery points)
- [ ] FSSAI registration complete
- [ ] Quality control checklist printed and posted in kitchen
- [ ] WhatsApp Business account set up with catalog

### Brand Readiness
- [ ] Logo and visual identity finalized
