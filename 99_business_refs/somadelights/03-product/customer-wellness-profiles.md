## 1. Why Profiles Matter

### The Problem with One-Size-Fits-All Juice

A 60-year-old woman managing Type 2 diabetes and a 25-year-old man training for a marathon have fundamentally different nutritional needs. Serving them the same Green Morning juice is like a doctor prescribing the same medicine to every patient.

The diabetic woman needs:
- Zero fruit sugars (no apple, no orange)
- Blood sugar-stabilizing ingredients (bitter gourd, amla, cucumber)
- Low glycemic index throughout

The marathon runner needs:
- Beetroot for nitric oxide (blood flow, endurance)
- Potassium-rich ingredients (recovery)
- Anti-inflammatory bases (ginger, turmeric for joint protection)
- Adequate natural sugars for energy

**Same base juices. Completely different ratios. That is the power of profiles.**

### Why This Is a Competitive Advantage

| Competitor Approach | Soma Delights Approach |
|---|---|
| Fixed menu, same juice for everyone | Same menu PLUS profile-based personalization |
| Customer self-selects from menu | Customer is matched to a wellness profile via quiz |
| No health consideration | Blends adjusted for health conditions and goals |
| Transactional: "buy juice" | Relational: "we know your health goals and blend for them" |
| Easy to switch providers | Switching cost is high — new provider doesn't know your profile |

In Hyderabad, no cold-pressed juice brand offers profile-based personalization. This is Soma Delights' moat.

### Why Profiles Are Operationally Feasible

Profiles would be impossible with per-SKU production. With the modular base system:

- All profiles use the SAME 10-12 base juices
- Only the RATIOS change per profile
- A profile blend takes the same mixing time as a standard SKU
- No new ingredients needed — just a different mixing sheet
- One morning pressing session serves ALL profiles and standard menu orders

## 2. How Profiles Work

### The Profile Flow

```
CUSTOMER JOURNEY:

1. DISCOVERY
   Customer finds Soma Delights (Instagram, referral, WhatsApp)

2. ONBOARDING
   Customer takes 5-question wellness quiz (WhatsApp or website)
   → Age group, health conditions, wellness goals, taste preference, allergies

3. PROFILE MATCH
   Based on answers, Soma Delights recommends 1-2 profiles
   Example: "Based on your answers, we recommend the Diabetic Wellness profile.
   Your daily blend will be optimized for blood sugar management with zero fruit sugars."

4. SUBSCRIPTION
   Customer subscribes to a plan (daily, alternate day, 3x/week)
   Their profile determines which blends they receive

5. DAILY PRODUCTION
   Customer's profile appears on the daily mixing sheet
   Kitchen mixes their specific blend using profile ratios

6. DELIVERY
   Bottle is labeled with customer name + profile name
   First delivery includes an education card explaining their profile

7. FEEDBACK LOOP
   After 1 week: "How are you feeling? Any taste adjustments needed?"
   After 1 month: "Would you like to adjust your profile or try a different focus?"

8. PROFILE SWITCH (anytime)
   Customer WhatsApps: "I want to switch to Immunity Booster"
   → Updated from next delivery
```

### Profile vs. Standard Menu

Profiles do NOT replace the standard menu. They augment it.

| Customer Type | What They Get |
|---|---|
| Casual buyer (no profile) | Picks from standard menu — Green Morning, Beet Vitality, etc. |
| Profile subscriber | Gets profile-specific blends — same bases, adjusted ratios |
| Mixed | Subscribes with a profile but can also order standard menu items anytime |

### The T-Shirt Size Analogy

Profiles are like T-shirt sizes (S, M, L, XL) — not custom tailoring.

- **Custom per person** = too complex, too slow, not scalable. Rejected.
- **One-size-fits-all** = ignores individual health needs. Rejected.
- **Pre-designed profiles** = covers 80% of customer needs with 10 well-designed templates. Adopted.

If a customer has ultra-specific needs that no profile covers, handle it manually at first. If you see the same request 5+ times, create a new profile.

### 10.2 Profile-Specific Safety Notes

| Profile | Safety Consideration | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Diabetic** | Carrot and beetroot contain natural sugars. Even vegetable-only blends affect blood sugar. | Advise customer to monitor blood sugar in first week. Adjust ratios if needed. |
| **Diabetic** | Bitter gourd can interact with diabetes medication (may cause hypoglycemia). | Bitter Wellness blend: warn customer to monitor blood sugar closely. Consult doctor. |
| **Senior** | Vitamin K in spinach and greens interacts with blood thinners (warfarin). | Ask about blood thinner medication during onboarding. If yes: reduce spinach, increase cucumber. Flag in production manifest. |
| **Senior** | Beetroot can lower blood pressure — on top of BP medication, could cause dizziness. | Start with lower beetroot ratio (20%). Monitor. Ask customer to track BP for first week. |
| **Kids** | Children under 5 have different digestive capacity. | Do not serve children under 5 without pediatrician approval. Profile is for 5-12 only. |
| **Kids** | Choking risk if pulp is too thick. | Ensure kid blends are well-mixed and pourable. No thick chunks. |
| **Pregnancy** | Certain ingredients should be limited: turmeric (uterine stimulant in high doses), amla (cooling), bitter gourd (not recommended). | If customer discloses pregnancy: REMOVE turmeric, amla, bitter gourd. Create a safe pregnancy sub-profile (cucumber, carrot, apple, mild ginger, lemon only). |
| **Allergies** | Any ingredient can be an allergen. Common: citrus, celery (rare). | Track allergies per customer. Flag on every production manifest. Cross-check before mixing. |
| **All profiles** | Cold-pressed juice has no preservatives — shelf life is 24 hours maximum. | Every label: "Best consumed within 24 hours. Keep refrigerated at 2-4 degrees C." |
| **All profiles** | Raw juice may contain trace bacteria from produce. | Wash all produce thoroughly (3 rinses). Sanitize equipment daily. Immunocompromised customers: discuss risks. |

### 10.3 Allergy Management Protocol

```
ALLERGY PROTOCOL:

1. COLLECTION: Ask about allergies during onboarding (Q5 of wellness quiz).

2. RECORDING: Add to customer record in RED. Example:
   "Priya K | Women's Wellness | ALLERGY: Citrus (lemon, orange)"

3. FLAGGING: Every time this customer appears on the mixing sheet,
   the allergy column must show the allergen.
   Example row: "Priya K | Iron Glow | ... | ALLERGY: NO CITRUS"

4. SUBSTITUTION:
   - Remove the allergen base entirely
   - Replace with closest safe alternative (see Section 4.4 above)
   - Note the substitution on mixing sheet

5. VERIFICATION: Before pouring into bottle, check mixing sheet one
   more time. "Does this bottle contain the allergen? No? Proceed."

6. LABELING: Allergy customers get an extra note on label:
   "Prepared without [allergen]. Please verify at delivery."

RULE: One allergy mistake = one lost customer (and potentially a lawsuit).
Take this seriously from Day 1, even at 10 customers.
```
