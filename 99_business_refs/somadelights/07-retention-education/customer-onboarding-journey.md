# 38 — Customer Onboarding Journey

> The first 7 days decide everything. A customer who makes it past Day 7 has a 75% chance of staying for a month. A customer who makes it past Day 21 has formed a habit. Every interaction in those first 21 days is carefully designed to build that habit.

## 3. Day 1 — The First Delivery

### 3.1 What's in the First Delivery

| Item | Purpose | Cost (INR) |
|------|---------|-----------|
| 300ml juice (their chosen or recommended SKU) | The product | Rs 80-120 (COGS) |
| Premium wellness booklet | Habit-building tool, personal tracker | Rs 40-60 |
| Ingredient card #1 (Spinach or matched to SKU) | Education layer 3 begins | Rs 2-3 |
| Handwritten welcome note | Personal touch, emotional anchor | Rs 5 (card stock) |
| Insulated delivery bag (branded) | Keeps juice cold, doorstep marketing | Rs 250-400 (one-time) |
| **Total first delivery cost** | | **Rs 377-588** |

The first delivery is the most expensive. Every subsequent delivery is just juice + card (Rs 95-140).

### 3.2 The Welcome Note

Handwritten on a clean card (or high-quality printed with handwriting-style font for scale):

```
Day 1 of your wellness journey.

Open your booklet. Write down one thing you
want to change about how you feel.

It doesn't have to be big. "More energy" or
"better mornings" is enough.

This isn't about juice. It's about showing up
for yourself every single day.

See you tomorrow.
— Sri
```

**Why handwritten:** A handwritten note in 2026 is so rare that it creates an emotional impact disproportionate to its cost. It signals: "This is not mass-produced. Someone cared enough to write this by hand."

**At scale (100+ customers):** Switch to high-quality printed notes with a handwriting-style font. Add a personal touch by writing the customer's name by hand.

### 3.3 Day 1 WhatsApp Message — 8:00 AM

Sent after delivery is confirmed dropped off:

```
Good morning! Your first Soma is at your door. 🌿

Pro tip: drink it on an empty stomach for best absorption.
The spinach and ginger work best when your digestive system
isn't competing with other food.

Let me know how you like it! Even a thumbs up is great.
— Sri
```

### 3.4 Handling Day 1 Responses

**If positive ("Loved it!", "So fresh!", "Tastes great!"):**
```
So glad! That freshness is because it was pressed at 4 AM
this morning. Nothing sits on a shelf. Tomorrow's bottle
will be just as fresh.

Quick question — did you get a chance to open the booklet?
```

**If neutral ("It's okay", "Different", "Not what I expected"):**
```
Totally normal! Cold-pressed green juice is very different
from what most of us are used to. Your taste buds adjust
within 3-4 days.

Give it until Day 3 — most people have an "aha" moment
around then. And if a different flavor would work better,
just let me know.
```

**If negative ("Too bitter", "Don't like the taste", "Not for me"):**
```
I appreciate the honesty — taste is personal! A few options:

1. I can switch you to a different blend tomorrow
   (Citrus Morning is sweeter, Beet Revival is earthier)
2. I can adjust the recipe (more apple for sweetness,
   less ginger for mildness)
3. If cold-pressed juice truly isn't your thing,
   no pressure at all — I'd rather you love it than
   force it

What feels right?
```

**If no response by 12 PM:**
Do nothing. Don't chase. Some people are busy. They'll respond when ready. Over-messaging on Day 1 feels needy.

**If no response by Day 2 evening:**
One light follow-up:
```
Hey! Just checking — did you find your delivery yesterday?
Want to make sure it reached the right spot. 🌿
```

## 4. Day 2 — Establishing the Routine

### 4.1 Delivery

- Juice (same or different SKU based on plan).
- New ingredient card (different from Day 1).
- No welcome kit, no booklet (that was Day 1 only).

### 4.2 Communication

**No WhatsApp message on Day 2.**

Why: Over-communication in the first few days creates message fatigue. Day 1 was active. Day 2 should feel like a quiet, reliable routine — the juice just shows up. That reliability IS the message.

## 5. Day 3 — The First Check-In

### 5.1 Why Day 3 Matters

Day 3 is the critical inflection point:
- By Day 3, the initial excitement of trying something new has faded.
- The taste novelty has worn off.
- Real life is competing for attention.
- If the customer has any doubts, they'll surface by Day 3.
- A well-timed check-in prevents silent drop-off.

### 5.2 Day 3 WhatsApp Message — 8:00 AM

```
Day 3! How are you feeling? Any difference in energy or mood?

Some people notice a subtle shift by now — a little more
alertness in the morning, slightly better digestion. Others
take a full week. Both are normal.

The key thing is: you've shown up for your health 3 days in
a row. That's already more consistent than most people
manage. 💪
```

### 5.3 Handling Day 3 Responses

**If positive ("I do feel a bit more energetic!", "My digestion seems better"):**
```
That's the consistency kicking in! Your gut bacteria are
literally changing right now — adapting to process these
nutrients more efficiently.

By Day 7, the change becomes more noticeable. You're
building something real.
```

**If neutral ("Not sure I feel different", "Can't tell yet"):**
```
Totally normal. Real, lasting changes happen around Day 7-10.
Think of it like exercise — you don't see results after 3
gym sessions, but the work is happening internally.

Your gut is adjusting. Your body is absorbing micro-nutrients
it may not have been getting before. The key is: don't stop.
```

**If they mention a specific concern:**

| Concern | Response |
|---------|----------|
| "Too much ginger" | "I can dial it back — less ginger, more apple. Tomorrow's bottle will be adjusted." |
| "Too green-tasting" | "Totally fair. Want to try Citrus Morning or Beet Revival? Less leafy, more fruity." |
| "Too cold in the morning" | "Try leaving it out for 10 min before drinking, or add a splash of warm water." |
| "My stomach feels unsettled" | "Some people's stomachs need a few days to adjust to raw greens. Try drinking after a few sips of warm water. If it persists past Day 5, we'll modify the recipe." |
| "I forget to drink it" | "Put it right next to where you keep your morning water. See it → drink it. Habit stacking works." |

### 5.4 Day 3 No-Response Protocol

If customer hasn't responded to the Day 3 check-in by end of day:
- Do nothing. Continue delivery.
- Some people don't chat. That's fine. Their continued subscription IS their response.
- Only worry if delivery is refused or paused.

## 7. Day 6 — The Value-First Tip

### 7.1 Day 6 WhatsApp Message

This message is deliberately NOT about Soma. It's a general wellness tip that positions the founder as a wellness guide, not a juice seller.

```
Quick tip I learned this week:

Drinking a glass of plain water first thing in the morning —
even before your Soma — helps your body rehydrate after 8
hours of sleep. Your cells absorb the juice nutrients much
more efficiently when you're hydrated first.

Try it tomorrow: water → wait 5 min → Soma → wait 15 min →
breakfast.

Your morning sequence matters more than any single thing
you eat.
```

**Why this works:**
- It gives advice about water, not juice. Shows generosity.
- "Even before your Soma" — subtly positions Soma as part of the routine without selling.
- It's genuinely useful. The customer feels smarter for knowing this.
- It builds trust: "This person cares about my health, not just my subscription."

## 14. Onboarding Automation Path

### 14.1 Phase 1: Fully Manual (0-50 customers)

- Every message typed by founder personally.
- Delivery notes handwritten.
- Customer preferences tracked in a Google Sheet.
- Time per new customer onboarding: 30-45 minutes (spread over 7 days).

### 14.2 Phase 2: Template-Assisted (50-100 customers)

- Pre-written templates for each day (copy, personalize, send).
- Templates stored in WhatsApp Business quick replies.
- Customer tracker in Notion with onboarding stage per customer.
- Time per new customer onboarding: 15-20 minutes (spread over 7 days).

### 14.3 Phase 3: Semi-Automated (100-200 customers)

- WhatsApp Business API with scheduled messages.
- Onboarding sequence triggered automatically at sign-up.
- Day 3 and Day 7 responses still handled personally by founder.
- Customer CRM (simple — Notion database or Airtable).
- Time per new customer onboarding: 10 minutes (only personal responses).

### 14.4 Phase 4: Scaled (200+ customers)

- Dedicated customer success person (part-time, Rs 15,000-20,000/month).
- Automated onboarding sequence with personal intervention triggers.
- Founder handles only escalations and milestone celebrations.
- Full CRM with lifecycle tracking.

## 16. The Emotional Arc

The onboarding journey is designed to follow an emotional arc:

```
Day 0:  ANTICIPATION — "Something new is coming"
Day 1:  DELIGHT — "This is premium, personal, beautiful"
Day 2:  ROUTINE — "It just showed up again. This is reliable."
Day 3:  REASSURANCE — "Someone is checking on me. I'm not alone."
Day 4-5: QUIET CONFIDENCE — "This is becoming normal."
Day 6:  LEARNING — "I'm becoming smarter about wellness."
Day 7:  PRIDE — "I did it. One week. I'm the kind of person who
         does this."
Day 14: IDENTITY — "This is part of who I am now."
Day 21: HABIT — "I don't think about it. I just do it."
Day 30: BELONGING — "I'm part of something. The booklet, the
         cards, the tips. This is my wellness community."
```

**Every message, every card, every delivery is designed to move the customer along this emotional arc. Skip a step and the habit formation weakens. Overshoot and it feels manipulative. The balance is: genuine care, delivered consistently.**

*Habits are not built by products. They're built by rituals. The juice is the excuse. The booklet is the anchor. The WhatsApp check-in is the accountability partner. Together, they become a morning ritual that customers defend because it's theirs.*
