# 37 — Education Content System

> Soma Delights does not advertise. It educates. The 3-layer education system replaces ad spend with trust-building, turning every customer into someone who understands what they're drinking and why it matters.

---

## 1. The Three Layers

| Layer | Channel | Frequency | Format | Cost/customer/month |
|-------|---------|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| Layer 1 | WhatsApp micro-education | 2x/week + 1x/month | Text, voice notes, PDF | Rs 0 (broadcast) |
| Layer 2 | QR codes on bottles | Every delivery | Mobile web page | Rs 50-100/month (hosting) |
| Layer 3 | Ingredient cards in delivery bag | Every delivery | Printed A6 card | Rs 50-75/customer/month |

**Why three layers, not one:**
- Different people absorb information differently. Some read. Some listen. Some collect physical things.
- Repetition across channels reinforces learning without feeling repetitive.
- Each layer feeds the next: WhatsApp tip mentions an ingredient → QR page goes deeper → card becomes a keepsake.

## 2. Layer 1 — WhatsApp Micro-Education

### 2.1 Format Rules

**What we send:**
- 2-3 line text tips (readable in 5 seconds)
- 60-second voice notes from the founder (personal, conversational)
- Monthly PDF wellness guide (1-2 pages, beautifully designed)

**What we NEVER send:**
- Promotional messages ("Buy now!", "Special offer!")
- Long paragraphs (nobody reads walls of text on WhatsApp)
- Stock images or generic health graphics
- Forwarded messages from other sources
- Messages more than 2x per week (Tuesday + Friday only)

**Tone:** Like a friend who just learned something fascinating and wants to share it. Not a doctor lecturing. Not a brand selling. A person sharing.

### 2.2 Delivery Schedule

| Day | Type | Format |
|-----|------|--------|
| Tuesday | Ingredient Spotlight | Text (2-3 lines) or voice note (60 sec) |
| Friday | Wellness Habit Tip | Text (2-3 lines) |
| 1st of month | Monthly Wellness Guide | PDF (1-2 pages) |

**Why Tuesday and Friday:**
- Tuesday: people are settled into the work week, receptive to learning.
- Friday: end of week, people are reflective, good time for habit tips.
- Never Monday (too busy) or weekend (personal time, don't intrude).

### 2.5 WhatsApp Broadcast Mechanics

**Technical setup:**
1. Create a WhatsApp Business account for Soma Delights.
2. Create broadcast lists (max 256 contacts per list).
3. Customers must save Soma's number for broadcasts to reach them.
4. At onboarding: "Save this number to receive our weekly wellness tips."

**Broadcast vs Group:**
- NEVER create a group. Groups are noisy, people leave, spam risk.
- Broadcast: message goes to each person individually, feels personal, they can reply privately.

**Opt-out:** Every 3rd month, include a line: "Reply STOP if you'd like to pause wellness tips. No questions asked."

**Content calendar management:**
- Prepare 1 month of messages in advance (8-9 messages per month).
- Schedule in a simple spreadsheet: date, message text, format (text/voice/PDF).
- Sunday evening: founder records voice notes for the coming week.

### 3.4 Technical Implementation

**Option 1: Google Sites (Free, Fast)**
- Create a Google Site with one page per SKU.
- Advantages: free, easy to update, mobile-responsive by default.
- Limitations: limited design control, Google branding at bottom.

**Option 2: Simple HTML pages (Low Cost, Full Control)**
- Host on Netlify or Vercel (free tier).
- 6 static HTML pages with inline CSS.
- Total development time: 4-6 hours.
- Full design control, no external branding.
- Can add analytics (Google Analytics or simple Plausible).

**Option 3: Notion public pages (Free, Easiest)**
- Create Notion pages, make them public.
- Advantages: easiest to update, looks clean.
- Limitations: Notion branding, slower load times.

**Recommended: Option 2 for launch.** Simple HTML pages on Vercel. Free, fast, professional, trackable.

### 3.5 QR Code Analytics

Track scans to understand engagement:

| Metric | How to Track | Target |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Total scans per month | QR code with UTM parameters | 30%+ of deliveries result in a scan |
| Scans per SKU | Separate URLs per SKU | Identify most engaging content |
| Time on page | Google Analytics or Plausible | 30+ seconds average |
| Share button clicks | Event tracking | 5%+ of page visitors share |
| Repeat scans | Cookie or device tracking | Indicates content is worth revisiting |

**Insight loop:** If a particular SKU page gets very low scans, the content might not be compelling enough — update it. If a page gets high shares, use that content style for other SKUs.

### 5.2 Content Research Sources

**For ingredient facts and health claims:**
- PubMed (free): search for peer-reviewed studies on each ingredient.
- Examine.com: evidence-based supplement and nutrition information.
- USDA FoodData Central: nutritional composition data.
- IFCT (Indian Food Composition Tables): India-specific nutrition data.

**Rules for health information:**
- Every fact must be traceable to a credible source.
- No exaggeration. If a study says "may help," don't write "will help."
- No medical claims. "Supports immune function" is okay. "Prevents disease" is not.
- When citing a statistic (like "2,000% better absorption"), include the qualifier if the study was in vitro vs in vivo.

## 6. Measuring Education Effectiveness

### 6.1 Key Metrics

| Metric | Source | Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------|--------|----------------|
| WhatsApp read rate | Blue ticks | 70%+ | Are people even reading? |
| WhatsApp reply rate | Manual count | 10%+ on tips | Are they engaged enough to respond? |
| QR scan rate | Analytics | 30%+ of deliveries | Are they curious about their juice? |
| QR page time on site | Analytics | 30+ seconds | Are they actually reading? |
| QR share button clicks | Event tracking | 5%+ of visitors | Are they sharing with others? |
| Card collection mentions | Organic WhatsApp replies | Any mention = success | Is the collection effect working? |
| Referral attribution to education | Ask new sign-ups "how did you hear about us" | Track "friend shared a page" or "saw ingredient card" | Is education driving acquisition? |

### 6.2 Feedback Loop

**Monthly education review:**
1. Which WhatsApp messages got the most replies? → Create more like those.
2. Which QR pages got the most scans? → Those SKUs have engaged customers.
3. Which cards are customers mentioning? → Those ingredients resonate.
4. What questions are customers asking? → Create content that answers those questions proactively.

**Quarterly content refresh:**
- Update QR pages with new seasonal information.
- Refresh WhatsApp message library (retire messages that got low engagement).
- Introduce new card designs based on customer interest.

## 7. Scaling the Education System

### 7.1 Phase 1: Manual (0-50 subscribers)

- Founder writes and sends everything personally.
- WhatsApp broadcasts from personal Business account.
- QR pages are simple HTML or Google Sites.
- Cards designed in Canva, printed at local shop.

### 7.2 Phase 2: Semi-Automated (50-200 subscribers)

- WhatsApp Business API for scheduled broadcasts (cost: Rs 500-1,000/month).
- Content calendar managed in Notion or Google Sheets.
- Card designs templated — swap content, keep layout.
- QR pages hosted on Vercel/Netlify with proper analytics.

### 7.3 Phase 3: Systematic (200-500 subscribers)

- Content assistant (part-time, Rs 10,000-15,000/month) handles production.
- Founder still writes core content but assistant formats, schedules, tracks.
- Card printing on quarterly contracts with preferred printer.
- QR pages become a proper mini-site with search and ingredient database.

## 8. Education as Competitive Moat

Any competitor can make cold-pressed juice. Not every competitor will invest in educating their customers, because education doesn't show immediate ROI. It's slow. It's personal. It doesn't scale as easily as running a Google Ad.

That's exactly why it works.

**A customer who understands WHY they drink spinach juice every morning is 5x harder to churn than a customer who just likes the taste.**

- Taste preferences change. Knowledge doesn't.
- A competitor can offer a cheaper juice. They can't offer the relationship.
- A customer who collected 24 ingredient cards has invested attention. Switching means abandoning that investment.
- A customer who replies to WhatsApp tips feels known. They're not a transaction — they're in a conversation.

**Education is Soma's moat. Protect it. Invest in it. Never outsource the voice.**
