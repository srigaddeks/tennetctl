# Retention Strategy

## The Core Truth

Acquiring a subscriber costs time, effort, and credibility. Keeping them costs consistency. In a subscription business with zero advertising, retention IS growth — every subscriber you keep is one you don't need to replace. A 10% improvement in retention compounds more powerfully than a 50% increase in acquisition.

**Retention target trajectory:**

| Period | Target | What It Means |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Week 1 → Week 2 | 85%+ | They liked the product enough to continue past the trial |
| Month 1 → Month 2 | 70%+ | The habit is forming, delivery fits their routine |
| Month 1 → Month 3 | 55%+ | Real habit lock-in, they notice when they miss a day |
| Month 1 → Month 6 | 40%+ | Soma Delights is part of their identity, not just their morning |

If any of these numbers fall below target, stop acquiring and fix retention first.

## Onboarding: The First 7 Days

The first 7 days determine everything. A subscriber who feels welcomed, educated, and cared for in Week 1 stays 3x longer than one who just starts getting bottles with no context.

### Day 0: Welcome (After Sign-Up, Before First Delivery)

**WhatsApp message (send within 1 hour of sign-up):**

> Hey [Name]! Welcome to Soma Delights. Your first delivery arrives tomorrow morning at [time].
>
> Here's what to expect:
> - Your [product name] will be at your door by [time]
> - There's an ingredient card in the bag — it explains exactly what's in your drink and why each ingredient matters
> - I've also included a wellness booklet — take a look at page 3 and write down your wellness goal. It helps make this real.
>
> Quick tip: drink your [product] on an empty stomach for best absorption. Most people have it right when they wake up, before chai or coffee.
>
> Any questions at all, just message me here. I read every message personally.
>
> — [Founder name]

**What this does:** Sets expectations, creates anticipation, positions the founder as accessible, frames the product as a routine (not a purchase).

---

### Day 1: First Delivery

**The delivery itself IS the onboarding experience:**
- Product arrives within the promised 15-minute window (non-negotiable)
- Premium booklet included (physical, not PDF — the weight and feel matter)
- Ingredient card tucked into the bag (first of a collectible series)
- Bottle has QR code linking to the ingredient deep-dive page

**WhatsApp follow-up (send at 10 AM, after they've had the drink):**

> Good morning! Hope you enjoyed your first [product name]. How was it?
>
> Fun fact: the [key ingredient, e.g., "turmeric-black pepper combination"] in today's drink takes about 2-3 days to show its full effect on inflammation. So if you feel a bit of warmth in your stomach today, that's the curcumin getting absorbed. Totally normal and a good sign.
>
> See you tomorrow morning at [time]!

### Day 3: Check-In

**WhatsApp message (personal, not broadcast):**

> Hey [Name], Day 3! How are you feeling?

**What you're doing:** Reinforcing the habit narrative ("Day 3" = streak counting), prompting them to notice results, driving them back to the booklet.

### Day 5: Education Push

**WhatsApp message (voice note works best here):**

> [Voice note, 30-40 seconds]: "Hey [Name], just a quick wellness tip — [specific tip related to their chosen product]. **What you're doing:** Deepening the habit by adding a micro-action around the product. The drink becomes part of a ritual, not a standalone item.

**What you're doing:** Getting feedback (which shows you care AND gives you data), introducing the referral program at the perfect moment (they've experienced the product for a full week).

## Habit Formation Strategy

### Delivery Consistency Is Non-Negotiable

The milkman model works because the milk is there every morning, same time, no thinking required. Soma Delights must work the same way.

| Rule | Standard |
|------|----------|
| Delivery window | Same time daily, within ±15 minutes |
| Missed delivery protocol | If delivery will be late, WhatsApp the customer BEFORE the expected time. Never let them wonder. |
| Delivery confirmation | No confirmation needed — the product being there IS the confirmation |
| Holiday schedule | Communicate 2 days in advance. Offer double delivery the day before if they want. |
| Substitute product | If their chosen product is unavailable, WhatsApp to offer alternative BEFORE delivery, not after |

**The psychology:** When delivery is so consistent that the customer stops thinking about it, the product has become infrastructure. Infrastructure is not cancelled — it's maintained.

---

### Streak Tracking

People are wired for streaks. Use this.

**Weekly milestone (every Monday):**
> "Good morning! You've been on your [product] wellness routine for [X] days straight. Consistency is where the real results happen."

**Monthly milestone:**
> "One month in! 30 days of daily [product]. That's 30 doses of [key benefit]. Your body is thanking you even if you can't see it yet. Here's what happens in Month 2: [specific benefit that builds over time]."

**Streak milestones to celebrate:**
- 7 days — "First week done!"
- 14 days — "Two weeks of daily wellness. Most people quit new habits by now. You didn't."
- 30 days — "One month. This is officially a habit."
- 60 days — "Two months. The research says it takes 66 days to lock in a habit. You're almost there."
- 90 days — "Three months. You're now part of the Soma 90 club. Less than 30% of subscribers hit this milestone."
- 180 days — "Six months. Half a year of daily wellness. I genuinely admire your consistency."
- 365 days — "One year. Founding member status." (Special recognition, exclusive access)

### Monthly Wellness Check-In

On the 1st of every month, every active subscriber gets a personal check-in.

**Template:**

> Hey [Name], monthly wellness check-in time.
>
> You've been with us for [X months] now, doing [product] [frequency].
>
> Two quick questions:
> 1. What's one positive change you've noticed since starting?
> 2. Anything you'd like to adjust — different product, different timing, different frequency?
>
> Also, I noticed [personalized observation — e.g., "you paused for 3 days last week — everything okay?" or "you've been consistent for 45 straight days — amazing"].

**What you're doing:** Prompting them to articulate the value they're getting (which reinforces their commitment), offering flexibility (which reduces cancellation impulse), and showing personal attention.

---

### Booklet Progress Follow-Up

The premium booklet asks customers to write down their wellness goals on page 3.

**Monthly follow-up (Month 1, 2, 3):**

> Hey [Name], remember the wellness goals you wrote in your booklet? Pull it out and take a look.
>
> If you wrote "more energy" — have you noticed a difference in your afternoon slump?
> If you wrote "better digestion" — how's your gut feeling compared to a month ago?
>
> The booklet isn't just information — it's your roadmap. Every ingredient card we include connects back to those goals.

This creates a feedback loop between the booklet (physical object they own), their stated goals (commitment they made), and the product (daily reinforcement).

## Retention Mechanics

### 1. Pause Instead of Cancel

**Rule:** Never let a customer cancel without offering pause first.

| Pause Option | Duration | How It Works |
|-------------|----------|-------------|
| Short pause | 3-7 days | "Traveling this week? No problem. I'll pause deliveries and restart on [date]." |
| Extended pause | 1-2 weeks | "Need a longer break? You can pause for up to 2 weeks. Your streak will be waiting when you come back." |
| Travel pause | Custom dates | "Traveling from [date] to [date]? I'll pause and auto-restart when you're back." |

**What this does:** Converts a potential cancellation into a temporary pause. Most people who pause for a week come back. Most people who cancel don't.

**Implementation:** When someone says "I want to stop" — first response is ALWAYS "Would you like to pause instead? You can pause for up to 2 weeks and restart anytime."

---

### 2. Flexible Plan Switching

Make switching easy so cancellation is never the answer to "I'm bored of this product."

| Scenario | Response |
|----------|----------|
| "I'm tired of the same drink" | "Let's switch! Which of these sounds interesting? [list options]. I'll start the new one tomorrow." |
| "It's too expensive daily" | "How about alternate-day delivery? Same product, half the cost. Many subscribers do 3x/week." |
| "I want to try something different" | "We're launching [seasonal product] next week. Want to be the first to try it?" |
| "My family wants different drinks" | "We can do a family plan — different products for different people, same delivery." |

---

### 3. Seasonal Menu Changes

Prevent "flavor fatigue" by rotating the menu:

| Season | Menu Addition | Why |
|--------|--------------|-----|
| Summer (Mar-Jun) | Watermelon-mint cooler, raw mango-turmeric | Heat relief, seasonal ingredients at peak freshness and lowest cost |
| Monsoon (Jul-Sep) | Immunity booster (ginger-tulsi-pepper), warm turmeric milk option | Monsoon illness prevention, emotional comfort |
| Winter (Oct-Feb) | Warm wellness shots, beetroot-carrot warmer | Seasonal craving for warmth, root vegetables in peak season |
| Festival seasons | Special edition drinks (Diwali detox, Ugadi neem-jaggery, Sankranti sesame) | Cultural connection, limited edition creates excitement |

**Announce seasonal changes 1 week early** to build anticipation. "New seasonal menu dropping next Monday. Here's a sneak peek..."

---

### 4. Credits and Rewards for Streaks

| Streak | Reward |
|--------|--------|
| 30 days continuous | Rs 100 wallet credit |
| 60 days continuous | Rs 200 wallet credit + exclusive seasonal product sample |
| 90 days continuous | Rs 300 wallet credit + "Soma 90" recognition in community |
| 180 days continuous | Rs 500 wallet credit + founder's personal thank-you note |
| 365 days continuous | Rs 1,000 wallet credit + founding member status + free product for a week |

**Important:** These credits go to the same wallet as referral credits. This keeps the wallet active and growing, which creates its own retention pressure ("I have Rs 400 in my wallet — I should keep going to use it").

---

### 5. Referral Credits Keep the Wallet Active

The referral program (10% of referred customer's spend for 12 months) is not just an acquisition tool — it's a retention mechanism.

**How it works for retention:**
- Subscriber refers a friend → friend subscribes at Rs 150/day → referrer earns Rs 15/day = Rs 450/month
- After 2 referrals, the referrer is earning Rs 900/month in wallet credits
- Wallet is cashable at Rs 500 — so by Month 2, active referrers have a financial reason to stay
- Cancelling means losing the wallet income from ongoing referral credits

**Monthly wallet statement (WhatsApp):**

> Your Soma Wellness Wallet update for [Month]:
> - Referral credits earned: Rs [X] (from [N] active referrals)
> - Streak bonus: Rs [X]
> - Total balance: Rs [X]
> - [If ≥ Rs 500: "Your wallet is cashable! Reply 'CASH' to transfer to your bank account."]
> - [If < Rs 500: "Rs [X] more to cashable. Refer 1 more friend to get there faster!"]

---

### 6. Monthly Ingredient Card Collection

Every delivery includes a physical ingredient card. Each card covers one ingredient in depth.

**Design:**
- Front: Beautiful photograph of the ingredient + name
- Back: Why it matters, how it works in your body, best combinations, sourcing info
- Card size: 4"x6" (postcard size, fits in the delivery bag)
- Series numbering: Card #1, #2, #3... (creates collectibility)

**Collection mechanics:**
- 4 new cards per month (one new card each week)
- Repeat customers accumulate a collection
- "Collect all 12 from Season 1 for a complete ingredient guide"
- Special edition cards for seasonal ingredients (limited run)
- "Did you know? Card #7 (Black Pepper) and Card #3 (Turmeric) go together — the piperine in pepper increases curcumin absorption by 2000%"

**Why this retains:** Completionist behavior. People who have 8 cards don't want to stop at 8 — they want all 12. It's the same psychology as collecting stamps, stickers, or trading cards. Low cost (Rs 2-3/card to print), high emotional attachment.

## Churn Prevention

### Warning Signs

| Signal | Severity | When to Act |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Skipped 1 delivery | Low | Note but don't act yet — could be travel |
| Skipped 2+ deliveries in a week | Medium | Day 2 of skip: "Hey, noticed you paused a couple of days. Everything okay?" |
| No WhatsApp engagement for 2 weeks | Medium | Personal voice note from founder |
| Wallet unused for 2+ months | Low-Medium | "Your wallet has Rs [X] — here's how to use it" |
| Asked to switch to every-other-day | Medium | Accommodate gracefully, but this is often a step toward cancellation |
| Complaint about product quality | High | Fix immediately, apologize, send free replacement |
| "I want to cancel" | Critical | Pause offer → feedback → fix → win-back attempt |

---

### Early Warning Response Playbook

**Signal: 2+ skipped deliveries**
1. Day 2: WhatsApp (text): "Hey [Name], noticed you've paused a couple of days. Hope everything's okay! Just let me know when you'd like to restart."
2. Day 4 (if no response): WhatsApp (voice note): "Hey [Name], just checking in. If you need to adjust your delivery schedule — fewer days, different timing, different product — I'm happy to work with you. No pressure at all."
3. Day 7 (if no response): Final message: "Hey [Name], I'll keep your subscription on hold for now. Whenever you're ready to restart, just message 'START' and I'll resume the next morning. Your wellness wallet balance (Rs [X]) will be waiting. Hope you're well."

**Signal: Complaint about quality**
1. Immediate response (within 30 minutes): "I'm really sorry about that. Can you tell me what was wrong? I want to fix this."
2. Same day: Send replacement product for free
3. Next delivery: Include a handwritten note: "We fixed [the issue]. Thank you for telling us — it makes us better."
4. Day 3 follow-up: "How was yesterday's delivery? Back to the standard you expect?"

**Signal: "I want to cancel"**
1. "I understand. Before I process that — would a pause work instead? You can pause for up to 2 weeks and restart anytime."
2. If they insist on cancelling: "Of course. Two quick questions that would really help us improve:"
   - "What made you want to stop?" (single WhatsApp reply)
   - "Is there anything that would have made you stay?"
3. Process the cancellation immediately. Do NOT make it difficult.
4. 2 weeks later, one final message: "Hey [Name], hope you're doing well. We miss you on the morning route! If you ever want to restart, just message 'START'. [If they had referral credits:] Your wallet still has Rs [X] waiting."

---

### Win-Back Tactics

For churned subscribers (cancelled 2+ weeks ago):

| Timing | Tactic |
|--------|--------|
| 2 weeks after cancel | Personal message from founder: "Hey [Name], I was thinking about your feedback about [specific reason they cancelled]. We've [made a change / have a new product / adjusted pricing]. Would you like to try again for a week?" |
| 1 month after cancel | Share a new seasonal product: "We just launched [seasonal product]. Thought of you because [reason]. Want me to send a sample?" |
| 2 months after cancel | Community event invite: "We're hosting a wellness meetup at [location]. Would love to see you there — no sales pitch, just good conversation about health." |
| 3 months after cancel | Final attempt: "It's been a few months. Just wanted you to know the door is always open. Reply 'START' anytime and I'll restart your delivery the next morning." |

After 3 months with no response, move to "dormant" list. No more messages. Respect their decision.

---

### Exit Survey (2 Questions Max)

When someone cancels, ask exactly two questions via WhatsApp:

1. "What's the main reason you're stopping?" (Multiple choice)
   - Too expensive
   - Not seeing results
   - Don't like the taste
   - Delivery issues
   - Traveling/relocating
   - Found an alternative
   - Just want a break
   - Other: ___

2. "What would have made you stay?"

Track these in a spreadsheet. Review monthly. If "too expensive" is > 30%, you have a pricing problem. If "not seeing results" is > 30%, you have an education problem. If "delivery issues" is > 20%, you have an operations problem.

## KPIs and Targets

### Core Retention Metrics

| Metric | Month 1-3 Target | Month 4-6 Target | Month 7-12 Target |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Week 1 → Week 2 retention | 80% | 85% | 90% |
| Month 1 → Month 2 retention | 65% | 70% | 75% |
| Month 1 → Month 3 retention | 50% | 55% | 60% |
| Month 1 → Month 6 retention | — | 35% | 40% |
| Average subscriber lifetime | 2.5 months | 3.5 months | 5+ months |
| LTV per subscriber | Rs 11,250 | Rs 15,750 | Rs 22,500+ |

**How to calculate:** Monthly retention = (subscribers active this month who were also active last month) / (subscribers active last month)

### Cohort Analysis Approach

Track every monthly cohort separately:

**Example:**

| Cohort | Month 0 (start) | Month 1 | Month 2 | Month 3 | Month 4 | Month 5 | Month 6 |
|--------|-----------------|---------|---------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Jan 2027 | 20 | 14 (70%) | 11 (55%) | 9 (45%) | 8 (40%) | 7 (35%) | 7 (35%) |
| Feb 2027 | 30 | 22 (73%) | 18 (60%) | 15 (50%) | — | — | — |
| Mar 2027 | 45 | 34 (76%) | — | — | — | — | — |

**What to look for:**
- Each new cohort should retain better than the previous one (your onboarding is improving)
- Retention should flatten after Month 3-4 (the remaining subscribers are locked in)
- If retention drops below target for any cohort, investigate immediately

**Track in Google Sheets:**
- One sheet per cohort
- Columns: subscriber name, start date, status (active/paused/cancelled), cancel date, cancel reason, referrals made, wallet balance
- Summary sheet: cohort retention curves overlaid

### Engagement Scoring

Score each subscriber monthly (0-10):

| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Delivery consistency (no skips) | 0-3 |
| WhatsApp responsiveness (replies to check-ins) | 0-2 |
| Referral activity (made at least 1 referral) | 0-2 |
| Wallet activity (credits earned or used) | 0-1 |
| Community participation (active in WhatsApp group) | 0-1 |
| Feedback provided (surveys, suggestions) | 0-1 |

**Score interpretation:**
- 8-10: Champion — nurture, recognize, give ambassador status
- 5-7: Healthy — maintain current engagement
- 3-4: At risk — increase personal touchpoints
- 0-2: Danger zone — immediate founder intervention

## Retention Economics

### Why Retention Beats Acquisition (Numbers)

Assume: Rs 150/day subscription, Rs 300 CAC (pop-up + trial cost), 50% gross margin.

| Scenario | Month 1 Subscribers | Month 6 Active | Total Revenue (6 months) | Total Profit |
|----------|--------------------|-----------------|-----------------------------|-------------|
| 70% retention, 10 new/month | 15 | 68 | Rs 11,47,500 | Rs 5,73,750 |
| 60% retention, 10 new/month | 15 | 52 | Rs 9,22,500 | Rs 4,61,250 |
| 50% retention, 10 new/month | 15 | 40 | Rs 7,42,500 | Rs 3,71,250 |

**10% retention improvement = Rs 1,12,500 more profit over 6 months.** That's without spending a single extra rupee on acquisition.
