# Supplier & Vendor Directory — Hyderabad

> Soma Delights operational sourcing guide. All prices in INR, all locations Hyderabad-specific.
> Last updated: March 2026. Review and update pricing quarterly.

---

## Sourcing Principles

1. **Two-vendor rule** — always have a primary and backup for every category
2. **Relationship over price** — a reliable vendor at fair price beats a cheap vendor who ghosts you
3. **Cash is king** — cash payment at wholesale markets gets 5-15% better rates
4. **Early bird** — fresh produce markets: arrive 5:00-6:00 AM for best selection
5. **Weekly negotiation** — once you buy weekly, negotiate a standing rate (less than daily retail)

## 1. Fresh Ingredients

### 1.1 Leafy Greens (Spinach, Mint, Coriander, Celery)

**Primary: Bowenpally Wholesale Market**
- Location: Bowenpally, near Secunderabad railway side. ~25 min from Kukatpally
- Hours: 4:00 AM - 11:00 AM (best selection before 7:00 AM)
- What to buy: palak (spinach), pudina (mint), dhaniya (coriander), celery
- Price range:
  - Spinach: Rs 15-25/kg (wholesale), Rs 40-60/kg (retail)
  - Mint: Rs 30-50/bunch (wholesale), Rs 10-15/small bunch (retail)
  - Coriander: Rs 20-40/kg (wholesale)
  - Celery: Rs 80-120/kg (less common, may need to request)
- MOQ: No formal minimum, but vendors prefer Rs 200+ per transaction
- Payment: Cash only at wholesale. Some accept GPay for repeat customers
- Quality check: bright color, no yellowing, crisp stems, no sliminess, no wilting
- Frequency: every 2 days (leafy greens don't last; 3 days max in fridge)

**Backup: Rythu Bazaar (Kukatpally)**
- Location: Multiple locations — Kukatpally, Erragadda, Mehdipatnam
- Hours: 6:00 AM - 1:00 PM
- Advantage: farmer-direct, fresher, no middleman markup
- Price: 10-20% cheaper than regular vegetable shops
- Disadvantage: inconsistent availability, seasonal only
- Best for: when Bowenpally trip isn't possible

**Backup: Erragadda Market**
- Location: Erragadda, near ESI Hospital. ~15 min from Kukatpally
- Similar pricing to Bowenpally, slightly smaller selection
- Good for quick top-ups when Bowenpally isn't worth the trip

**Tips for leafy greens:**
- Buy Monday and Thursday (freshest supply days after weekend/mid-week harvests)
- Store in damp cloth inside fridge — extends life by 1-2 days
- Never buy pre-cut or wilted and try to "save" it — quality shows in the juice
- Build a relationship with one palak vendor — they'll set aside the best bunches for you

### 1.2 Root Vegetables (Carrots, Beetroot, Ginger, Turmeric)

**Primary: Bowenpally Wholesale Market**
- Carrots (Ooty/Delhi variety, orange): Rs 25-40/kg wholesale
- Beetroot: Rs 20-35/kg wholesale
- Ginger: Rs 80-150/kg (seasonal variation is huge — cheapest Oct-Feb)
- Fresh turmeric: Rs 40-80/kg (seasonal: Dec-Mar is peak fresh turmeric)
- Dry turmeric: Rs 150-250/kg (use only when fresh unavailable)

**Backup: Erragadda Vegetable Market**
- Slightly higher prices but closer to Kukatpally
- Good for: urgent same-day restocking

**Quality checks:**
- Carrots: firm, bright orange, no cracks, heavy for size (means juicy)
- Beetroot: firm, deep purple, smooth skin, small-to-medium size (sweeter)
- Ginger: tight skin (not wrinkled), snap when broken (not bendy), strong aroma
- Turmeric (fresh): firm, bright orange inside when scratched, earthy smell

**Storage:**
- Carrots/beetroot: 7-10 days in fridge (store dry, not in plastic bags)
- Ginger: 2-3 weeks in fridge wrapped in paper towel
- Fresh turmeric: 1-2 weeks in fridge

**Frequency: twice per week**

### 1.3 Fruits (Apple, Lemon, Amla, Cucumber)

**Primary: Kothapet Fruit Market (for apples, amla, specialty fruits)**
- Location: Kothapet/Dilsukhnagar area. ~40 min from Kukatpally
- Hours: 5:00 AM - 2:00 PM (wholesale), retail all day
- This is Hyderabad's largest fruit wholesale market
- Apple (green/Granny Smith): Rs 100-180/kg (imported, price varies by season)
- Apple (Shimla/Kinnaur): Rs 80-140/kg (Aug-Feb, best value)
- Apple (Royal Gala/Fuji): Rs 120-200/kg
- Lemon: Rs 60-120/kg (price swings wildly — cheapest in winter)
- Amla (Indian gooseberry): Rs 40-80/kg (Oct-Mar season, hard to find off-season)
- Cucumber: Rs 15-30/kg
- MOQ: Apples often sold by the box (4-5 kg, Rs 400-800)
- Payment: Cash, some wholesale vendors accept UPI for Rs 1000+ orders

**Backup: Bowenpally (for cucumber, lemon, common fruits)**
- Cucumber and lemon available at Bowenpally vegetable market
- Prices similar to Kothapet for these items
- Saves a trip across the city

**Backup: Miyapur/Chandanagar local fruit shops**
- For emergency small quantities only
- Expect 30-50% higher than wholesale
- Useful when you need 2 kg of apples today and can't make the trip

**Seasonal calendar for fruits:**

| Fruit | Peak Season | Off-Season Alternative |
|-------|------------|----------------------|
| Green apple | Year-round (imported) | Use Shimla apple in season (Aug-Feb) |
| Lemon | Oct-Mar (cheapest) | Always available, just pricier Apr-Sep |
| Amla | Oct-Mar | Skip or use amla powder (not ideal) |
| Cucumber | Year-round | Slightly pricier in monsoon |
| Orange/mosambi | Nov-Mar | Use for seasonal specials only |

### 1.4 Organic vs. Conventional — When It Matters

At bootstrap scale (under 50 customers), going full organic doubles your ingredient cost for marginal benefit. Be strategic:

**Worth going organic:**
- Leafy greens (spinach, coriander) — high pesticide surface area, can't peel
- Celery — one of the "dirty dozen" for pesticide residue
- Apples (if using with skin) — high pesticide retention

**Conventional is fine:**
- Beetroot — thick skin, peeled before use
- Carrots — peeled before use
- Ginger/turmeric — peeled, very thick skin
- Cucumber — if peeled; organic if using with skin
- Lemon — juice only, minimal pesticide transfer

**Where to source organic in Hyderabad:**
- 24 Mantra (organic store, Kukatpally/Gachibowli locations): Rs 60-100/kg for greens
- Sresta Natural (online delivery in Hyderabad)
- Sunday organic farmers markets (HITEC City, Jubilee Hills — irregular schedule)
- Direct farmer contacts via Hyderabad Organic Farming groups on WhatsApp/Facebook

**Recommendation:** Start conventional, switch leafy greens to organic by Month 2-3 when you've stabilized operations. Market it honestly: "We source organic where it matters most — our leafy greens."

## 2. Bottles & Caps

### 2.1 PET Bottles (250ml and 300ml)

**Option 1: IndiaMART Suppliers (Best for bulk)**
- Search: "250ml PET juice bottle Hyderabad"
- Multiple Hyderabad-based suppliers available
- Price: Rs 4-8 per bottle (depending on MOQ and quality)
- MOQ: typically 100-500 units minimum
- Lead time: 3-7 days for Hyderabad-based suppliers
- Payment: 50% advance, 50% on delivery (typical for first order; net 15-30 after trust built)

**Option 2: Amazon India (Quick start, small quantities)**
- Search: "250ml PET bottle food grade"
- Price: Rs 8-15 per bottle (premium for small quantity convenience)
- MOQ: packs of 10-50
- Lead time: 1-3 days (Prime)
- Good for: first 2-3 weeks while you find a bulk supplier

**Option 3: Local Packaging Dealers**
- Begum Bazaar area — several packaging material shops
- Abids / General Bazaar — packaging supplies
- Balanagar Industrial Area — manufacturers and distributors
- Price: Rs 5-9 per bottle
- Advantage: can see and test before buying, negotiate in person
- Ask for: "food-grade PET, BPA-free, with tamper-evident cap"

**Cap types:**
- Standard screw cap: Rs 1-2 per cap (included with most bottle orders)
- Tamper-evident cap (band breaks on first open): Rs 2-4 per cap
- Recommendation: start with tamper-evident. It looks more professional and builds trust

**For 60ml shot bottles:**
- Harder to find locally in small quantities
- Amazon: "60ml PET bottle with cap" — Rs 5-8 each
- IndiaMART: Rs 2-4 each at 500+ quantity

### 2.2 Glass Bottles (Future Upgrade)

- Not recommended for launch (breakage risk in delivery, heavier, costlier)
- When ready (Month 6+): Firozabad (UP) is India's glass capital — can order via IndiaMART
- Hyderabad local: Begum Bazaar has some glass bottle dealers
- Price: Rs 15-35 per bottle (250ml)
- MOQ: usually 100-200 minimum
- Requires deposit/return system — adds operational complexity

## 3. Labels & Printing

### 3.1 Bottle Labels (Sticker Labels)

**Option 1: Local Print Shops in Kukatpally**
- Several flex/sticker printing shops along Kukatpally main road and KPHB area
- Price: Rs 2-5 per sticker label (for waterproof vinyl/BOPP stickers)
- MOQ: 50-100 minimum per design
- Turnaround: 1-3 days
- Ask for: "BOPP sticker, waterproof, matte or gloss lamination" — this survives condensation from cold bottles
- Bring your design as a print-ready PDF (CMYK, 300 DPI)

**Option 2: PrintStop (Online)**
- Website: printstop.co.in
- Price: Rs 3-7 per sticker (depends on size and material)
- MOQ: 50+
- Turnaround: 5-7 business days + shipping
- Advantage: consistent quality, easy reorder
- Good for: when you've finalized design and want uniform quality

**Option 3: Vistaprint India**
- Website: vistaprint.in
- Price: slightly higher than PrintStop
- Better for business cards, not ideal cost-wise for bottle labels

**Option 4: Nampally / Abids Printers (for offset printing at scale)**
- When you need 500+ labels: offset printing becomes cheaper per unit
- Nampally area has several commercial printers
- Price: Rs 1-3 per label at 1000+ quantity
- Lead time: 5-7 days
- Requires print plate setup (one-time Rs 500-1500)

**Label specifications for Soma Delights:**
- Size: approximately 8cm x 12cm (wraps around 250ml bottle)
- Material: BOPP with matte lamination (premium feel, waterproof)
- Print: full color (greyscale background + product color accents per brand guidelines)
- Must include: product name, ingredients, net volume, FSSAI number, manufacturing date, best before, storage instructions

### 3.2 Education Booklets & Cards

**Ingredient cards / Daily knowledge cards:**
- Size: A6 (postcard size) or business card size
- Digital printing for under 500 copies: Rs 3-8 per card (local Kukatpally printers)
- Offset for 500+: Rs 1-3 per card (Nampally printers)
- Paper: 300 GSM art card with matte lamination (durable, premium feel)

**Starter kit booklet (8-12 pages):**
- Saddle-stitch (stapled) booklet
- Size: A5
- Digital printing (under 200 copies): Rs 30-60 per booklet
- Offset (200+): Rs 15-30 per booklet
- Find: offset printers in Nampally or Abids, get 3 quotes

**Business cards:**
- 300 GSM, matte lamination, spot UV optional
- Rs 1-2 per card at 200+ quantity (any local printer)

## 6. Ice & Cold Chain

### 6.1 Gel Ice Packs

- Amazon: "reusable gel ice pack food grade" — Rs 50-100 each
- Medical supply stores (Ameerpet): Rs 30-50 each
- Buy 15-20 to maintain rotation (half in freezer, half in use)
- Replace every 2-3 months (they lose effectiveness)

### 6.2 Cooler Boxes

- Decathlon HITEC City: insulated cooler boxes Rs 800-2,500
- Amazon: "insulated cooler box 10L/20L" — Rs 600-2,000
- For storage during production: one 20L box
- For delivery: insulated bags are better (lighter for rider)

### 6.3 Cold Chain Protocol

- Juicing → immediate bottling → into fridge (target: under 8 degrees C within 15 minutes)
- Delivery bags: 2 gel packs per bag maintains cold for 2-3 hours
- Delivery window: 6:00-8:00 AM (Hyderabad morning temp is 18-28 degrees C depending on season)
- Summer (Mar-Jun): add extra gel pack, tighten delivery window to 90 minutes max
- Monitor: digital thermometer check before dispatch (must be under 8 degrees C)

## 7. Vendor Relationship Management

### Building Relationships at Wholesale Markets

**First visit (reconnaissance):**
- Walk the entire market, note vendors, prices, quality
- Don't buy in bulk — buy small from 3-4 vendors to test quality
- Be friendly, introduce yourself, mention you'll be a regular buyer

**Second-third visit (testing):**
- Return to the 2 best vendors from first visit
- Buy your weekly requirement
- Pay cash, be respectful, arrive early
- Ask: "What days do you get the freshest stock?"

**After 2-3 weeks (relationship):**
- You're now a "regular" — ask for weekly rate
- "I'll be buying X kg every week — what's your best price?"
- Typical discount: 10-20% below daily retail
- Some vendors will set aside premium stock if you're reliable

**Monthly check-in:**
- Pay on time, every time (this matters more than haggling)
- Let vendors know if you're scaling up — they may offer better rates
- Get their phone number — call ahead to confirm stock availability

| Sep-Oct | Post-monsoon abundance, prices stabilize | Nothing major | Good time to test new recipes |
| Nov-Dec | Amla season starts, Shimla apples arrive, lemons cheap | Cucumber (slight rise) | Launch seasonal immunity specials |
