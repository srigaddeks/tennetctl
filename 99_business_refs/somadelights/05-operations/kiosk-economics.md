# 33. Kiosk & Pop-Up Economics — Acquisition Engine

> Pop-ups are the primary customer acquisition channel for Soma Delights. No ads. No influencers. Just a table, fresh samples, a smile, and a booklet.

---

## Why Pop-Ups Are the Growth Engine

| Channel | CAC | Conversion Quality | Scalability | Cost Control |
|---------|-----|-------------------|-------------|--------------|
| Meta/Instagram Ads | Rs 500-2,000 | Low (impulse clicks, high churn) | High but expensive | Poor — algorithm controls cost |
| Influencer Marketing | Rs 5,000-20,000/campaign | Variable (depends on influencer) | Moderate | Poor — one-time, unpredictable |
| Flyer Drops | Rs 1,000-2,000 CAC (0.1% conversion) | Very low (no taste experience) | Low | Good but low ROI |
| **Pop-Up Sampling** | **Rs 400-900** | **High (taste + conversation + booklet)** | **Moderate (founder's time)** | **Excellent — fully controllable** |
| Referrals | Rs 100-300 effective CAC | Highest (trusted recommendation) | Grows with customer base | Excellent — pay only for results |

**Pop-ups + referrals is the growth formula.** Pop-ups seed the first customers in a cluster. Referrals compound from there.

## Pop-Up Economics — The Funnel

### Conversion Funnel (Per 2-Hour Pop-Up)

```
People who walk past the pop-up:     100-200
                                        │
                                        ▼ (30-50% stop)
People who try a sample:               30-50
                                        │
                                        ▼ (30-40% leave contact)
Sign-ups (contact captured):            10-20
                                        │
                                        ▼ (20-30% order)
First-time orders:                      3-6
                                        │
                                        ▼ (50% subscribe)
Monthly subscribers:                    2-3
```

### Subscriber Lifetime Value vs. Acquisition Cost

| Scenario | Subscriber CAC | 3-Month LTV | 6-Month LTV | 12-Month LTV | ROI (3-month) |
|----------|---------------|-------------|-------------|---------------|----------------|
| Conservative (1 sub/pop-up) | Rs 2,000 | Rs 6,897 | Rs 13,794 | Rs 27,588 | 3.4x |
| Moderate (3 sub/pop-up) | Rs 667 | Rs 6,897 | Rs 13,794 | Rs 27,588 | 10.3x |

**Even the conservative scenario delivers 3.4x ROI in 3 months.** Pop-ups are the most efficient acquisition channel for a local, premium food brand.

### Break-Even Analysis Per Pop-Up

- Variable cost per pop-up: Rs 2,000
- Average subscriber monthly revenue: Rs 2,299
- Average gross margin: 55% = Rs 1,264/month profit per subscriber
- **Break-even: 2 months of 1 subscriber, or 1 month of 2 subscribers**
- After break-even, every additional month of subscription is pure margin contribution

### Setup Timeline

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| T-60 min | Load kit into vehicle. Check ice packs are frozen. Bottles packed. |
| T-30 min | Arrive at location. Scout exact spot (shade, footfall, visibility). |

| T-0 | Start engaging. Smile. Stand in front of table, not behind it. |
| T+2 hrs | Pack up. Count sign-ups. Note observations. |
| T+2.5 hrs | Send WhatsApp to all sign-ups (within 2 hours — urgency matters). |

### The Pop-Up Script

**Opening (when someone walks by):**

> "Good morning! Would you like to try a free sample of fresh cold-pressed juice? Made this morning at 4 AM."

*Hold out a sample cup. Smile. Don't block their path.*

**If they take a sample:**

> "That's our Green Vitality — spinach, cucumber, amla, and ginger. Everything in there was at the market yesterday and in your cup this morning."

*Wait for their reaction.*

**If they seem interested:**

> "We deliver these to your door every morning by 7 AM — like a milkman, but for wellness. No preservatives, no added sugar."

> "Here's a booklet that explains our philosophy and products. It also has a 21-day wellness tracker — it's actually kind of fun to fill in."

*Hand them a booklet. This is the key moment — the booklet does the selling from here.*

**If they want to order:**

> "Great! Can I get your name and WhatsApp number? I'll send you our menu tonight and we can start your first delivery tomorrow morning."

*Write on sign-up sheet or have them scan QR code to send a WhatsApp message.*

**If they're hesitant:**

> "No pressure at all! Take the booklet home, flip through it. If you're curious, the WhatsApp number is on the back — just send us a message anytime."

*Never be pushy. The booklet and word of mouth will do the work.*

### Post Pop-Up Follow-Up (CRITICAL)

**Within 2 hours of pack-up**, send a WhatsApp message to every person who signed up:

> Hi [Name]! Great meeting you at [Location] this morning. 🌿
>
> Here's our menu: [link or PDF]
>
> We deliver fresh to your door every morning by 7 AM. Starting at Rs 80/bottle, or Rs 1,999/month for daily delivery.
>
> Want to try your first delivery tomorrow? Just reply with your address and preferred juice!
>
> — [Your name], Soma Delights

**Why within 2 hours:** The sample taste is still fresh in their memory. The booklet is still in their hand. By evening, they've forgotten. Speed = conversion.

**Follow-up sequence:**
- Day 0 (same day): Initial message (above)
- Day 2: "Hi [Name], just checking — did you get a chance to look at our menu? Happy to recommend based on your preference!"
- Day 7: Final follow-up: "Hi [Name], last message from me! If you ever want to try Soma, we're just a WhatsApp message away. Have a great week!"
- After Day 7: No more messages. They're in your contact list for future pop-up invites.

## Scaling: From Pop-Up to Permanent Counter

### When to Consider a Permanent Counter

| Signal | Threshold | Action |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Pop-ups at same location 3+ times | 3 pop-ups | Evaluate for permanent presence |
| 10+ subscribers from one location | 10 subscribers | Strong demand signal — explore options |
| Gym partnership with 15+ member subscribers | 15 subscribers | Counter at gym reception |
| Apartment complex with 20+ subscriber families | 20 families | Counter in clubhouse/reception |

### Permanent Counter Options

#### Option A: Gym Reception Counter

| Spec | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **What** | Small branded shelf/stand at gym reception with daily-stocked bottles |
| **Cost** | Rs 2,000-5,000/month (negotiable — could be free if gym gets revenue share) |
| **Stock** | 5-10 bottles/day, restocked during morning delivery route |
| **Branding** | Branded shelf talker, standee, booklets on counter |
| **Payment** | QR code for UPI payment, or monthly subscription collected via WhatsApp |
| **Staff needed** | None — self-service. Gym reception staff points customers to the shelf. |
| **Break-even** | 8 bottles/day x Rs 99 = Rs 792/day revenue. At 55% margin = Rs 436/day profit. Counter rent Rs 3,000/month = Rs 100/day. **Break-even: 3-4 bottles/day.** |

#### Option B: Apartment Clubhouse Fridge

| Spec | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **What** | Small branded fridge (or shelf in shared fridge) in apartment clubhouse |
| **Cost** | Rs 3,000-5,000/month (or free with RWA partnership) |
| **Stock** | 10-15 bottles/day for pickup subscribers |
| **How it works** | Subscribers pick up from fridge instead of doorstep delivery. Saves delivery time for founder. |
| **Branding** | Branded fridge sticker, booklets on nearby table |
| **Staff needed** | None — self-service pickup |
| **Break-even** | 10 pickups/day x Rs 99 = Rs 990/day. Margin Rs 545/day. Rent Rs 4,000/month = Rs 133/day. **Break-even: 3-4 pickups/day.** |

#### Option C: Co-Working Space Partnership

| Spec | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **What** | Daily delivery of 10-20 bottles to co-working space pantry |
| **Cost** | Rs 0 (co-working space offers it as a member perk) |
| **Revenue model** | Co-working space buys at wholesale (Rs 70/bottle), sells at Rs 99 or offers free to premium members |
| **Break-even** | Immediate — wholesale price covers production cost |
| **Target** | 91Springboard, WeWork, T-Hub (Hyderabad co-working spaces) |

### Permanent Counter Economics

| Scenario | Monthly Revenue | Monthly Cost | Monthly Profit | ROI |
|----------|-----------------|--------------|----------------|-----|
| Gym counter (8 bottles/day, 26 days) | Rs 20,592 | Rs 12,376 (product + rent) | Rs 8,216 | 66% |
| Apartment fridge (12 pickups/day, 26 days) | Rs 30,888 | Rs 17,564 (product + rent) | Rs 13,324 | 76% |
| Co-working wholesale (15 bottles/day, 22 days) | Rs 23,100 (wholesale) | Rs 13,200 (production) | Rs 9,900 | 75% |

**Key insight:** Permanent counters become profitable at very low volumes (3-4 units/day). The real value is passive customer acquisition — new people discover Soma without pop-ups.

## Advanced: Pop-Up Variations

### The "Tasting Party" Pop-Up

- **Format:** Invite-only tasting event at apartment clubhouse
- **Size:** 10-15 people (intimate, conversational)
- **Cost:** Rs 1,500-2,500 (samples + booklets + small venue setup)
- **Duration:** 45 minutes (20 min presentation + 15 min tasting + 10 min Q&A)
- **Content:** Brief wellness talk, taste 3-4 SKUs, explain the habit philosophy
- **Conversion:** 40-60% to first order (much higher than open pop-up)
- **When:** After first 30 subscribers in an apartment complex — leverage existing customers to invite neighbours

### The "Gym Challenge" Pop-Up

- **Format:** 7-day challenge in partnership with gym
- **Offer:** 7 free bottles for gym members who sign up for the challenge
- **Cost:** Rs 3,500-5,000 (70 bottles for 10 participants)
- **Duration:** 1 week (deliveries to gym, members pick up after workout)
- **Conversion:** 30-50% to monthly subscriber after challenge
- **When:** After gym partnership is established (Month 2+)

### The "Office Wellness" Pop-Up

- **Format:** Pop-up in office building lobby during lunch break
- **Target:** IT professionals in Hyderabad tech hubs
- **Size:** 20-30 samples in 1 hour
- **Special offer:** Corporate subscription for teams (10+ people, 15% discount)
- **When:** After 50 subscribers (Month 3+), focus on weekday acquisition
