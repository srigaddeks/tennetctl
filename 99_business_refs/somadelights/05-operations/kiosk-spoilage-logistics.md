# 40 — Kiosk, Spoilage & Pop-Up Logistics

> Cold-pressed juice has no preservatives. Hyderabad reaches 45°C. A pop-up lasts 2 hours. If the cold chain breaks, the product is unsafe. This document is the operational manual for keeping juice safe, cold, and delicious at every outdoor touchpoint.

---

## 1. The Core Challenge

**The physics of the problem:**
- Cold-pressed juice (no HPP, no preservatives) must stay below 10°C at all times.
- Hyderabad ambient temperature: 28-45°C depending on season.
- A 300ml bottle left at 35°C will reach unsafe bacteria levels within 2-3 hours.
- Pop-up duration: 90-120 minutes.
- Margin for error: very small.

**The business reality:**
- Pop-ups are the primary customer acquisition channel (zero ads).
- Every pop-up must serve fresh, cold, safe samples.
- One food safety incident can destroy brand trust permanently.
- Cost of safety equipment must be amortized across many pop-ups.

**Rule #1: When in doubt, discard. No exceptions. No "it's probably fine."**

### 2.2 Cooler Box Selection Guide

**Not all cooler boxes are equal.** The cheapest Rs 500 boxes from local stores lose cold within 90 minutes in Hyderabad summer. Invest in a quality box.

**What to look for:**
- Wall thickness: minimum 3cm insulation
- Material: rotomolded polyethylene (not injection-molded — injection-molded has seams that leak cold)
- Lid seal: tight-fitting, ideally with a rubber gasket
- Drain plug: useful for draining meltwater without opening the lid
- Color: white or light-colored exterior (reflects heat better than dark colors)

**Recommended brands in India:**
- Cello (mid-range, good for Rs 1,500-2,000)
- Coleman (if available, excellent quality)
- Decathlon's Quechua cooler boxes (best value for quality)

### 2.3 Gel Ice Packs vs Ice

**Always use gel ice packs. Never use loose ice.**

| Factor | Gel Ice Packs | Loose Ice |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| Temperature consistency | Steady, gradual release | Melts fast, temperature swings |
| Mess | None — sealed packets | Meltwater everywhere, wet bottles |
| Reusability | Yes, freeze and reuse | No |
| Cooling duration | 4-6 hours | 2-3 hours |
| Cost over time | Lower (reusable) | Higher (buy each time) |
| Label damage | None | Meltwater soaks labels, ruins appearance |
| Hygiene | Clean | Meltwater can contaminate if dirty |

**Gel pack preparation:**
- Freeze packs at least 12 hours before use (overnight).
- A home freezer at -18°C will fully solidify 500g gel packs in 10-12 hours.
- Keep packs in the back of the freezer (coldest spot).
- Have a backup set so you always have frozen packs ready.

## 3. Pop-Up Cold Chain Protocol — Step by Step

### 3.1 Night Before (8-10 PM)

| Step | Action | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| 1 | Confirm pop-up location and time | Avoid morning surprises |
| 2 | Freeze gel ice packs (if not already frozen) | Must be solid by morning |
| 3 | Prep sample bottles (100ml portions in clean bottles) | Smaller portions, less waste |
| 4 | Count booklets, cups, gloves, sanitizer | Nothing worse than showing up without supplies |
| 5 | Charge phone (for QR sign-ups) | Dead phone = lost sign-ups |
| 6 | Check weather forecast for morning | Rain? Heat wave? Adjust plan |

### 3.2 Early Morning (5-6 AM on Pop-Up Day)

| Step | Action | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| 1 | Retrieve gel packs from freezer — check they're fully frozen | Partially frozen packs fail quickly |
| 2 | Pack cooler: bottom layer of gel packs | Cold sinks, so packs on bottom |
| 3 | Place sample bottles on top of bottom gel packs | Bottles surrounded by cold |
| 4 | Layer more gel packs around and on top of bottles | Encased in cold from all sides |
| 5 | Close cooler lid. Do not open again until at location | Every opening loses 3-5 minutes of cooling |
| 6 | Place cooler in vehicle — never in direct sun | Even in a car, shade matters |
| 7 | Pack separate bag: cups, gloves, sanitizer, booklets, thermometer, tray, table cover | Keep equipment separate from cold chain |

### 3.3 At Pop-Up Location (Arrival, 15 min before start)

| Step | Action | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| 1 | Set up table in shade. If no shade, use an umbrella | Direct sun on cooler = rapid temperature loss |
| 2 | Lay table cover, arrange booklets and cards | Professional presentation |
| 3 | Open cooler, check temperature with probe thermometer | Must read below 8°C. If above 10°C already, something went wrong |
| 4 | Place pouring jug + cups on table | Ready for first taster |
| 5 | Pour samples only when someone is ready to taste | Do not pre-pour. Pre-poured samples warm up in 10-15 min |
| 6 | Put on gloves before handling bottles or pouring | Hygiene standard |
| 7 | Keep cooler lid closed between pours | Every second open = temperature rise |
| 8 | Place hand sanitizer visibly on table | Shows hygiene commitment, customers appreciate |

### 3.4 During Pop-Up (90-120 minutes)

| Interval | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Every 15 min | Glance at cooler — is lid closed? Is it still in shade? |
| Every 30 min | Open cooler briefly, insert thermometer probe into a sample bottle. Record temperature |
| Every tasting | Pour into cup → hand to customer → close cooler. Never leave cooler open while chatting |
| Every 5-10 tastings | Change gloves. Sanitize hands before putting on new pair |
| If someone asks about ingredients | Have the full ingredient list ready (laminated card on table) |
| If someone asks about allergens | Check ingredient list. Common concerns: dairy, nuts, gluten (Soma is typically all three-free, but confirm per SKU) |

**Temperature log:**

| Time | Temperature (°C) | Action |
|------|-----------------|--------|
| Start | Should be 4-6°C | Good to go |
| +30 min | Should be 6-8°C | Normal rise |
| +60 min | Should be 8-10°C | Watch closely |
| +90 min | Should be 9-11°C | Consider wrapping up |
| +120 min | If above 12°C | STOP immediately |

### 3.5 Wrapping Up

| Step | Action | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| 1 | Stop serving when scheduled time is up, OR when temperature exceeds 12°C | Safety first |
| 2 | Close cooler. Take remaining bottles home | Don't leave anything behind |
| 3 | Clean table, collect all cups and waste | Leave location cleaner than you found it |
| 4 | Thank anyone who helped (security, apartment management) | Relationship building |
| 5 | Count: booklets distributed, cups used, sign-ups collected | Metrics for ROI |
| 6 | At home: discard any opened/poured-from bottles | Never re-seal and store |
| 7 | Rinse and air-dry cooler, gloves, equipment | Ready for next pop-up |

## 4. Spoilage Management

### 4.1 What Counts as Spoilage

| Situation | Classification | Action |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| Unopened bottles, temp stayed below 10°C, within 72 hours of pressing | Safe | Can be consumed or sold normally |
| Unopened bottles, temp exceeded 12°C for any period | Spoiled | Discard. Do not sell, do not consume |
| Opened/poured-from bottles, regardless of temperature | Spoiled | Discard after pop-up |
| Bottles past 72 hours from pressing, even if cold | Spoiled | Discard. No preservatives = strict shelf life |
| Juice that looks or smells different (off-color, fizzy, sour smell) | Spoiled | Discard immediately, do not serve |
| Juice that tastes different from expected | Potentially spoiled | Stop serving, discard remaining, investigate |

### 4.2 Spoilage Budget

**Expect 10-15% of prepared pop-up inventory to be unused/spoiled.** This is not waste — it's the cost of food safety.

| Pop-up size | Juice prepared (L) | Expected spoilage (L) | Spoilage cost (INR) |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------------|-------------------|
| Small (apartment gate) | 1.5L | 0.15-0.22L | Rs 75-110 |
| Medium (gym or park) | 2L | 0.2-0.3L | Rs 100-150 |
| Large (pandal or event) | 3L | 0.3-0.45L | Rs 150-225 |

### 4.3 Reducing Spoilage

| Strategy | How | Impact |
|----------|-----|--------|
| Better foot traffic estimation | Track attendance at each location over multiple pop-ups | Prepare right amount, not too much |
| Smaller sample portions | 50ml tastings, not 100ml | Same impressions, half the juice |
| Smaller batch bottles | Use 100ml sample bottles, not 300ml production bottles | Open one small bottle at a time, keep rest sealed |
| Timing | Choose high-traffic windows (7-9 AM morning walkers, 6-8 PM evening walkers) | More tastings per hour, less unused juice |
| Pre-registration | Post on apartment WhatsApp group: "Tasting at gate tomorrow 7-8 AM. Reply if interested." | Estimate demand before prepping |

### 4.4 What to Do with Unsold/Unused Samples

In order of preference:

1. **Founder drinks them.** If the bottles are sealed, within temperature, and within 72 hours — perfectly good. Save money on your own breakfast.
2. **Give to nearby people.** Security guards, apartment maintenance staff, helpers, delivery workers. Genuine goodwill that creates word-of-mouth among people who talk to everyone in the complex.
3. **Share with neighbors/friends.** If you're doing a pop-up in your own apartment complex, give extras to your neighbors. "Here, try this, I was doing a tasting downstairs."
4. **Compost.** If you've started the pulp/dehydrated strategy (see doc 08), unused juice can be mixed into pulp batches. Fiber and nutrients are preserved even if the liquid form is past its serving window.
5. **Discard.** If none of the above are feasible, pour down the drain. Do not re-bottle, re-label, or attempt to sell.

**NEVER:**
- Re-sell leftover pop-up samples as regular product
- Re-bottle into fresh bottles with new labels
- Serve the next day ("it was refrigerated overnight") — unless it was sealed, never opened, and within 72 hours of pressing
- Mix leftover juice into a new batch

## 5. Seasonal Pop-Up Adjustments

### 5.1 Summer Pop-Ups (March-June)

**Hyderabad summer reality:** 35-45°C, direct sun can push surfaces to 55-60°C. Asphalt radiates heat upward. Standing outdoors for 2 hours is exhausting for the founder, let alone the juice.

**Summer protocol changes:**

| Standard Protocol | Summer Adjustment | Why |
|------------------|-------------------|-----|
| Pop-up duration: 2 hours | Reduced to 90 min max | Beyond 90 min, cold chain is unreliable in 40°C |
| Any time of day | Early morning only: 6:30-8:00 AM | Before the real heat. After 9 AM it's dangerous for juice AND founder |
| Standard ice pack quantity | Double the ice packs. Fill cooler more fully | Extra thermal mass slows warming |
| Open-air location | Shade is mandatory. Under a tree, porch, awning, or umbrella | Direct sun on cooler = game over in 30 min |
| Standard cooler | Pre-cool the cooler: put ice packs in cooler 30 min before loading juice | A warm cooler warms the juice |
| Standard sample cups | Consider insulated cups (Rs 5-6/cup vs Rs 3-4) | Sample stays cold 5 min longer in customer's hand |

**Summer pop-up schedule:**
| Day | Time | Location Type |
|-----|------|--------------|
| Saturday | 6:30-8:00 AM | Apartment complex gate (morning walkers) |
| Sunday | 6:30-8:00 AM | Public park entrance (joggers) |
| Weekday (1x/week) | 7:00-8:30 AM | Gym entrance (pre-work fitness crowd) |

**Summer hydration angle:** Position tastings as "Hyderabad heat hydration" — people are actively looking for cooling, hydrating options. Cucumber Calm and Watermelon Cooler are hero products for summer tastings.

### 5.2 Monsoon Pop-Ups (July-September)

**Monsoon reality:** 28-35°C (cooler, which helps cold chain) but rain is unpredictable. Outdoor pop-ups can be rained out with 10 minutes' notice.

**Monsoon protocol changes:**

| Standard Protocol | Monsoon Adjustment | Why |
|------------------|-------------------|-----|
| Outdoor locations | Indoor only: apartment lobby, gym entrance, office building atrium | Rain ruins everything |
| 2 pop-ups/week | 1 pop-up/week | Fewer opportunities, focus on quality |
| Morning timing | Flexible — morning or evening (rain permitting) | Less heat constraint |
| Normal booklet quantity | Fewer booklets (10-15 per pop-up) | Reduced foot traffic |
| Standard cooler | Standard cooler is fine — cooler ambient helps | No extra ice needed |

**Monsoon content angle:** "Monsoon immunity" — ginger, turmeric, tulsi are monsoon hero ingredients. People worry about seasonal infections; Soma positions as daily immunity support.

**Rain contingency:**
- If rain starts during pop-up: pack up immediately. Wet booklets are ruined, wet customers don't taste.
- If rain is forecast but not started: proceed with indoor location only.
- If it rained overnight but morning is clear: go ahead, but bring extra towels and check if the ground/setup area is dry.

### 5.3 Winter Pop-Ups (November-February)

**Winter reality:** 18-28°C. The best season for pop-ups. Cold chain is easy. People are comfortable outdoors. Produce quality is at its peak.

**Winter advantages:**
- Pop-ups can run 2-2.5 hours (cold chain holds easily).
- Morning and evening slots both viable.
- Outdoor locations are comfortable for both founder and customers.
- Festive season (Diwali, Christmas, New Year) means higher foot traffic and gifting interest.

**Winter protocol changes:**

| Standard Protocol | Winter Adjustment | Why |
|------------------|------------------|-----|
| Standard ice packs | Can reduce by 25% — ambient cold helps | Save ice pack capacity for summer |
| 2 pop-ups/week | Can increase to 3/week | More comfortable, more productive |
| Morning only | Morning AND evening (5-7 PM) | Pleasant evenings attract walkers |
| Summer SKUs featured | Feature Golden Balance, Amla Charge, Winter Warmer | Warm-spice SKUs match the season |

### 5.4 Post-Monsoon (October-November)

Transitional period. Temperature rising again after monsoon, but not yet peak summer. Humidity can be high.

**Protocol:** Standard. No special adjustments needed. Watch humidity — high humidity doesn't affect cold chain directly but can make labels peel if they get damp.

## 7. Food Safety Standards at Pop-Ups

### 7.1 Non-Negotiable Rules

These rules apply at every pop-up, every time, no exceptions:

1. **Temperature:** Juice must be below 10°C at all times. Check every 30 minutes.
2. **Clean hands:** Sanitize before handling any bottle or cup. Wear gloves.
3. **Single-use cups:** Never reuse. Never rinse and reuse. New cup for every person.
4. **No double-dipping:** One pour per bottle opening. Do not pour from a bottle that someone has drunk from.
5. **Separate pouring and display:** Use dedicated pouring bottles. Display bottles are for visual only.
6. **Allergen readiness:** Know every ingredient in every SKU. Have the full list on the table.
7. **Discard opened bottles:** Any bottle that has been opened at a pop-up is discarded at the end. No exceptions.
8. **No reuse of leftovers:** Pop-up juice does not go into tomorrow's regular delivery.

### 7.2 Allergen and Dietary Information

Keep a laminated card on the table with:

| SKU | Contains | Free From |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| Green Vitality | Celery (if included) | Dairy, nuts, gluten, soy |
| Golden Balance | Honey (not vegan) | Dairy, nuts, gluten, soy |
| Beet Revival | — | Dairy, nuts, gluten, soy |
| Citrus Morning | Honey (if included) | Dairy, nuts, gluten, soy |
| Cucumber Calm | — | Dairy, nuts, gluten, soy |
| Amla Charge | Honey (if included) | Dairy, nuts, gluten, soy |

**When someone asks about allergies:**
- If the answer is clearly on the card: point to it.
- If you're unsure: "I'll need to check the full ingredient list. Give me one moment." Never guess.
- If the person has a severe allergy (anaphylaxis risk): "I want to be completely transparent — I make these in a home kitchen, not a dedicated allergen-free facility. I can't guarantee zero cross-contamination. I'd rather be honest than risk your health."

### 7.3 FSSAI Compliance at Pop-Ups

- Display your FSSAI license number visibly (on the table or on the bottle label).
- If anyone (customer, inspector, competitor) asks to see your license, you should have it on your phone (photo) or a printed copy.
- FSSAI registration is required for selling food products. Registration, not license (for turnover under Rs 12 lakh/year). Cost: Rs 100 via FoSCoS portal.
- As turnover grows, upgrade to FSSAI State License (Rs 2,000-5,000/year).

## 8. Emergency Protocols

### 8.1 Temperature Breach

**If temperature exceeds 12°C:**
1. Stop serving immediately. Close cooler lid.
2. Do not panic. Do not argue with the thermometer.
3. Announce (if there's a queue): "I'm pausing for a quality check. One moment please."
4. Check all bottles with thermometer. If any bottle is above 12°C, it's out.
5. If you have sealed, cold bottles in the backup cooler: switch to those.
6. If no backup: end the pop-up early. "Quality is everything to us, and the samples have warmed past our standard. I'd rather end early than serve you something that isn't perfect."
7. Note: "warmed past our standard" not "unsafe" — avoid alarming language.

**If temperature exceeds 15°C:**
1. STOP. Discard everything. No salvage attempt.
2. Pack up. Thank anyone waiting. Explain simply: "Our freshness window has closed for today."
3. Log the incident: why did it happen? Cooler quality? Too much sun? Too many lid openings?
4. Fix the root cause before the next pop-up.

### 8.2 Product Quality Issue

**If juice looks off (unusual color, separation, cloudiness beyond normal):**
1. Do not serve. Do not taste-test in front of customers.
2. Step away. Smell and visually inspect privately.
3. If in doubt: discard. It costs Rs 150 to waste juice. It costs your brand to serve bad juice.
4. If you identify the issue (e.g., oxidation from slow-pressing the previous day), note it and fix the process.

**If juice smells off (fermented, sour, "fizzy" smell):**
1. Fermentation has begun. Bacteria are active. Discard immediately.
2. Do not let customers smell it ("Does this smell weird to you?" — NO).
3. Pack up that SKU. If other SKUs are fine (sealed, cold, good smell), continue with those.

### 8.3 Customer Complaint

**If a customer says "this doesn't taste right":**
1. Listen. Do not argue. "Thank you for telling me. Can you describe what tastes off?"
2. Taste from the same bottle privately. If you agree: discard the batch, switch to backup.
3. If you disagree (taste seems normal): "I appreciate the feedback. Cold-pressed green juice has a stronger flavor than packaged juice — it's more raw and earthy. But your feedback is important, and I want you to have a good experience."
4. Offer: a different SKU to try, or a booklet to learn more about the flavors.
5. Log every complaint. Patterns reveal real issues.

**If a customer reports feeling unwell after tasting:**
1. Take it seriously. Ask what symptoms, when they started, what else they ate/drank.
2. If symptoms suggest foodborne illness (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea within 4-24 hours): "I'm very sorry. Can I have your contact details? I want to follow up personally."
3. Immediately check: was the juice within temperature? Within 72-hour window? Any signs of spoilage?
4. Stop all pop-ups until the cause is identified.
5. If confirmed food safety issue: contact FSSAI, report transparently, fix root cause, communicate to all customers.
6. Document everything: timestamps, temperatures, batch details, customer details.

## 9. Pop-Up Operations Kit — Packing Checklist

### 9.1 The Night-Before Checklist

Print this and check off before every pop-up:

**Cold Chain:**
- [ ] Gel ice packs in freezer (fully frozen — check by squeezing, must be solid)
- [ ] Cooler box clean and dry
- [ ] Sample bottles prepped (100ml each, sealed, labeled)
- [ ] Backup sealed bottles (2-3 extra, in separate small cooler)
- [ ] Digital thermometer (battery working — test before packing)

**Serving:**
- [ ] Disposable cups (count: expected tastings + 50% extra)
- [ ] Pouring jug (clean, dry)
- [ ] Disposable gloves (2 pairs per hour of pop-up)
- [ ] Hand sanitizer (pump bottle, at least half full)
- [ ] Serving tray

**Marketing:**
- [ ] Booklets (20-30 per pop-up)
- [ ] Ingredient cards (5-10 sample cards to show, not give away at pop-ups)
- [ ] Sign-up form (Google Form on phone, or paper backup)
- [ ] Business cards / referral code cards
- [ ] Laminated allergen/ingredient card
- [ ] FSSAI registration display

**Logistics:**
- [ ] Fold-up table (if location doesn't provide one)
- [ ] Table cover (white, clean)
- [ ] Umbrella or shade solution (summer essential)
- [ ] Waste bag (for used cups, gloves)
- [ ] Phone charged (for sign-ups, photos, emergency contact)
- [ ] Water bottle for yourself (you'll be standing for 2 hours in Hyderabad)
- [ ] Cash (Rs 500 — for unexpected expenses like buying extra ice)

## 11. Scaling Pop-Up Operations

### 11.1 Phase 1: Founder Only (0-50 subscribers)

- Founder does everything: makes juice, sets up pop-up, serves, cleans up.
- 2-3 pop-ups per week maximum (time constraint).
- Locations: walking distance or short drive from home kitchen.

### 11.2 Phase 2: Founder + Helper (50-100 subscribers)

- Hire a morning helper (Rs 5,000-8,000/month, part-time) for pop-up days.
- Helper handles setup, serving, cleanup. Founder focuses on conversations and sign-ups.
- Founder still preps all juice and packs coolers.
- Can increase to 3-4 pop-ups per week.

### 11.3 Phase 3: Pop-Up Kit Model (100-200 subscribers)

- Create 2 standardized pop-up kits (cooler + equipment + materials).
- Train ambassadors to run pop-ups at their gym/studio/apartment.
- Founder supplies juice; ambassador handles the pop-up.
- Ambassador earns commission on sign-ups from "their" pop-up.
- Can run 5-8 pop-ups per week across multiple locations simultaneously.

### 11.4 Phase 4: Permanent Kiosk (200+ subscribers)

- Investigate a small permanent kiosk at a high-traffic apartment complex or gym.
- Kiosk model: daily supply from home kitchen, staffed by part-time employee.
- Monthly kiosk rent: Rs 5,000-15,000 depending on location.
- Kiosk doubles as delivery hub for that neighborhood.
