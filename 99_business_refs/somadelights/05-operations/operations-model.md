# 12 — Operations Model

> This is the operating blueprint. Every morning is a production run. Consistency is the product. A customer who gets their juice at 6:45 AM every day trusts you. A customer who gets it at 6:45 one day and 8:15 the next day cancels.

## Procurement

### Source Strategy (Hyderabad)

| Source | What to Buy | When to Visit | Pricing | Quality | Reliability |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Bowenpally Wholesale Market** | Roots (carrot, beetroot), fruits (apple, orange), ginger | 2-3x/week, 5:00-7:00 AM | Cheapest in city. 20-40% below retail. | Variable — inspect carefully. | High volume, always available. |
| **Rythu Bazaar (Kukatpally)** | Leafy greens (spinach, mint), amla, fresh turmeric, lemons | Daily or alternate days (leafy greens wilt fast) | 10-20% below retail. | Better than Bowenpally for greens. | Good — government-run, direct from farmers. |
| **Local farm direct** | Spinach, cucumber (if you find a reliable farmer) | Weekly order, they deliver | Negotiable. Often at par with Rythu Bazaar. | Best — picked same day. | Low until relationship is established (3-6 months). |
| **BigBasket / Jiomart (backup)** | Emergency restocking only | When you run out mid-week | Retail price. 30-50% more expensive. | Consistent but not wholesale-fresh. | App-based, same-day delivery. |

### Procurement Schedule

| Ingredient Category | Buy Frequency | Why | Storage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Leafy greens** (spinach, mint) | Daily or alternate day | Wilts in 2-3 days. Buy only what you need for 1-2 days. | Damp cloth in fridge, use within 48 hours |
| **Root vegetables** (carrot, beetroot) | 2x/week | Lasts 5-7 days refrigerated | Cool, dry place or fridge. Remove tops. |
| **Fruits** (apple, orange) | 2x/week | Apples last 1-2 weeks. Oranges 5-7 days. | Room temp (apples), fridge (oranges) |
| **Ginger, turmeric** | 1x/week | Lasts 2 weeks refrigerated | Wrapped in paper towel, fridge |
| **Lemons** | 2x/week | Dry out after 5-7 days | Room temp, away from direct light |
| **Coconut water** | 1x/week | Packaged — shelf stable | Room temp |
| **Honey, pepper, salt** | 1x/month | Shelf stable | Pantry |
| **Bottles, caps, labels** | 1x/month (bulk order) | No spoilage risk | Dry storage |

### Supplier Relationship Management

1. **Build relationships at Bowenpally.** Go to the same 2-3 vendors every visit. Within a month, they will hold quality produce for you and offer better rates.
2. **Pay cash at wholesale markets.** UPI is accepted but cash gets you 5-10% better deals and faster service at 5 AM.
3. **Inspect every purchase.** Check for:
   - Wilted or yellowed greens → reject
   - Soft spots on apples → reject
   - Sprouting ginger → acceptable if firm
   - Cracked or dried lemons → reject
   - Beetroot with soft spots → reject
4. **Weigh everything.** Wholesale vendors round up. A "1 kg" bag might be 800g. Carry a small digital scale (Rs 500).
5. **Track prices weekly.** Maintain a simple notebook/sheet of what you paid per kg. Seasonal price spikes are normal — adjust menu or absorb, don't cut quality.

### Quality Checks at Receiving

| Check | Method | Pass/Fail Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| Visual inspection | Look at every item | No mold, no excessive bruising, no wilting |
| Smell test | Sniff greens and fruits | Should smell fresh and clean. Any sour/off smell = reject. |
| Firmness test | Squeeze gently | Firm = good. Soft/mushy = reject (except ripe oranges). |
| Weight verification | Digital scale | Within 10% of paid weight. If consistently short, switch vendor. |
| Temperature (if delivered) | Touch test / thermometer | Should not be warm/hot. Greens should feel cool. |

## Batching Logic

### Produce-to-Order Model

| Principle | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Subscriptions = predictable demand** | You know by 8 PM exactly how many of each SKU to make tomorrow |
| **Buffer** | Make 10% extra (round up). 20 orders = make 22 bottles. |
| **Buffer purpose** | Replacement for quality failures, dropped bottles, extra for new trial requests |
| **Unsold buffer** | Use for personal consumption, give to building security/watchman (goodwill), or deliver as "surprise bonus" to a subscriber |
| **Never make more than 20% buffer** | Overproduction = waste = lost money |

### Batch Sequence (Always Juice in This Order)

1. **Lightest color first** — Green Morning, Hydration Cooler
2. **Medium color** — ABC Classic, Citrus Immunity
3. **Darkest color last** — Beetroot Recharge
4. **Staining ingredients last** — Turmeric Ginger Shot

**Why:** Beetroot and turmeric stain the juicer. If you juice beetroot first, your Green Morning will be pink. Always go light-to-dark.

Quick rinse between batches (30 seconds of water through the juicer). Full disassembly cleanup only at end.

## Quality SOP

### Ingredient Freshness (Pre-Production Check)

| Check | How | Action if Failed |
|---|---|---|
| Greens still perky | Visual + touch — should be crisp, not limp | Discard wilted portions. If >30% wilted, skip that SKU for the day, notify affected subscribers. |
| Fruits firm | Squeeze test | Cut off bruised sections. If >20% bruised, replace from backup stock. |
| No mold on any ingredient | Visual inspection of every item | Discard entire piece (mold spreads invisibly). Never cut around mold. |
| Ginger/turmeric firm | Squeeze — should not bend easily | Trim soft sections. If entirely soft, skip for the day. |
| Lemons juicy | Roll on counter — should feel heavy for size | Dry lemons give no juice. Replace. |

### Equipment Sanitization

| Step | When | Method |
|---|---|---|
| Full disassembly wash | After every production run | Hot water + food-safe detergent. Scrub all juicer parts. |
| Surface sanitization | Before production starts | Food-safe sanitizer spray on all surfaces. Wipe with clean cloth. |
| Cutting board sanitization | Before and after use | Hot water + vinegar soak. Separate boards for fruits and vegetables. |
| Bottle inspection | Before filling | Visual check — no cracks, no debris inside, cap threads clean |
| Hand sanitization | Before production, after any break | Soap + water (30 seconds), then food-safe hand sanitizer |

### Temperature Monitoring

| Checkpoint | Target | Method |
|---|---|---|
| Juice immediately after pressing | Below 15°C | Cold-press generates minimal heat. If juice feels warm, something is wrong with the juicer. |
| Juice in bottle before delivery | Below 10°C | Refrigerate immediately after bottling. Check with food thermometer. |
| Juice at customer delivery | Below 12°C | Insulated bag + ice pack should maintain. Random check: carry thermometer on 1 delivery/week. |
| Fridge temperature | 2-5°C | Check daily. Invest in a fridge thermometer (Rs 200). |

### Taste Test Protocol

1. Open 1 bottle from each batch produced
2. Taste for: sweetness (natural, not fermented), bitterness (if unexpected, something is off), freshness (should taste clean)
3. Visual check: color is consistent with previous batches, no separation (except citrus — slight separation is normal), no floating particles/foam (slight foam on green juice is normal)
4. **If any batch fails taste test:** Do not deliver that SKU. Notify affected subscribers with WhatsApp: "Today's [SKU name] did not meet our quality standard. We are replacing it with [alternate SKU]. Quality is non-negotiable for us."

This message, sent proactively, builds more trust than 100 perfect deliveries.

### Shelf Life Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Maximum shelf life | 24 hours from production (cold-pressed, no HPP, no preservatives) |
| Label every bottle | Production date + time. "Best before" = production time + 18 hours. |
| Customer instruction | "Refrigerate immediately. Consume within 24 hours of production time on label." |
| Internal rule | Any bottle not delivered within 2 hours of production window goes to personal consumption or disposal. Never deliver a bottle produced yesterday. |

## Cold Chain Management

### Equipment

| Item | Specification | Cost | Quantity Needed | Lifespan |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Insulated delivery bag | 15-20L, food-grade, zip closure | Rs 300-500 | 2-3 bags (1 per route) | 6-12 months |
| Gel ice packs (reusable) | 200-400g, food-safe | Rs 60-80 each | 10-15 packs (rotate) | 100+ uses |
| Fridge thermometer | Digital, -20 to 50°C | Rs 200 | 1 | 2+ years |
| Probe thermometer | Instant-read, for spot checks | Rs 300-500 | 1 | 2+ years |

### Cold Chain Protocol

```
Production (4-5:30 AM)
    ↓ Juice pressed at ~10°C
Bottling (5:30 AM)
    ↓ Immediately into fridge (2-5°C)
Packing (5:50-5:55 AM)
    ↓ From fridge into insulated bag with ice packs
    ↓ Bag zipped shut, stays closed until delivery
Delivery (6:00-7:30 AM)
    ↓ Each bottle handed to customer or placed at door
Customer
    ↓ Refrigerate immediately
    ↓ Consume within 24 hours
```

### Temperature Failure Scenarios

| Scenario | Likely Cause | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Juice warm at delivery | Ice pack melted, bag left open, delayed route | Apologize, offer replacement next day. Add extra ice pack to that route. |
| Fridge too warm (>8°C) | Fridge overloaded or malfunctioning | Reduce fridge contents. If persistent, repair/replace. |
| Hot weather (Hyderabad summer 42°C+) | Ambient temperature overwhelms insulation | Double ice packs in summer. Consider frozen gel packs (freeze overnight). |

## Hygiene SOP

### Personal Hygiene

| Requirement | Standard |
|---|---|
| Hand washing | Before production, after bathroom, after touching phone/face. Soap + water 30 seconds. |
| Gloves | Disposable food-safe gloves during production. Change between batches. Rs 300/box of 100. |
| Hair covering | Hairnet or cap during production. Non-negotiable. Rs 200/pack of 50. |
| Apron | Clean apron every day. Dedicated production apron (not cooking apron). |
| Illness policy | If sick (cold, cough, stomach issue), do NOT produce. Cancel deliveries for the day. Notify subscribers. |

### Kitchen Hygiene

| Area | Cleaning Frequency | Method |
|---|---|---|
| Production surface | Before and after every session | Food-safe sanitizer spray + clean cloth |
| Floor | After every session | Mop with disinfectant |
| Sink | After every session | Scrub with detergent |
| Fridge interior | Weekly | Empty, wipe with baking soda solution |
| Waste bin | Daily | Empty, wash with disinfectant |
| Drain | Weekly | Hot water + baking soda flush |

### Pest Control

| Measure | Frequency | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Kitchen inspection for ants/cockroaches | Daily (visual) | Check under sink, behind fridge, near drain |
| Seal all food containers | Always | No open bags of produce overnight |
| Pest spray (food-safe) | Monthly | Use herbal/food-safe only. Never chemical spray near production area. |
| Professional pest control | Quarterly | Rs 500-800 per visit. Worth it. |
| Fly screens on windows | Always | Install if not present (Rs 500-1,000 per window) |

## Failure Modes and Recovery

| Failure | Probability | Impact | Recovery |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Juicer breaks** | Medium (1x per 3-6 months) | High — no production | Stage 2 has backup juicer. Stage 1: have repair contact saved, buy parts proactively (blade, filter). |
| **Rider doesn't show up** | Medium | High — no delivery | You deliver. Always have a backup plan. Keep your own bike/scooter fueled. |
| **Power outage** | Low-Medium (Hyderabad is decent but not perfect) | Medium — fridge warms, can't juice | Inverter/UPS for fridge (Rs 8,000-12,000). Juicer can run on inverter. |
| **Ingredient shortage at market** | Low | Medium — can't make specific SKU | Always have 1-day buffer of root vegetables. Substitute SKU and notify customer. |
| **You get sick** | Low-Medium | Critical at Stage 1 | Train a family member on basic production. Have 1-day emergency plan. At Stage 2+, helper can run production solo. |
| **Customer complaint (quality)** | Medium | Low per incident, high if repeated | Same-day replacement or next-day free bottle + apology. Log every complaint. 3 complaints about same issue = systemic fix needed. |
