# Week 1 Founder Checklist — Before Your First Bottle
### Everything that must be done before you press, bottle, and deliver to your first paying customer

> This is your personal pre-launch playbook. Read it once end-to-end before you start. Then work through it day by day. Do not skip items — each one exists because a real founder learned the hard way that skipping it costs more time and money than doing it upfront.

---

## Before You Begin

A few things to keep in mind as you work through this week:

- **Done is better than perfect.** Your first label does not need to be beautiful. Your first batch does not need to be flawless. Your first 5 customers are buying because they believe in you — give them a good product and honest communication, and you will earn the right to iterate.
- **Write everything down from Day 1.** Every batch, every rupee spent, every piece of feedback. The habit of documentation will save you when you scale and when an inspector shows up.
- **You are not ready to take a paid order until you clear the Pre-Launch Gate at the bottom of this document.** Do not rush past it.

---

## Day 1 — Compliance, Accounts & First List

The goal today is to get your paperwork in order, your business tools set up, and your first 20 names written down. No pressing, no shopping — just foundation.

- [ ] **Print your FSSAI license and store it in a plastic sleeve in the kitchen.** You are legally required to display it at your production site. If an inspector walks in on Day 5, you need it visible. Keep the digital copy in your Google Drive under Compliance.
- [ ] **Create a WhatsApp Business account for Soma Delights — separate from your personal number.** If you do not have a spare SIM, pick one up at any Airtel or Jio store in Kukatpally for under Rs 100. Your personal number stays personal. This matters more than it seems when you have 40 customers messaging you at 6 AM.
- [ ] **Configure WhatsApp Business fully:** set the display name to "Soma Delights", upload a profile photo (your logo, a bottle photo, or even a clean fruit-on-white photo for now), set business hours (6 AM–8 PM), and write an away message that explains you will respond within 2 hours during production mornings.
- [ ] **Create a Google Drive folder called "Soma Delights Operations"** with these subfolders: Recipes, Batch Logs, Customer List, Finances, Compliance. This is your operations brain. Every document you create this week goes in here.
- [ ] **Open a dedicated UPI ID or bank account for Soma Delights business transactions.** Even a separate Google Pay account linked to a secondary number works. Do not mix personal and business money — not even for Rs 50 of coriander. It will cause accounting pain within 3 weeks.
- [ ] **Write down your first 20 potential customers** in a Google Sheet — columns: Name, WhatsApp Number, Relationship (friend / family / neighbor / colleague / gym contact), Likelihood to Buy (High / Medium / Low). These 20 people are your launch cohort. Treat this list seriously.

## Day 2 — Kitchen Setup & Equipment

Today you are turning your kitchen into a compliant micro-production unit. This is physical work — plan for 4–6 hours.

- [ ] **Deep clean and sanitize all kitchen surfaces.** Use a diluted bleach solution (1 tablespoon bleach per 4 litres of water), wipe all surfaces, let sit for 2 minutes, then rinse thoroughly with clean water. Sanitizing is not the same as cleaning — do both.
- [ ] **Designate a dedicated production zone** — a specific counter or table that is only used for Soma production during production hours. Mark it off mentally or physically. No personal food prep happens in this zone when you are producing.
- [ ] **Test your cold-press juicer.** Run a 500g test batch with whatever produce you have — oranges, apples, anything. Confirm it runs cleanly, there are no leaks, and the output looks correct. If it has not been used in weeks, clean it thoroughly first. Do not discover a broken juicer on Day 5 at 4 AM.
- [ ] **Confirm or purchase all packaging and delivery supplies:**
  - Glass bottles: minimum 50 (200ml or 250ml — decide your standard size now and stick with it)
  - Caps that fit your bottles — buy 100
  - Labels: blank printable or space to hand-write for now — you will order printed ones on Day 4
  - Insulated delivery bag: 1 minimum (available at Amazon or local kitchen supply stores in SR Nagar)
  - Ice packs: minimum 6 (re-usable gel packs, available at any pharmacy or kitchen store for Rs 80–150 each)
  - Stainless steel trays or food-safe containers for staging
  - Disposable gloves (box of 100, food-grade)
- [ ] **Dedicate a refrigerator shelf or a mini-fridge exclusively to Soma production.** Set it to 2–4°C. Check with your thermometer that it actually holds that temperature — domestic fridge dials are often inaccurate.
- [ ] **Buy a digital probe thermometer** (Rs 300–500 at any kitchen supply shop in Kukatpally or on Amazon). You will use this every single day to confirm your cold chain — production temp, fridge temp, and post-delivery confirmation. Without it, your cold chain claims are guesswork.
- [ ] **Buy a kitchen scale accurate to 1g** (Rs 600–1,200 at kitchen supply stores or Amazon). Recipe standardization requires gram-level accuracy. Eyeballing 200g of ginger will give you inconsistent product batch to batch.

## Day 3 — Recipes, Test Batches & Costings

This is the most important day of the week. Do not rush it. You are confirming that your product is real before you tell anyone it exists.

- [ ] **Select your 3 launch SKUs.** Recommended starting point: a green juice (cucumber, spinach, ginger, lime), a citrus blend (orange, carrot, turmeric), and a beet-based blend (beet, apple, ginger). Keep it to 3 — you can always add later. More SKUs now just means more complexity for the same number of customers.
- [ ] **Run a full test batch of each SKU** using gram-level measurements. Write the exact recipe in your Recipes folder — ingredient, grams, preparation notes (peel or no peel, chop size, soaking required). This becomes your standard recipe. Every batch from here will reference this document.
- [ ] **Weigh your input and output for each batch.** Input: total grams of produce going in. Output: total ml of juice coming out. Calculate your yield percentage: (output ml / input grams) × 100. A cold-press should yield 60–80% depending on produce. If you are getting less, check your press technique.
- [ ] **Calculate your actual COGS per bottle for each SKU:**
  - Add up the cost of all ingredients used in the batch (use market prices from where you actually bought them)
  - Add packaging cost: one glass bottle + one cap + one label (estimate Rs 18–25 per unit depending on your bottle source)
  - Divide total batch cost by number of bottles produced
  - Target: under Rs 55/bottle total COGS
  - If you are over Rs 55, identify which ingredient is pulling the cost up and adjust the recipe before proceeding
- [ ] **Taste every SKU properly.** Drink a full 50ml pour. Then note: colour (vibrant or dull?), smell (fresh or off?), taste (balanced, too sweet, too bitter, too thin?), texture (clean finish or pulpy?). Write these notes down. This is your baseline quality standard.
- [ ] **If any SKU fails on COGS or taste: adjust and retest before moving to Day 4.** Do not print labels for a product you are not confident in. It is better to add an extra day here than to deliver something you are not proud of.

## Day 4 — Labels, Packaging Order & First Outreach

Today you create your first customer-facing materials and make your first quiet announcement.

- [ ] **Design your label in Canva (free at canva.com).** It does not need to be elaborate — clean and legible is enough for launch. Your label must include by FSSAI regulation:
  - Product name (SKU name)
  - Ingredients list in descending order by weight
  - Allergen declaration (if any nuts, dairy, or common allergens used — even cross-contamination risk)
  - Net volume in ml
  - "Best consumed within 48 hours of pressing"
  - "Pressed On:" with a blank field you fill by hand daily, or a printed space for a sticker date
  - Your FSSAI license number
  - Your business name and city (Hyderabad)
  - "Keep refrigerated at 2–8°C"
- [ ] **Print a test sheet at home first** to verify the label dimensions fit your bottles, text is legible at actual size, and nothing is cut off. Most glass bottles fit a label of 8–9cm wide × 5–6cm tall — confirm before ordering.
- [ ] **Order 200 printed labels** (enough for your first 2–3 weeks) from a local print shop. Kukatpally and SR Nagar both have multiple print shops that handle short-run label printing. Budget Rs 600–1,000 for 200 labels on waterproof sticker paper. Ask specifically for waterproof or water-resistant label stock — wet bottles will cause paper labels to peel. Turnaround is typically 24–48 hours.
- [ ] **Write your first WhatsApp message to your 20-person list.** Send it as a broadcast (not a group — broadcasts feel personal, groups feel noisy). The message should introduce Soma, explain what cold-pressed means in 2 sentences, tell them you are in the final prep phase, and offer a free taste test within the next 2 days — no commitment, no sale. Keep it conversational and genuine. You are not selling yet — you are starting a real conversation.
- [ ] **Set up your Daily Batch Log in Google Sheets.** Columns: Date, SKU, Input Weight (g), Output Volume (ml), Yield %, Bottles Produced, COGS per Bottle, QC Pass/Fail, Notes. This takes 10 minutes to build and will be invaluable when you are pressing 50 bottles a day and need to trace a quality issue.

## Day 5 — First Real Batch & Sample Delivery

This is the day everything becomes real. You will wake up before sunrise and make your first proper batch. Keep your phone on silent before 4 AM — the world does not need to know yet.

- [ ] **Wake up at 4:00 AM.** Production starts now.
- [ ] **Follow your SOP in exact sequence:** wash hands → gloves on → sanitize all surfaces and equipment → prep ingredients (wash, peel, chop per recipe) → press each SKU → QC check (taste, colour, temperature) → bottle → cap → label → log → refrigerate. Do not skip or reorder steps. The sequence exists to prevent contamination.
- [ ] **Fill in your Batch Log for every SKU** as you go — not after. Logging from memory is less accurate than logging in real time.
- [ ] **Press enough for 8–10 sample bottles** (2–3 of each SKU). These are for your taste test outreach — not for sale. Label them clearly with the SKU name and "Pressed On: [today's date]."
- [ ] **By 6:00 AM: all bottles labeled, logged, and inside refrigerator.** Check temperature with your probe thermometer — confirm below 8°C. Write the temperature in your Batch Log.
- [ ] **Pack your delivery bag by 6:30 AM.** Ice packs at the bottom and sides, bottles standing upright (or lying flat if your bag is designed for that), insulated bag closed securely.
- [ ] **Deliver your 8–10 sample bottles to your closest contacts by 8:00 AM.** Hand deliver personally — do not use Swiggy or a neighbor for this first delivery. The conversation you have at the door is worth more than the juice. Explain what they are holding, tell them it is best within 48 hours, and ask them to message you their honest reaction.
- [ ] **Follow up by WhatsApp at 10:00 AM.** A simple message: "Did you get a chance to try it? Would love your honest take — what worked and what could be better 🙏". Keep the door open for genuine feedback, not just compliments.

## Day 6 — Customer Conversations & Payment Setup

Yesterday was your product. Today is your business.

- [ ] **Call or message everyone who received a sample.** Collect honest feedback — ask specifically: What did you like? What was off? Would you pay Rs 99 for this regularly? Why or why not? You are not trying to get validation. You are trying to find out what is real.
- [ ] **For anyone who expresses interest in subscribing:** share pricing clearly and directly. Do not be vague. Tell them the plan options, the delivery days, and how they pay. Let them decide. Hesitation here loses customers.
- [ ] **For anyone who asks for more before subscribing:** schedule a second delivery for tomorrow morning. Momentum matters more than margin on a Rs 99 bottle right now.
- [ ] **Set up and test your payment collection.** Google Pay, PhonePe, or a UPI QR code printed on a small card. Send yourself Rs 1 from a family member's phone to confirm the flow works end-to-end before a real customer tries it. The worst moment in a launch is a customer trying to pay and hitting a technical issue.
- [ ] **Build your Customer List in Google Sheets.** Columns: Name, Phone Number, Area (for delivery routing), Plan Type (daily / alternate days / weekly), Start Date, Next Delivery Date, Amount per Delivery, Payment Status, Notes. Every paying customer goes in here from Day 1.
- [ ] **Write down the top 3 pieces of feedback from today's conversations** and decide — for each one — whether you will change anything before launch, or hold and revisit after your first 15 paying customers. Do not change recipes based on one person's preference. Change only when multiple people say the same thing.

## Day 7 — Pre-Launch Readiness Review

Today is a checkpoint day, not a production day. Be honest with yourself on each of these.

- [ ] **Can you press 20+ bottles in under 2 hours consistently?** If you ran your Day 5 batch and it took 3.5 hours, you are not ready to take paying customers yet. Practice one more full batch today. Speed comes from a clean workflow, not from rushing.
- [ ] **Are all labels printed, in stock, and compliant?** Pull out a label and read every field. Does it have everything listed in the Day 4 requirements? Is the FSSAI number on it?
- [ ] **Is your delivery route planned for your actual customer addresses?** Open Google Maps, add every address, and check the total time. If 5 customers takes 45 minutes, 20 customers will take close to 3 hours — plan for that before it happens to you on a Tuesday morning.
- [ ] **Do you have at least 5 confirmed paying customers lined up for your launch day?** "Confirmed" means they said yes, they know the price, and they know the start date. Not "I think they'll order" — confirmed.
- [ ] **Is your Batch Log tested with real data?** Open it. Does it have data from at least 2 real batches? Are the formulas calculating COGS correctly?
- [ ] **Is your WhatsApp Business account operational?** Do you have at least 10 contacts saved in your broadcast list? Is your away message active? Have you tested sending a broadcast to 5 contacts?
- [ ] **Have you tested the full cold chain end-to-end?** Press → fridge (confirm temp) → pack with ice packs → deliver to a nearby address → check temperature of a bottle on arrival. Your target is that a bottle arrives at or below 10°C. If it is arriving warmer, add more ice packs or tighten your delivery window.
- [ ] **Do you have enough supplies for 2 weeks of production?** Count your bottles, caps, and labels. If you have 50 bottles and 5 customers ordering daily, you have 2 days of stock. Order more today.

## Pre-Launch Gate

**Do NOT accept your first paid order until every item below is checked off.**

This gate exists because taking money before you are ready destroys trust, and trust is your only competitive advantage as a micro-brand. One failed delivery to a paying customer in the first week will cost you 3 future customers through word of mouth.

- [ ] At least 3 full test batches completed successfully with logged data
- [ ] COGS confirmed below Rs 55/bottle on all 3 launch SKUs
- [ ] Labels printed, compliant, and fitted to bottles
- [ ] Payment method tested and confirmed working
- [ ] Delivery route timed — you know exactly how long your morning route takes
- [ ] At least 5 people have confirmed they want to subscribe (not "probably" — confirmed)
- [ ] Cold chain validated end-to-end with thermometer data

When all 7 are checked, you are ready. Message your first customer. Welcome to being open for business.

## Contacts to Have Saved Before Day 1

Save these in your phone before you begin. You will need at least one of them before the end of Week 2.

- **Produce market contact** — Bowenpally Rythu Bazaar (early morning, 3:30 AM onwards) or Kothapet Market. Get the mobile number of a vendor you trust for at least 2 of your key ingredients. Emergency restocking happens — know who to call at 5 AM.
- **A trusted neighbor or friend** who knows your address and can receive a delivery if you are running late. You do not need them often. You will need them at least once.
- **FSSAI Telangana regional office** — Hyderabad office number available at fssai.gov.in under "Contact Us → State Offices → Telangana." Save it. If an inspector visits and you have a question about labeling compliance, this is who you call.
- **A local label printer** who can turn around a reprint in 24 hours. Confirm this explicitly when you place your first order — ask: "If I need 200 more labels tomorrow, can you do it?" Get their direct WhatsApp number.
- **A reliable packaging supplier** for bottles and caps. SR Nagar and Secunderabad have wholesale glass bottle suppliers. Find one, confirm they stock your bottle size, and save their number. Running out of bottles on a Thursday with paying customers waiting is avoidable if you have this contact.
