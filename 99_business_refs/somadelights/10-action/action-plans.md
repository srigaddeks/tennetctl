## Top 10 Priorities

1. **Get the product right** — taste, freshness, and consistency matter more than variety.
2. **Deliver on time, every time** — 7 AM means 7 AM; reliability builds trust faster than quality.
3. **Build the subscription base** — recurring revenue is survival; chase subscribers, not one-time orders.
4. **Obsess over retention** — one retained customer is worth five new trials.
5. **Keep unit economics healthy** — if you're not making Rs 40+ margin per bottle, fix costs before scaling.
6. **Use pop-ups relentlessly** — they're your only scalable acquisition channel at this stage.
7. **Make the booklet irresistible** — it's your most powerful brand asset; invest in quality.
8. **Build the WhatsApp community early** — it's your free, owned, high-engagement marketing channel.
9. **Track everything from Day 1** — revenue, costs, customers, retention, CAC — in a single Google Sheet.
10. **Stay in one cluster until it's dense** — resist the urge to expand geography before hitting 50 customers in Cluster 1.

---

## Top 10 Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Launching too many SKUs** — start with 3-4, master them, then expand based on demand.
2. **Underpricing** — Rs 80 minimum; don't compete on price with store-bought juice, compete on value.
3. **Spending on ads** — zero paid advertising; your budget goes to booklets, samples, and pop-ups.
4. **Scaling before systems** — don't add a second cluster until Cluster 1 has reliable delivery and 50+ customers.
5. **Ignoring spoilage** — cold-pressed juice has a 24-hour window; overproduction = waste = margin killer.
6. **Skipping the booklet** — it's not optional; it's the brand differentiator and conversation starter.
7. **Being shy at pop-ups** — every sample not given is a customer lost; talk to everyone, follow up the same day.
8. **Not tracking finances daily** — if you don't know your margin by Day 7, you're flying blind.
9. **Treating it like a juice shop** — you're building a wellness habit company; every touchpoint should educate.
10. **Hiring too early** — solo until 25 customers, one helper until 80 customers, second helper after 100.

## Recommended Launch Blueprint

### What to Sell
3-4 cold-pressed juice SKUs (300ml bottles): one green, one root-based, one citrus, one seasonal. Monthly subscription plans at Rs 1,999-2,599. Single bottles at Rs 80-129.

### Where to Sell
Kukatpally/Miyapur/Chandanagar — start with a 3-5 km radius from your kitchen. Apartment complexes with 200+ flats. Gyms with 100+ active members. Weekend pop-ups at gates and parks.

### Who to Sell To
Health-aware families (25-45 years), dual-income households, fitness enthusiasts, new parents looking for clean nutrition. Not price-sensitive bargain hunters — wellness-motivated buyers who value convenience and consistency.

### How to Deliver
Milkman model: produce at 4 AM, deliver by 7 AM, 6 days a week (Monday-Saturday). Founder delivers in Month 1. Hire helper for delivery in Month 2, founder focuses on production and growth. Insulated bags with ice packs. Contactless doorstep delivery.

### How to Grow
- **Weeks 1-4:** Personal network, free samples, apartment WhatsApp groups
- **Weeks 5-8:** Pop-ups every Saturday, referral program live, gym partnerships
- **Weeks 9-12:** Second cluster, community meetups, WhatsApp group content
- **Ongoing:** Referral flywheel (10% for 12 months), booklet distribution, pop-up calendar

### How to Retain
- Daily delivery habit (cue-routine-reward loop)
- 21-day booklet tracker (visible progress)
- Weekly WhatsApp wellness tips (ongoing education)
- Monthly ingredient spotlights (variety and learning)
- Community meetups (belonging and accountability)
- Streak celebrations (gamification without an app)

### What NOT to Do
- No paid ads (Meta, Google, influencers) — not until 500+ customers
- No physical store or kitchen rental — home kitchen until 200 customers
- No app development — WhatsApp is the interface until proven need
- No discounting below Rs 70/bottle — protect margins, sell value
- No geographic sprawl — dense clusters beat wide coverage
- No custom orders — standardized menu only, adjust monthly based on demand
