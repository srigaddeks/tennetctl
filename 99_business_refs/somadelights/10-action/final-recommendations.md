### Why Only 2 Plans

- **Decision simplicity:** More than 2 plans = decision paralysis = no purchase
- **Operational simplicity:** 2 plans to manage, not 5
- **Upgrade path clear:** Start weekly → commit monthly when habit forms
- **Add-ons later:** Shots, plans, pulp products become add-on revenue on top of base subscription

### Pricing Psychology

- Individual bottles priced at premium (Rs 99–149) — for trials and one-off purchases
- Subscription priced for value (Rs 83–92/bottle) — reward commitment
- Never discount the individual price. Add value instead (free sample of new SKU, bonus wellness card)
- Monthly plan = Rs 67/day (less than a Starbucks, frame it that way)

## Biggest Traps to Avoid

| # | Trap | Why It Kills You |
|---|------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Launching with 10+ SKUs** | Multiplies complexity, ingredient waste, and production time. 5 SKUs is the maximum at launch. |
| 2 | **Accepting orders from far-away areas** | Destroys delivery economics and route efficiency. Hard geographic boundary, no exceptions. |
| 3 | **Spending money on ads or Instagram marketing** | Zero ROI at this stage. Every rupee on ads is a rupee NOT spent on product quality. Word of mouth only. |
| 4 | **Building an app before 200 customers** | WhatsApp + Google Sheets handles everything until 200. Apps cost Rs 2–5 lakh and distract from the real work. |
| 5 | **Discounting to get customers** | Trains customers to expect deals. Attracts price-sensitive customers who churn when prices normalize. Add value, never discount. |
| 6 | **Not taking a day off** | Sunday is no-delivery day from Day 1. Communicate it. Customers understand. Burnout is the #1 business killer. |
| 7 | **Hiring friends or family for operations** | They won't take 4 AM seriously. They're harder to fire. Professional relationships require professional hires. |
| 8 | **Perfectionism on packaging and branding** | A Rs 5 sticker label with correct information beats a Rs 15 premium label that delays launch by 2 weeks. Ship first, beautify later. |
| 9 | **Ignoring compliance (FSSAI, labels)** | One violation or one customer illness from poor hygiene ends the business. Rs 100 and 1 hour to apply for FSSAI. No excuse. |
| 10 | **Trying to be a juice company instead of a wellness company** | Juice companies compete on taste and price. Wellness companies compete on outcomes and trust. Frame everything as habit and wellness, not flavor and freshness. |

## Decision Framework for the First 90 Days

When you face a decision and aren't sure, use this filter:

1. **Does it increase density?** If yes, prioritize it. If it spreads you thinner, reject it.
2. **Does it improve retention?** Product quality, delivery consistency, and education improve retention. Everything else is secondary.
3. **Does it simplify operations?** At this stage, simpler is always better. Complexity is a luxury for later.
4. **Can it wait 30 days?** If yes, it probably should. Focus on the 20 actions above first.
5. **Would the customer notice?** If a change isn't visible to the customer, it's probably not urgent.

## The One Thing That Matters Most

If you read nothing else in this entire document set, read this:

**The business succeeds or fails on Week 2 retention.**

If the people who try your product in Week 1 come back in Week 2, everything else is solvable — pricing, routes, team, compliance, growth, all of it. The problems become operational, not existential.

If they don't come back, nothing else matters. No amount of marketing, referral programs, or beautiful packaging will save a product people don't want to consume daily.

So before you worry about FSSAI registration, or label design, or route optimization, or investor pitches — make 5 bottles that taste so good and feel so right that the person who drinks one on Tuesday morning is genuinely looking forward to Wednesday morning.

That's the whole business. Everything else is logistics.
