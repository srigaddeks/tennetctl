# Insurance & Liability

> FSSAI is a license. Insurance is protection. They are not the same thing.

---

## 1. Overview

Running a food business from a home kitchen creates real product liability exposure. Standard home insurance does not cover commercial food production. A single product complaint — an allergic reaction, a contamination claim, a customer hospitalisation — can result in legal and financial consequences that end the business if there is no coverage.

The good news: comprehensive insurance for a micro-food business in India is affordable. The full annual cost is Rs 8,000-20,000 depending on coverage levels. This is a line item in the Year 1 financial plan, not an optional expense.

Get covered before the first sale. Not after the first complaint.

---

## 2. What FSSAI Does and Does Not Cover

FSSAI registration or licensing establishes that Soma is a registered food business operator operating under regulatory compliance. It does not:

- Protect against customer claims for illness or adverse reactions
- Cover legal costs if a customer sues
- Compensate a customer for harm caused by a product
- Cover equipment damage or loss
- Protect the founder or the business against third-party claims

FSSAI compliance reduces regulatory risk. Insurance covers commercial and liability risk. Both are required. Neither replaces the other.

## 3. Essential Insurance Types

### Type 1 — Product Liability Insurance

**Status: CRITICAL — get before Day 1 of first sale**

**What it covers:** Customer claims arising from consuming Soma products. This includes allergic reactions, food poisoning, contamination, foreign object in product, and illness attributed to the product. It covers legal defence costs and any settlement or judgment up to the policy limit.

**Why it is critical:** One hospitalisation claim without coverage can exceed Rs 5-10 lakh in legal fees, medical costs, and settlement — before any judgment. A home kitchen operation cannot absorb this without ceasing operations.

**Coverage needed at launch:** Minimum Rs 10 lakh per incident. This is the entry-level for a micro food business and is adequate for the first 12-18 months of operations at Soma's scale.

**Estimated annual cost:** Rs 5,000-15,000 for Rs 10 lakh per incident coverage. Cost varies by insurer, coverage limit, and whether you have FSSAI registration (which typically reduces the premium).

**Where to get it in Hyderabad:**

- New India Assurance — government insurer, reliable claims process, branch offices across Hyderabad including Kukatpally and HITEC City area. Ask specifically for a "product liability rider" or "commercial food product liability" policy.
- National Insurance Company — government insurer, similar profile to New India.
- Bajaj Allianz General Insurance — private insurer, faster claims processing, digitally accessible. Ask for "commercial general liability with product liability extension."
- HDFC Ergo — private insurer, available online or through advisors in Hyderabad.
- PolicyBazaar — useful for comparing quotes across multiple insurers. Search "product liability insurance India" on their platform.

**Practical note:** Not all general insurance agents know this product category well. Be specific: "I run a home-based food business producing cold-pressed juices. I need product liability insurance covering customer claims from consuming my products." If the first agent you speak to is unhelpful, call another.

---

### Type 2 — General Liability / Third-Party Liability

**Status: Get before first pop-up kiosk or public event**

**What it covers:** Slip-and-fall injuries at a kiosk or event, property damage caused during delivery (e.g., a spill damaging a customer's furniture), customer injury on premises where Soma operates. Covers legal defence and compensation up to the policy limit.

**When needed:** Not urgent at home kitchen / delivery-only stage. Becomes necessary before any physical presence — pop-up kiosk, market stall, wellness event at an office, participation in a food festival.

**Estimated annual cost:** Rs 3,000-8,000 for standard third-party liability coverage.

**Where to get it:** Same insurers as product liability. Often available as a combined "commercial general liability" policy that includes both product liability and third-party liability — ask for a combined policy to avoid gaps and reduce administrative overhead.

---

### Type 3 — Commercial Property / Equipment Insurance

**Status: Get when equipment value exceeds Rs 50,000**

**What it covers:** Cold press juicer, refrigerator/cold storage, packaging equipment, blenders — against fire, theft, electrical damage, and accidental damage.

**When needed:** At launch, if all equipment is under Rs 30,000 total replacement value, the annual premium may exceed the expected claim value — evaluate carefully. Once equipment crosses Rs 50,000 replacement value (e.g., after purchasing a commercial-grade cold press), get coverage.

**Estimated annual cost:** Rs 2,000-5,000 for Rs 1 lakh equipment coverage.

**What to document:** Before getting coverage, list every piece of equipment with purchase date and price. Keep invoices. Insurers will ask for this during a claim.

---

### Type 4 — Workmen's Compensation / Employer's Liability

**Status: Get when first employee or paid helper is hired**

**What it covers:** Injury or illness sustained by an employee in the course of work. Legally mandatory in India under the Employees' Compensation Act, 1923 for businesses with employees engaged in hazardous or physical work.

**When needed:** The moment Soma hires a delivery person, a kitchen assistant, or any paid worker — even part-time.

**Estimated annual cost:** Rs 2,000-5,000 for a small number of employees doing food production and delivery work.

---

### Type 5 — Health Insurance (Founder)

**Status: Separate personal policy — not business insurance**

Sri as the founder is the entire production operation. If Sri is sick for a week, there is no production. Health insurance is not a business expense in the accounting sense, but it is a business continuity instrument. Make sure a personal health policy with hospitalisation cover is in place independent of the business.

## 4. Allergen Liability

Undeclared allergens are one of the most common causes of product liability claims in food businesses. Soma must:

**Declare all allergens on every label.** Common allergens present in or potentially present in Soma products:

- Tree nuts (if any nut-based blend is developed)
- Ginger (sensitivity exists in some individuals, especially in high concentrations)
- Citrus (sensitivity exists, particularly with skin contact or high consumption)
- Amla / Indian gooseberry (acidity-sensitive individuals)
- Beetroot (can cause beeturia — harmless but alarming to some, disclose proactively)

**Label format must include:**
- Full ingredient list in descending order by weight
- "Contains: [allergen list]" if applicable
- "May contain traces of: [cross-contamination allergens]" if shared equipment is used for different blends

**FSSAI-compliant labels are the first line of liability protection.** A customer who claims an allergic reaction and can be shown a label that clearly declared the allergen is in a much weaker legal position. A customer who claims an allergic reaction when the allergen was not declared on the label is a serious exposure.

## 5. Delivery Liability

When a delivery partner (own vehicle or hired) causes an accident en route to a customer:

- The delivery person's own personal vehicle insurance covers them for third-party damage under mandatory Indian motor vehicle insurance law.
- Soma is not automatically liable for a delivery partner's road accident unless Soma is employing them as a full employee (not a contractor) and the vehicle is owned by Soma.

**Include in every delivery partner agreement:**
- Clause stating the delivery partner is responsible for compliance with traffic laws
- Clause confirming they hold valid vehicle insurance
- Clause confirming they are operating as an independent contractor, not an employee (if applicable)

This does not eliminate Soma's exposure if someone argues Soma was directing and controlling the delivery activity — but it establishes a documented basis for the contractor relationship.

## 6. Customer Consent for Free Trial

For the free week trial, consider a simple digital consent process. Before dispatching the first trial box:

- WhatsApp the customer the full ingredient list for the week's juices
- Ask them to confirm: "Please confirm you've reviewed the ingredients and have no known allergies to any of these."
- Their WhatsApp reply ("confirmed" or any affirmative response) creates a timestamped record

This is not legally airtight — it does not constitute a formal legal waiver. But it documents due diligence: Soma disclosed the ingredients and the customer confirmed they reviewed them before consuming. In any dispute, this record matters.

## 7. Incident Response Protocol

If a customer contacts Soma with a health complaint or adverse reaction:

**Step 1 — Take it seriously immediately.** Do not dismiss, deflect, or ask them to "wait and see." Respond within 30 minutes regardless of time of day for health complaints.

**Step 2 — Collect information:**
- Batch details (which blend, which date was it pressed)
- The "Pressed On" date on the bottle
- What they consumed and how much
- What symptoms they are experiencing
- Whether they have seen a doctor

**Step 3 — Pull the same batch from other deliveries** if there is any possibility of contamination. WhatsApp the other customers from that batch: "We're doing a proactive quality check on [date]'s [blend]. Have you experienced any discomfort?" Do not mention the specific complaint — do not cause alarm unnecessarily — but check.

**Step 4 — Offer replacement and refund immediately.** Do not fight a health complaint. Refund + replacement costs far less than the legal and reputational cost of being seen as dismissive.

**Step 5 — Notify insurance** if there is any hospitalisation or if the customer mentions legal action. Call the insurer before responding further to the customer's legal claims.

**Step 6 — Document everything:**
- Timestamped record of every WhatsApp message
- Notes from any phone calls
- Batch production log for the date in question
- Actions taken and their outcomes

Store incident records separately from the general feedback sheet. Maintain for a minimum of 3 years.

## 8. Label Compliance as Liability Protection

A properly labelled product is the strongest defence against most product liability claims.

**Every Soma bottle label must include:**

| Element | Example |
|---|---|
| Product name | "Soma Ginger-Lemon Immunity Shot" |
| Ingredients (descending by weight) | "Lemon (60%), ginger (25%), water (10%), rock salt (5%)" |
| Net volume | "250 ml" |
| Pressed On date | "Pressed On: 23-Mar-2026" |
| Best Before | "Best Before: 23-Mar-2026, 6 PM" |
| Storage instructions | "Refrigerate immediately. Do not consume if opened." |
| FSSAI License number | "FSSAI Lic. No. XXXXXXXXXXXX" |
| Manufacturer name and address | "Soma Delights, [address], Kukatpally, Hyderabad" |
| Allergen declaration | "Contains: ginger, citrus" |

Non-compliant labels are both a regulatory risk (FSSAI penalty) and a liability risk (undeclared allergen claim). Invest in proper label design once and reuse.

## 9. Budget Estimate

Annual insurance budget for Soma at launch and early growth:

| Policy | When | Estimated Annual Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Product Liability (Rs 10L coverage) | Before Day 1 of sales | Rs 5,000-15,000 |
| General / Third-Party Liability | Before first kiosk or event | Rs 3,000-8,000 |
| Equipment Insurance | When equipment > Rs 50K | Rs 2,000-5,000 |
| Workmen's Compensation | When first employee hired | Rs 2,000-5,000 |
| **Total (full coverage)** | | **Rs 12,000-33,000/year** |
| **Minimum at launch** | Product liability only | **Rs 5,000-15,000/year** |

Budget Rs 15,000-20,000/year in Year 1 financial plan to cover product liability and general liability. This is approximately Rs 1,250-1,700/month — a rounding error in the operating budget relative to the protection it provides.

## 10. Insurance Upgrade Triggers

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Annual revenue reaches Rs 10L | Increase product liability coverage to Rs 25L per incident |
| First full-time or part-time employee hired | Add workmen's compensation / employer's liability |
| First permanent kiosk location | Add location-specific property and liability coverage for that location |
| B2B channel active (Rs 2L+/month from offices) | Review product liability limits — bulk delivery to offices increases exposure |
| First corporate gifting order above Rs 50,000 | Confirm product liability covers gifted products |

Review insurance coverage at every financial quarter. Coverage limits that were adequate at Rs 5L/year revenue are not adequate at Rs 30L/year revenue.
