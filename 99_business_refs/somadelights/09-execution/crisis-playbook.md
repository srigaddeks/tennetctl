# Crisis Playbook — Soma Delights

> What to do when things go wrong. Because they will.
> For each scenario: severity, response time, step-by-step protocol, communication templates, prevention measures.
> Print this document. Keep it accessible. Review it monthly.

---

## Response Framework

Every crisis follows the same framework:

1. **ACKNOWLEDGE** — recognize the problem immediately
2. **CONTAIN** — stop it from getting worse
3. **COMMUNICATE** — tell affected people what happened and what you're doing
4. **FIX** — resolve the root cause
5. **FOLLOW UP** — check that the fix worked and the affected people are satisfied
6. **DOCUMENT** — record what happened, why, and how to prevent it

**Severity Levels:**

| Level | Description | Response Time | Who Handles |
|-------|------------|---------------|-------------|
| CRITICAL | Health risk, legal exposure, brand-threatening | Within 30 minutes | Founder personally |
| HIGH | Service failure affecting multiple customers | Within 1 hour | Founder personally |
| MEDIUM | Single customer issue, operational hiccup | Within 2 hours | Founder or trained assistant |
| LOW | Minor inconvenience, cosmetic issue | Within 24 hours | Anyone on team |

## Scenario 1: Customer Reports Illness After Consuming Product

**Severity: CRITICAL**
**Response time: Within 30 minutes**

### Immediate Response (First 30 Minutes)

1. **Call the customer immediately.** Not WhatsApp text — voice call. Express genuine concern.
2. Ask for details:
   - Which product did they consume? (SKU, date on label)
   - What time did they consume it?
   - What are their symptoms? (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach pain, allergic reaction)
   - How severe? (mild discomfort vs. needing medical attention)
   - Did anyone else in their household consume the same product?
3. If symptoms are severe (difficulty breathing, swelling, severe vomiting):
   - Tell them to go to the nearest hospital immediately
   - Offer to arrange transport or accompany them
   - Do not attempt to diagnose or minimize
4. Record everything: customer name, product details, batch number, symptoms, timeline

### Within 1 Hour

5. **Pull the entire batch.** Identify the batch number from production logs. Contact ALL customers who received bottles from the same batch.
6. Check: did any other customer from the same batch report issues? If yes, this is a confirmed product problem. If no, it may be coincidental but still treat seriously.
7. **Send proactive message to all same-batch customers:**

> **Template (WhatsApp):**
> "Hi [Name], this is [Founder] from Soma Delights. We are conducting a quality check on today's [Product Name] batch. As a precaution, please do not consume the bottle delivered today. We will replace it with a fresh bottle tomorrow. I apologize for the inconvenience and appreciate your understanding."

8. Review production log for the batch: ingredients used, times, temperature, any anomalies noted.

### Same Day

9. If the customer is local and willing, visit them personally. Bring a replacement product (different batch) and express concern in person.
10. If medical attention is needed, offer to cover medical expenses. This is not just ethical — it prevents escalation.
11. If multiple customers report illness from the same batch: **HALT ALL PRODUCTION.** Deep clean the entire kitchen. Do not resume until root cause is identified.

### Investigation (24-72 Hours)

12. Root cause analysis:
    - Ingredient: was any ingredient past its freshness? Purchased from a different vendor?
    - Equipment: was the juicer properly cleaned? Any residue from previous batch?
    - Temperature: did cold chain break? Was product above 8 degrees C at any point?
    - Process: was the production checklist followed completely?
    - External: was it actually the product, or did the customer eat something else?
13. Check remaining bottles from the batch (if any) — look, smell, taste (carefully)
14. If the cause is identified: fix it, document the fix, update SOPs
15. If the cause is NOT identified: err on the side of caution. Extra cleaning, extra checks, consider testing ingredients from a food lab (Hyderabad has several — Rs 2,000-5,000 per test)

### Customer Follow-Up

16. Call the customer daily until they confirm they are fully recovered
17. Once recovered:
    - Apologize again
    - Explain what you found and what you changed
    - Offer 1 week of free product as a gesture
    - Ask if they want to continue their subscription (no pressure either way)

### Documentation

- Date and time of incident
- Customer details and symptoms
- Batch number and production log
- Other customers on same batch and their status
- Root cause finding
- Corrective action taken
- Customer resolution and outcome

### Prevention

- [ ] Production checklist followed 100% (no shortcuts ever)
- [ ] Ingredients checked for freshness at purchase AND before use
- [ ] Cold chain verified at every stage
- [ ] Juicer deep-cleaned between different SKU batches
- [ ] Taste test by founder on every batch
- [ ] Batch tracking system maintained rigorously

## Scenario 2: Contaminated Batch Discovered (Before or After Delivery)

**Severity: CRITICAL**
**Response time: Immediately upon discovery**

### If Discovered BEFORE Delivery

1. Do not deliver the batch. Set it aside
2. Identify the cause: visible contamination? Off smell? Color anomaly? Foreign particle?
3. Discard the entire batch (even if only one bottle seems affected — the whole batch is compromised)
4. Assess whether you have time to produce a replacement batch before delivery window
5. If yes: produce replacement, deliver late but clean
6. If no: notify customers of the delay

> **Template (WhatsApp — delayed delivery):**
> "Good morning [Name]. Our quality check flagged an issue with this morning's batch, so we're making a fresh batch to ensure you get the best quality. Your delivery will arrive by [TIME] today. Sorry for the delay — your health comes first."

### If Discovered AFTER Delivery

7. This is more serious. Customers may have already consumed it
8. Immediately message ALL affected customers:

> **Template (WhatsApp — urgent recall):**
> "Hi [Name], this is [Founder] from Soma Delights. We've identified a quality concern with today's [Product Name]. Please DO NOT consume the bottle delivered today. I am arranging a replacement immediately. I sincerely apologize. Your safety is our top priority. Please reply to confirm you received this message."

9. Track confirmations. Call anyone who doesn't reply within 30 minutes
10. Arrange replacement delivery same day if possible
11. If customer already consumed: monitor for any reports of illness (see Scenario 1)
12. Investigate root cause immediately — do not produce again until resolved

### Common Contamination Causes

| Cause | Signs | Fix |
|-------|-------|-----|
| Spoiled ingredient | Off smell, discoloration, sliminess | Better ingredient inspection at purchase and before use |
| Equipment not cleaned | Off taste, unusual particles | Mandatory deep clean between batches |
| Cross-contamination (from kitchen) | Foreign taste, particles | Dedicated juicing area, no other cooking during production |
| Insect/pest | Visible contamination | Kitchen pest control, ingredient inspection |
| Chemical contamination (cleaning agents) | Chemical taste/smell | Rinse all surfaces after sanitizing, food-grade sanitizers only |

## Scenario 3: Delivery Rider No-Show

**Severity: HIGH**
**Response time: Within 30 minutes of missed delivery window**

### Immediate Response

1. Attempt to contact the rider (call + WhatsApp). Wait 10 minutes for response
2. If rider is unreachable or confirmed unavailable:

**Option A: Founder delivers (under 15 customers)**
- Prioritize by subscriber tenure (longest subscribers first)
- Delivery may be delayed 30-60 minutes
- Notify all customers of the delay

**Option B: Too many deliveries for founder alone**
- Identify customers by zone
- Deliver closest zone first, then expand
- Any customers you cannot reach: notify with credit

3. Notify affected customers immediately:

> **Template (WhatsApp — delivery delay):**
> "Good morning [Name]. Our delivery is running about [30-45] minutes behind schedule today due to a logistics issue. Your fresh juice is on its way. Sorry for the delay — I'll make sure it arrives cold and fresh."

### Same Day Resolution

4. Assess why the rider didn't show: personal emergency? Quit? Overslept?
5. If temporary (one-time): address with rider, move on
6. If pattern or permanent: activate backup rider immediately

### Prevention — Rider Reliability System

- **Always have a backup rider** — someone who can be called with 1 hour notice
- Backup rider options in Hyderabad:
  - Dunzo / Porter for emergency same-day delivery (expensive but available)
  - Neighborhood college students (Rs 200-300 per morning shift)
  - Part-time delivery persons from local delivery services
- **Pay riders fairly and on time.** A rider who feels valued doesn't no-show
- **Confirm with rider every evening** (8-9 PM WhatsApp: "Confirmed for tomorrow morning?")
- Keep a list of 3 potential backup riders at all times

## Scenario 4: Juice Delivered Warm (Cold Chain Failure)

**Severity: HIGH**
**Response time: Within 1 hour of report**

### Response

1. Apologize immediately
2. Ask customer NOT to consume the product (unpasteurized juice above 10 degrees C for extended time may harbor bacteria)
3. Offer replacement on next delivery or same-day if feasible
4. Add credit to their account for the inconvenience

> **Template (WhatsApp):**
> "I'm sorry about that. Please don't drink that bottle — we'll bring you a fresh, properly chilled replacement [today/tomorrow morning]. I've also added a credit to your account. We're looking into what went wrong with the cold chain."

### Investigation

5. Check: were ice packs fully frozen before dispatch? (They take 8-12 hours to freeze fully)
6. Check: was the insulated bag closed properly?
7. Check: how long was the delivery route? Did the rider make extra stops?
8. Check: was it an unusually hot day? (Hyderabad summer = 40+ degrees C — standard cold chain may be insufficient)

### Fixes by Root Cause

| Cause | Fix |
|-------|-----|
| Ice packs not fully frozen | Put in freezer by 6 PM the night before. Mark "ready" packs |
| Bag not sealed | Training: always zip the bag after each delivery stop |
| Route too long | Shorten routes, split into two trips, or add more bags |
| Summer heat overwhelming standard cold chain | Add extra ice packs (3 instead of 2), use reflective foil on bags, tighten delivery window to 60 minutes max |

### Summer-Specific Cold Chain Protocol (March - June)

- 3 gel ice packs per bag (instead of standard 2)
- Maximum 60 minutes from kitchen to last delivery
- Pre-chill insulated bags (put in fridge 30 minutes before packing)
- Route planning: farthest customer first, closest last (so farthest customer gets the coldest product)
- If ambient temperature exceeds 40 degrees C: consider adding a frozen water bottle to each bag as extra thermal mass

## Scenario 5: Social Media / Public Complaint

**Severity: HIGH**
**Response time: Within 30 minutes**

### The Golden Rule

**Speed and empathy beat perfection.** A fast, genuine response defuses 90% of public complaints. Silence or defensiveness turns a complaint into a crisis.

### Response Protocol

1. **See the complaint.** Monitor WhatsApp groups, Instagram comments/DMs, Google reviews. Check at least every 2 hours during the day
2. **Respond publicly within 30 minutes.** Even if you don't have a solution yet, acknowledge it:

> **Template (public reply — Instagram/social media):**
> "I'm really sorry about this, [Name]. This isn't the experience we want for you. Let me fix this right away. I'm sending you a message directly so we can sort this out."

3. **Move to private immediately.** Send a DM or WhatsApp message:

> **Template (private follow-up):**
> "Hi [Name], I'm [Founder] from Soma Delights. I saw your message and I want to make this right. Can you tell me exactly what happened? I'll take care of it personally."

4. **Resolve quickly.** Offer replacement, credit, or whatever is appropriate per the refund policy. Be generous — the cost of a lost customer (and their word-of-mouth) far exceeds the cost of a free bottle
5. **Close the loop publicly.** Once resolved, reply to the original public complaint:

> **Template (public resolution):**
> "Update: we connected with [Name] directly and resolved this. We replaced the product and are taking steps to make sure this doesn't happen again. Thank you for your patience, [Name], and for helping us improve."

### What NOT to Do

- **Never argue publicly.** Even if the customer is wrong. Argue in private if you must, but never in public
- **Never delete complaints.** People screenshot. Deleted complaints look worse
- **Never ignore.** Silence is interpreted as not caring
- **Never be defensive.** "Well, actually..." is the worst way to start a response
- **Never blame the customer.** Even if they stored the juice in the sun for 4 hours
- **Never offer a generic response.** Use their name. Reference their specific issue

### Troll or Fake Complaint

If you suspect a complaint is from a non-customer or competitor:
- Still respond politely and publicly
- Ask for order details privately ("Can you share your subscription details so I can look into this?")
- If they can't provide details, the public will notice — you don't need to call them out
- Never accuse someone of being fake publicly

## Scenario 6: FSSAI Inspector Visit

**Severity: MEDIUM (if compliant) / CRITICAL (if not)**
**Response time: Immediate — cooperate fully**

### Before It Happens (Preparation)

Keep these ready at all times, printed and visible in the kitchen:

- [ ] FSSAI registration certificate / license (framed, on kitchen wall)
- [ ] Production log (daily: date, batch, ingredients, quantities, temperatures)
- [ ] Hygiene log (daily: cleaning records, who cleaned, what was cleaned)
- [ ] Ingredient sourcing records (receipts from vendors with dates)
- [ ] Water quality report (if using tap water for cleaning — Hyderabad municipal water report suffices)
- [ ] Pest control records (if applicable)
- [ ] Personnel hygiene records (gloves used, hair nets worn — note in daily log)

### During the Visit

1. **Stay calm. Be cooperative.** Inspectors are doing their job. Hostility or evasion makes everything worse
2. Greet the inspector professionally. Offer them a seat. Ask for their identification
3. Show them your FSSAI license without being asked
4. Walk them through your production process. Show the logs
5. Answer questions honestly. If you don't know something, say "I'll find out" — don't make things up
6. If they want to take samples: cooperate. They are within their rights
7. Take notes of everything they say, ask, or flag

### If Issues Are Found

8. Thank the inspector for pointing them out
9. Ask for a written list of non-compliances
10. Ask for the deadline to fix them
11. Fix immediately — most inspectors appreciate responsiveness
12. Document all fixes with photos and dates
13. If a fine is imposed: pay it. Appeal only if genuinely unfair and you have clear documentation

### Common FSSAI Concerns for Home Kitchens

| Concern | How to Address |
|---------|---------------|
| No FSSAI license displayed | Get it framed, put on wall. Always |
| No production logs | Start logging from Day 1. No exceptions |
| Hygiene: no gloves/hair nets | Wear them always during production. Keep extras visible |
| Pest presence | Basic pest control in kitchen. Mesh on windows. No open food |
| Labeling non-compliant | Labels must have: FSSAI no., ingredients, net weight, mfg date, best before, storage instructions |
| No water testing | Get municipal water report or RO service record |
| Cross-contamination risk | Dedicated workspace, separate boards, cleaning between batches |

### FSSAI Registration vs. License

- **Registration (Form A):** for businesses with turnover under Rs 12 lakh/year. Simpler, cheaper (Rs 100 for 1 year). This is what you likely start with
- **License (Form B):** required if turnover exceeds Rs 12 lakh/year. More documentation. Rs 2,000-5,000 for 1-5 years
- **Apply at:** foscos.fssai.gov.in
- Processing time: 7-60 days (online application is faster)
- Renewal: apply 30 days before expiry

## Scenario 7: Ingredient Supply Disruption

**Severity: MEDIUM**
**Response time: Within same day**

### Types of Disruption

| Type | Cause | Frequency |
|------|-------|-----------|
| Market closed | Bandh, holiday, weather | 3-5 times/year |
| Specific ingredient unavailable | Seasonal end, crop failure, logistics | Monthly |
| Price spike | Seasonal demand, monsoon, supply shortage | Seasonal |

### Immediate Response

1. Check your buffer stock. You should always have 2 days of critical ingredients on hand (see Prevention below)
2. If buffer covers the gap: proceed normally. Restock ASAP
3. If buffer is insufficient: activate substitution plan

### Substitution Plan

| Missing Ingredient | Substitute | SKU Affected | Customer Impact |
|-------------------|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Spinach | Kale (if available) or increase cucumber | Green Morning | Slight taste difference. Notify customer |
| Celery | Extra cucumber + pinch of salt | Green Morning | Minimal impact |
| Green apple | Red apple | Green Morning, ABC | Sweeter, slightly different color |
| Beetroot | No direct substitute | Beet Vitality, ABC | Swap SKU for the day — offer alternative |
| Fresh turmeric | Dry turmeric powder (1/4 tsp per bottle) — not ideal | Golden Shield | Different flavor profile. Notify customer |
| Ginger | No substitute at required quantities | Golden Shield, Ginger Shot | Reduce ginger content or skip SKU for the day |
| Amla | Extra lemon | Citrus Immunity | More citrusy, less astringent |
| Orange | Mosambi (sweet lime) | Citrus Immunity | Very similar taste |

### Communication

If substitution significantly changes the product:

> **Template (WhatsApp):**
> "Good morning [Name]. Quick heads up — [ingredient] wasn't available at the market today due to [reason]. Your [Product] today has [substitute] instead, which is very similar in taste and nutrition. Normal recipe resumes tomorrow. Enjoy your juice."

If an SKU can't be made at all:

> **Template (WhatsApp):**
> "Good morning [Name]. [Ingredient] was unavailable today, so we couldn't make [Product]. You'll receive [Alternative Product] instead — it's one of our favorites. A credit for the difference (if any) has been added to your account. [Product] will be back tomorrow."

### Prevention — Buffer Stock Protocol

**Always maintain 2-day buffer of these critical ingredients:**

| Ingredient | 2-Day Buffer (20 subscribers) | Storage |
|-----------|------------------------------|---------|
| Spinach | 1.5 kg | Fridge, damp cloth, 2 days max |
| Carrots | 3 kg | Fridge, dry, lasts 7+ days |
| Beetroot | 2 kg | Fridge, dry, lasts 7+ days |
| Ginger | 500g | Fridge, paper towel, lasts 2 weeks |
| Lemons | 1.5 kg | Countertop, lasts 5-7 days |
| Apples | 2 kg | Fridge, lasts 7+ days |

**Leafy greens are the weakest link** — they perish fastest. Buy every 2 days. If market trip is missed, have a backup source (local Rythu Bazaar, vegetable vendor near home, online grocery like BigBasket/Zepto for emergency).

### Price Spike Response

If a key ingredient price spikes more than 50%:
- Short term (1-2 weeks): absorb the cost. Do not change the recipe
- Medium term (1 month+): adjust recipe slightly (reduce expensive ingredient by 10-15%, increase a cheaper one)
- Long term: adjust pricing at next billing cycle with 7 days' notice
- NEVER: reduce bottle size without telling customers

## Scenario 8: Cash Flow Crisis

**Severity: HIGH**
**Response time: Act when you have less than 2 weeks of operating cash**

### Early Warning Signs

- Wallet balance covers less than 14 days of operations
- Subscription renewals are slowing down
- More cancellations than new sign-ups for 2 consecutive weeks
- Ingredient costs rising without corresponding revenue increase
- You're delaying rider payment or vendor payments

### Response Steps

**Week 1 — Tighten**

1. Calculate exact daily operating cost (ingredients + packaging + delivery + any wages)
2. Calculate exact daily revenue (active subscribers x daily rate)
3. If revenue is greater than daily cost: you have a collection problem, not a revenue problem. Focus on collecting prepayments faster
4. If daily cost is greater than revenue: you have a margin problem. See below

**Week 2 — Act**

5. **Reduce costs (without reducing quality):**
   - Negotiate better rates with existing vendors (you have 2+ months of relationship now)
   - Reduce ingredient waste (tighter portioning, better storage)
   - Reduce free starter kits temporarily (from full week to 3-day trial)
   - Pause expansion to new areas — focus on current subscribers
   - If delivery rider cost is high, deliver some routes yourself

6. **Increase revenue:**
   - Push for monthly prepayment instead of weekly (better cash flow)
   - Activate referral program aggressively (ask happy customers to refer)
   - Offer a "3-month prepay" discount (e.g., 10% off) to bring in cash upfront
   - Sell surplus to walk-in / one-time customers at a slight premium

7. **What to NEVER do:**
   - Never reduce product quality (smaller bottles, diluted juice, cheaper ingredients)
   - Never delay rider payment (they will leave, and you can't deliver)
   - Never skip vendor payments without communication (tell them you'll pay in 3 days — honesty preserves relationships)
   - Never take on debt for operating expenses (debt for equipment is acceptable, debt for daily ingredients is a red flag)

### If Cash Runs Out

8. Be transparent with subscribers:

> **Template (WhatsApp — to close subscribers you trust):**
> "Hi [Name], I want to be honest with you. We're going through a tight patch as we grow Soma Delights. I may need to pause operations for [1-2 weeks] to regroup. Your existing balance will be fully honored when we resume. I'm committed to this and will be back stronger. Thank you for being part of this journey."

9. Use the pause to: renegotiate costs, plan better, consider a small capital infusion if the business fundamentals are sound
10. Resume with a tighter, leaner operation

## Scenario 9: Copycat Competitor Launches in Same Area

**Severity: MEDIUM**
**Response time: No panic. Strategic response over 1-2 weeks**

### What NOT to Do

- Do not panic
- Do not drop prices
- Do not talk negatively about the competitor (ever, to anyone)
- Do not change your strategy because of them
- Do not stalk their social media obsessively

### What to Do

1. **Assess:** Are they actually competing with you, or just in the same general category? A Juice bar opening 3 km away is not the same as someone doing milkman-model cold-pressed delivery on your street

2. **Acknowledge your moat:** What they can NOT easily copy:
   - Your existing subscriber relationships and habits (switching cost is high once the 6 AM delivery is part of someone's routine)
   - Your education content and knowledge reputation
   - Your referral network (people don't refer to strangers)
   - Your founder's personal touch (hard to scale, but hard to replicate)

3. **Double down on what makes you different:**
   - Increase education content (daily WhatsApp knowledge, ingredient deep-dives)
   - Personal check-ins with subscribers ("How are you feeling after 2 weeks of Green Morning?")
   - Improve packaging, unboxing, small delights (handwritten notes, seasonal surprises)
   - Launch a subscriber-only benefit (early access to new flavors, voting on next SKU)

4. **Strengthen retention:**
   - Contact your top 10 subscribers personally. Ask for feedback. Make them feel valued
   - Accelerate referral rewards for existing subscribers
   - Consider a loyalty bonus (6-month subscribers get a free upgrade/gift)

5. **Monitor, don't obsess:** Check what they're doing once a week. Learn from anything they do well. Ignore the rest

### Long-term View

In a market as large as Hyderabad (1 crore+ population), there is room for multiple wellness brands. Competition validates the market. The winner is not who launches first — it's who builds the deepest habits and strongest trust.

## Scenario 10: Founder Health / Burnout / Single Point of Failure

**Severity: CRITICAL (for business continuity)**
**Response time: Preventive — act before it happens**

### The Problem

At launch, Soma Delights = the founder. If the founder is sick for 3 days, there is no Soma Delights for 3 days. This is the single biggest operational risk.

### Prevention — The "2-Day Rule"

By Month 2, someone other than the founder should be able to run production and delivery for at least 2 days independently. This means:

1. **Document everything** — every recipe, every process, every vendor contact, every customer preference. This recipe standardization document is step 1. The supplier directory is step 2. The crisis playbook (this document) is step 3

2. **Train a kitchen helper:**
   - Hire a part-time helper (Rs 5,000-8,000/month for 3-4 hours/day, morning shift)
   - Train them on: ingredient prep, juicer operation, cleaning protocol, bottling, labeling
   - They should shadow you for 1 week, then you shadow them for 1 week
   - Test: can they produce a batch alone while you just watch?

3. **Create a "Founder Down" emergency protocol:**
   - Kitchen helper produces juice
   - Delivery rider delivers as normal
   - Customer communication: founder texts from bed if conscious, or helper sends pre-written message
   - No new customer onboarding during this period
   - Focus: keep existing subscribers served

4. **Train the delivery rider** to handle basic customer interactions:
   - "Juice is fresh, enjoy!"
   - "I'll let [Founder] know about your question"
   - Do NOT train rider to resolve complaints — that's founder's job, even remotely

### Burnout Prevention

The early days of a startup are intense. A home-kitchen food business is physically demanding. Burnout is not hypothetical — it's expected.

**Physical burnout prevention:**
- Production time should be under 3 hours/day. If it's more, you're either over-subscribed or under-equipped
- Get the anti-fatigue mat. Your feet and back will thank you
- Take one full day off per week (Sunday: no production, no delivery, no WhatsApp replies). Communicate this to subscribers: "Soma Delights delivers Monday to Saturday"
- Stretch and warm up before the 4 AM production shift (seriously)

**Mental burnout prevention:**
- Set WhatsApp business hours: respond to non-urgent messages between 9 AM and 8 PM only
- Batch customer communication (don't reply to every message instantly — check 3 times a day)
- Celebrate milestones: 10th subscriber, 100th bottle, first referral, first month. Write them down
- Keep a "nice things customers said" file. Read it when things feel hard

**When to pause:**
- You dread the morning alarm for more than a week straight
- Quality is slipping because you're too tired to care
- You're snapping at customers
- Physical symptoms: persistent back pain, repetitive strain, chronic fatigue

Pausing for 3-5 days (with subscriber notice) is infinitely better than burning out and quitting entirely. Your subscribers will understand. Most will wait.

> **Template (WhatsApp — temporary pause):**
> "Hi [Name]. I'm taking a short break to recharge and come back stronger. Deliveries will pause from [DATE] to [DATE]. Your subscription balance is safe and will be extended by the same number of days. Thank you for understanding. I'll be back soon with even better juice for you."

### Scaling Past Single Point of Failure

| Subscribers | Risk Level | Mitigation |
|-------------|-----------|------------|
| 1-15 | Acceptable — founder can manage | Document SOPs anyway |
| 15-30 | High — too many for one person long-term | Hire part-time kitchen helper |
| 30-50 | Critical — founder is the bottleneck | Kitchen helper + dedicated rider. Founder shifts to management + quality |
| 50+ | Unacceptable without team | Full kitchen team. Founder is quality + growth + customer relationships only |

## Monthly Crisis Review Checklist

On the first Sunday of every month, spend 30 minutes reviewing:

- [ ] Are all vendor backup contacts current?
- [ ] Is the delivery backup rider still available?
- [ ] Is the kitchen helper trained and recently practicing?
- [ ] Are production logs up to date?
- [ ] Are FSSAI documents current and displayed?
- [ ] Has any near-miss happened this month that should be documented?
- [ ] Is the 2-day ingredient buffer being maintained?
- [ ] Is the cold chain working properly (spot-check temperatures)?
- [ ] Are gel ice packs being replaced on schedule?
- [ ] Is the emergency contact sheet accurate?
- [ ] How is the founder feeling? Honest self-check on burnout risk
