# 22 — Team Design

> Staged hiring plan for Soma Delights. Who to hire, when, how much, and what the founder controls at each stage. Hyderabad labor market rates, cold-pressed drinks operations reality.

---

## Hiring Philosophy

Three principles that override everything else:

1. **Hire operations first, marketing never.** Word of mouth IS the marketing. The first 3 hires are always: kitchen helper, delivery rider, kitchen lead. You will never hire a "marketing person."
2. **Founder does everything first.** You cannot manage what you haven't done yourself. Every role you eventually hire for, you must have personally performed for at least 4 weeks.
3. **Hire for reliability, train for skill.** In Hyderabad's labor market for kitchen and delivery roles, showing up on time every day at 4 AM is rarer and more valuable than culinary skill. Hire the person who will be there at 4 AM in January rain.

| 4:00–5:00 PM | Ingredient sourcing for next day (Rythu Bazaar or supplier delivery) |
| 5:00–6:00 PM | Prep for next morning — wash ingredients, organize, pre-label bottles |

### What Founder Does

| Function | Owner | Hours/Week |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| Sourcing | Founder | 5 |
| Production | Founder | 14 |
| Delivery | Founder | 14 |
| Kitchen cleanup | Founder | 7 |
| Customer service (WhatsApp) | Founder | 5 |

| Bookkeeping & admin | Founder | 3 |

| **Total** | | **~60 hrs/week** |

### Why This Stage Is Critical

This is unsustainable and that's the point. You need to:
- Understand exact production timing per SKU
- Feel every delivery pain point (traffic, parking, customer availability)
- Know which customers are high-maintenance vs. easy
- Build muscle memory for quality control
- Identify which tasks to delegate first (hint: it's always kitchen prep)

### Decision Gate

Move to Stage 1 when: 20+ active customers AND you physically cannot produce + deliver for all of them alone AND you've done it for at least 4 weeks.

## Stage 1: Founder + Helper (20–50 Customers)

**Duration:** 2–3 months
**Monthly people cost:** Rs 8,000–10,000

### Team Structure

| Role | Person | Hours | Monthly Pay | Key Responsibilities |
|------|--------|-------|-------------|---------------------|
| Everything except prep | Founder | 50 hrs/week | — | Delivery, customer mgmt, growth, ops, QC |
| Kitchen Helper | Hire #1 | 4 AM – 8 AM (4 hrs, 6 days) | Rs 8,000–10,000 | Wash, chop, assist pressing, bottling, cleanup |

### Kitchen Helper — Hire Profile

| Criteria | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **Must-have** | Punctual (4 AM, non-negotiable), clean habits, physically able to stand 4 hours |
| **Nice-to-have** | Previous kitchen/hotel experience, familiarity with vegetables/fruits |
| **Where to find** | Local labor networks in Kukatpally, apartment complex staff referrals, Sulekha, OLX classifieds |
| **Trial period** | 1 week paid trial (Rs 300/day). Evaluate punctuality above all else. |
| **Training time** | 2–3 days working alongside founder |

### What Changes for Founder

| Before (Stage 0) | After (Stage 1) |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| 4 AM: production alone | 4 AM: supervise helper, handle pressing/bottling |
| Prep takes 1.5 hours | Prep takes 45 min with helper |
| Exhausted by delivery time | Fresher for delivery, better customer interactions |
| Zero time for growth | 2–3 hours freed up for pop-ups, partnerships |

### What Founder Tightly Controls

- Quality check on every bottle before dispatch (taste, appearance, label, seal)
- Customer communication — no one else touches WhatsApp
- Ingredient sourcing decisions — helper doesn't choose suppliers
- Recipe — helper follows exact instructions, no improvisation

### Monthly Cost Breakdown

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Kitchen helper salary | Rs 8,000–10,000 |
| Helper bonus (attendance, 26/26 days) | Rs 1,000 |
| **Total Stage 1 people cost** | **Rs 9,000–11,000/month** |

### Kitchen Lead — Hire Profile

This is your most important hire. This person runs production without you.

| Criteria | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **Must-have** | 2+ years kitchen/food production experience, can manage another person, understands food safety basics, extremely reliable |
| **Nice-to-have** | Experience with cold-press/juice bars, basic inventory management, can read/write recipes |
| **Where to find** | Hotel/restaurant kitchens (cooks looking for day-shift work), catering company alumni, juice bar staff |
| **Pay** | Rs 15,000–18,000 + attendance bonus Rs 1,000 + quality bonus Rs 1,000 |
| **Trial period** | 2 weeks alongside founder |
| **Training** | 1 week of side-by-side production, then 1 week of supervised solo production |

### What Kitchen Lead Controls

- Daily production schedule (which SKUs, how many of each)
- Ingredient prep and quality check (reject bad produce)
- Production process (pressing, bottling, labeling)
- Kitchen cleanup and sanitization
- Inventory tracking (what's running low)

### What Founder Still Controls

- Recipe development and changes
- Supplier selection and pricing negotiation
- Quality standards (periodic spot-checks, not daily oversight)
- Customer-facing everything
- Financial decisions
- Growth and partnerships

### Route Design at 100+ Customers

With 2 riders, you need 2 efficient routes:

| Route | Area | Customers | Deliveries/Morning | Rider |
|-------|------|-----------|--------------------|----|
| Route A | KPHB Colony, Pragathi Nagar | 50–70 | 50–70 in 2.5 hrs | Rider 1 |
| Route B | Miyapur, Chandanagar | 50–70 | 50–70 in 2.5 hrs | Rider 2 |

Target: 25–30 deliveries per hour per rider in dense apartment clusters. This is achievable when you have 8–15 customers per apartment complex.

## Compensation Design

### Base Principles

1. **Pay above market for reliability.** A kitchen helper who shows up at 4 AM every day is worth Rs 10,000, not Rs 7,000. The cost of a no-show (you do their work + yours) far exceeds the Rs 3,000 difference.
2. **Attendance bonus, not absence penalty.** Rs 1,000 bonus for 26/26 days. Positive framing works better than deductions.
3. **Performance bonuses tied to measurable outcomes.** Not subjective assessments.

### Bonus Structure

| Role | Bonus Metric | Bonus Amount |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Kitchen Helper | 26/26 days attendance | Rs 1,000/month |
| Kitchen Lead | Attendance + zero quality complaints | Rs 1,000 + Rs 1,000/month |
| Delivery Rider | 98%+ on-time delivery | Rs 1,000/month |
| Delivery Rider | Zero customer complaints in month | Rs 500/month |
| Production Manager | Waste < 5% + on-time production | Rs 2,000/month |
| Customer Support | Response time < 30 min avg | Rs 500/month |

### Benefits (Even for Part-Time Staff)

| Benefit | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Festival bonus | 1 month salary bonus at Diwali/Sankranti (after 6 months tenure) |
| Products | Free Soma Delights products daily (they should know what they're making) |
| Medical emergency | Rs 5,000 emergency advance available (deducted over 3 months) |
| Leave | 1 paid day off per month (after 3 months) |
| Training | Food safety training at company expense |

## Common Hiring Mistakes in Food Startups

| Mistake | Why It Happens | What to Do Instead |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| Hiring a "marketing person" first | Founder thinks growth = marketing hire | Growth comes from product quality + referrals. Hire operations. |
| Hiring friends/family for kitchen | Feels safe, avoids awkward hiring | Friends don't take 4 AM seriously. Hire professionals, keep friendship. |
| Not paying enough and losing people | "It's just a helper role" | A reliable 4 AM person is rare. Pay Rs 10,000 not Rs 7,000. The no-show cost is higher. |
| Hiring delivery riders without trial | "He has a bike and knows the area" | 1 week trial. On-time % is everything. One late delivery = one unhappy subscriber. |
| Founder not delegating QC | "Only I can check quality" | Train the kitchen lead, do spot-checks. You can't taste every bottle at 300 customers. |
| Hiring too many people at once | "We're growing fast" | One hire at a time. Integrate for 2 weeks before the next hire. |
| No written SOPs for kitchen staff | "I'll show them" | You won't remember to show the 14th detail. Write it down. Laminate it. Put it on the wall. |

---

*The right hiring sequence is the difference between a business that scales and a business that collapses under its own weight. Hire operations. Hire for reliability. Pay well. Train relentlessly. And never hire a marketing person — if the product is right, the customers will do that work for you.*
