# 21 — Compliance & Food Safety

> India-specific regulatory, licensing, labeling, and hygiene requirements for a home-kitchen cold-pressed drinks business in Hyderabad, Telangana. Practical, staged — what you need at launch vs. what you need at scale.

---

## FSSAI Registration

### Stage 1: Launch (Under Rs 12 Lakh Turnover)

**FSSAI Basic Registration (Form A)** — this is all you need to start selling legally.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Form | Form A — Basic Registration |
| Eligibility | Petty food manufacturers, turnover < Rs 12 lakh/year |
| Fee | Rs 100 |
| Validity | 1–5 years (choose 5 years, pay Rs 500 total) |
| Processing time | 7 working days (often faster online) |
| Apply at | [foscos.fssai.gov.in](https://foscos.fssai.gov.in) |
| Can you sell while application is pending? | Yes — the application receipt number is your interim license |

**What you need to apply:**
- Aadhaar card (identity proof)
- Passport-size photo
- Proof of address (utility bill, rent agreement)
- Self-declaration of food safety compliance

**Critical:** Print and display the FSSAI registration certificate in your kitchen. Keep a copy with delivery bags. Inspectors can ask anytime.

### Stage 2: Scale (Rs 12 Lakh+ Turnover)

**FSSAI State License** — required when you cross Rs 12 lakh annual turnover or if you start selling across Telangana state boundaries.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Form | Form B — State License |
| Eligibility | Turnover Rs 12 lakh – Rs 20 crore |
| Fee | Rs 2,000/year |
| Validity | 1–5 years |
| Processing time | 30–60 days |
| Inspection | Yes — food safety officer will visit your premises |

**Additional documents for State License:**
- Food safety management plan
- List of food products with brand names
- Blueprint/layout of the processing area
- Water test report (potable water certificate)
- List of equipment with capacity
- NOC from municipality (if applicable)
- Medical fitness certificates for food handlers

**Timeline reality:** You won't need the State License for at least 6–9 months. By then you should have a proper production space, not a home kitchen. Plan the transition around Month 6.

## Business Structure

### Comparison: Which Structure When

| Criteria | Sole Proprietorship | LLP | Pvt Ltd |
|----------|-------------------|-----|---------|
| **Setup cost** | Rs 0 (just PAN + bank account) | Rs 5,000–8,000 | Rs 10,000–15,000 |
| **Setup time** | 1 day | 7–15 days | 10–20 days |
| **Compliance burden** | Minimal (ITR only) | Annual filing with MCA | Annual filing, audit, board meetings |
| **Liability** | Unlimited (personal assets at risk) | Limited to contribution | Limited to share capital |
| **Bank account** | Open with trade name + PAN | Separate LLP account | Separate company account |
| **Investment ready** | No — investors won't put money into proprietorship | Possible but uncommon | Yes — standard for investment |
| **Best for** | Launch to Rs 20 lakh | Rs 20 lakh – Rs 1 crore, no outside investors | Taking investment, scaling beyond Rs 1 crore |

### Recommendation

**Launch as sole proprietorship.** Zero cost, zero delay. You can open a current account at any bank with your PAN card and a trade name declaration. "Soma Delights" as a trade name is sufficient.

**Convert to LLP or Pvt Ltd when:**
- Annual turnover approaches Rs 20 lakh (GST threshold)
- You want to bring in a partner or co-founder
- You are ready to take outside investment
- You need limited liability protection

**Trademark:** File a trademark application for "Soma Delights" early. Costs Rs 4,500 for proprietorship (Rs 9,000 for company). Takes 6–18 months for registration but the TM symbol can be used from Day 1 of filing. Apply at [ipindia.gov.in](https://ipindia.gov.in).

## GST

### GST Rates for Your Products

| Product Type | HSN Code | GST Rate | Notes |
|-------------|----------|----------|-------|
| Fresh fruit/vegetable juice (unpackaged, no preservatives) | 2009 | 0% (exempt) | Only if sold loose, unbranded, unpackaged |
| Packaged/branded fruit juice | 2009 | 12% | Your bottled cold-pressed juices fall here |
| Packaged/branded vegetable juice | 2009 | 12% | Green juices, beet blends, etc. |
| Fruit pulp products | 2007 | 12% | Future pulp SKUs |
| Fermented beverages (non-alcoholic) | 2202 | 18% | Future kombucha, fermented shots |
| Meal plans / prepared food | 2106 | 5% (if < Rs 7,500/day room + food) or 18% | Complex — get CA advice when you launch meals |

### Registration Threshold

| Category | Threshold |
|----------|-----------|
| Goods (Telangana) | Rs 40 lakh annual turnover |
| Services | Rs 20 lakh annual turnover |
| Voluntary registration | Available at any turnover |

### Recommendation

**Register voluntarily from Day 1.** Here's why:

1. **Input tax credit** — You pay GST on bottles, labels, packaging, equipment. With registration, you claim this back. Without registration, it's a pure cost.
2. **Professional credibility** — GSTIN on invoices signals a real business, not a hobby.
3. **Future-proofing** — No disruption when you cross thresholds.
4. **Cost** — Registration is free. Filing returns is quarterly (GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B). Use ClearTax or Zoho Books to auto-file. CA cost: Rs 1,000–2,000/quarter.

**Composition Scheme option:** If turnover < Rs 1.5 crore, you can opt for Composition Scheme — pay flat 1% GST on turnover, no input credit. Simpler but less beneficial if your input costs are high (packaging, equipment). Evaluate at Month 6.

## Labeling Requirements

Every bottle leaving your kitchen must have a label with the following. No exceptions.

### Mandatory Label Elements

| Element | Requirement | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Product name** | Clear, prominent | "Green Morning — Cold-Pressed Juice" |
| **Ingredients list** | Descending order by weight | "Cucumber, Spinach, Green Apple, Lemon, Ginger, Mint" |
| **Net quantity** | ml or g, prominently displayed | "300 ml" |
| **MRP** | Inclusive of all taxes | "MRP Rs 99 (inclusive of all taxes)" |
| **Date of manufacture** | DD/MM/YYYY format | "Mfg: 15/03/2026" |
| **Best before** | Hours or days from manufacture | "Best Before: 24 hours from manufacture (keep refrigerated)" |
| **Manufacturer name & address** | Full address | "Soma Delights, [Your Address], Kukatpally, Hyderabad 500072" |
| **FSSAI logo + license number** | FSSAI logo with number | FSSAI logo + 14-digit number |
| **Veg/Non-veg symbol** | Green dot in green square for veg | Green dot (all your products are veg) |
| **Batch number** | Unique per production batch | "Batch: 20260315-A" |
| **Storage instructions** | How to store | "Keep refrigerated below 8 deg C. Consume within 24 hours." |
| **Allergen declaration** | If applicable | "Contains: None of the 8 major allergens" |

### Nutritional Information

FSSAI technically requires nutritional info on packaged food. However:

- **At launch (Basic Registration):** Nutritional labeling is not strictly enforced for small manufacturers. Include "per serving" basics if you can: calories, carbs, protein, fat, sugar.
- **At scale (State License):** Full nutritional panel required. Get lab testing done — costs Rs 3,000–5,000 per SKU at an NABL-accredited lab. Labs in Hyderabad: SGS, Eurofins, CFTRI-approved labs.
- **Practical approach:** Use a nutrition calculator (e.g., IFCT 2017 database or Nutritionix) for approximate values at launch. Get formal lab tests before applying for State License.

### Label Design — Practical

**Phase 1 (first 50 customers):** Pre-printed adhesive stickers. Design once in Canva, print at any Kukatpally print shop. Cost: Rs 3–5 per sticker for small runs.

**Phase 2 (50–200 customers):** Professional label design + bulk printing. Flex printing on waterproof sticker paper. Cost: Rs 1.50–2.50 per label at 1,000+ quantity.

**Phase 3 (200+ customers):** Custom-designed labels with die-cut shapes, premium feel. Consider shrink-wrap sleeves. Cost: Rs 2–4 per label.

## Kitchen & Hygiene Standards

### Mandatory Setup — Before First Sale

Your home kitchen must meet these minimum standards. An FSSAI inspector likely won't visit during Basic Registration, but these are non-negotiable for product quality and customer safety.

#### Physical Space

| Requirement | Standard | Estimated Cost |
|-------------|----------|---------------|
| Separate production area | Dedicated space, not shared with household cooking | Rs 0 (use existing room/kitchen extension) |
| Stainless steel work surface | At least one 4x2 ft SS table for prep | Rs 3,000–5,000 |
| Food-grade flooring | Clean, non-porous, washable floor. Tiles work. | Rs 0 (if existing tiles) |
| Adequate lighting | Bright enough to see ingredient quality, defects | Rs 500 (LED tube lights) |
| Ventilation | Exhaust fan or open window for airflow | Rs 1,500 |
| Handwash station | Dedicated sink with soap dispenser near prep area | Rs 0 (existing sink) |
| Waste bins | Separate wet and dry waste bins with lids | Rs 500 |
| Storage shelving | SS or plastic racks for ingredients, bottles, supplies | Rs 2,000 |

#### Equipment Hygiene

| Item | Protocol |
|------|----------|
| Cold-press juicer | Disassemble and clean after every batch. No residue in filter screens. |
| Cutting boards | Color-coded: green for vegetables, white for fruits. Replace every 3 months. |
| Knives | Clean between ingredient types. Sharpen weekly. |
| Bottles | Wash with hot water + food-grade sanitizer before filling. Air dry on clean rack. |
| Work surfaces | Wipe with food-grade sanitizer before and after every production session. |
| Refrigerator | Dedicated fridge for finished products. Temperature: 2–6 deg C. Check daily with thermometer. |

#### Personal Hygiene

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Handwashing | Before starting, after touching phone/hair/face, after handling waste |
| Gloves | Food-grade disposable gloves during bottling. Change between batches. |
| Hair covering | Cap or hairnet during production. Non-negotiable. |
| Apron | Clean apron daily. |
| No jewelry | Remove rings, bracelets, watches during production. |
| Health | Do not handle food if ill (fever, cold, stomach issues). |
| Nails | Short, clean, no nail polish. |

#### Water Quality

- Use RO-purified water for all juice production.
- If using municipal water: get tested quarterly for TDS, coliform, heavy metals.
- Water testing labs in Hyderabad: Telangana State Pollution Control Board lab, or private labs (Rs 500–1,000 per test).
- Keep water test reports filed — inspectors ask for these.

#### Pest Control

- Monthly pest control for production area. Professional service: Rs 500–800/visit.
- Door screens/strips to prevent insect entry.
- No open food storage — everything in airtight containers.
- Daily visual inspection: check for droppings, gnaw marks, insect presence.
- Keep pest control receipts filed.

#### Temperature Monitoring

| Point | Target | Check Frequency |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| Ingredient storage fridge | 2–6 deg C | Twice daily (morning + evening) |
| Finished product fridge | 2–6 deg C | Before dispatch, midday, evening |
| Delivery bag (insulated) | Below 10 deg C at delivery | Spot-check with probe thermometer |
| Room temperature during production | Below 30 deg C | During production (use fan/AC if needed) |

Buy a digital probe thermometer (Rs 300–500) and a fridge thermometer (Rs 200). Log temperatures in a simple register.

## Telangana-Specific Local Permissions

### Operating from Home (Launch Phase)

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Can you run a food business from a residential apartment? | Technically restricted by most apartment bylaws. In practice, small-scale home baking/cooking is widely tolerated in Hyderabad. |
| Do you need apartment association permission? | Not legally, but practically — yes. Inform your association, frame it as "home kitchen for subscription wellness drinks." Most associations in KPHB/Miyapur won't object if there's no nuisance (noise, smell, delivery traffic). |
| Is there a municipal restriction? | GHMC doesn't actively enforce home-kitchen businesses at micro scale. Risk is very low during Phase 0–1. |
| When do you need formal commercial permission? | When you move to a separate production unit, hire multiple staff, or get formal complaints. |

### GHMC Trade License

Required when operating from a commercial space or when your business is formally recognized beyond home scale.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Apply at | GHMC office or online at ghmc.gov.in |
| Fee | Rs 2,000–5,000/year depending on area and business size |
| Documents needed | FSSAI license, address proof, ID proof, business registration |
| Processing time | 15–30 days |

### Telangana State Food Safety Authority

- Telangana has an active food safety enforcement wing under the Commissioner of Food Safety.
- Inspections are more common for restaurants and retail. Home-based small manufacturers rarely get inspected during Basic Registration period.
- Keep your paperwork clean anyway — registration certificate displayed, labels compliant, temperature logs maintained.

### Shop and Establishment Act

If you hire employees, you need to register under the Telangana Shops and Establishments Act.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| When required | When you hire your first employee |
| Apply at | Labour Department, Telangana (online) |
| Fee | Rs 500–2,000 |
| Documents | Business registration, address proof, employee details |
| Compliance | Maintain employee register, follow working hours/leave rules |

## Record-Keeping Requirements

Maintain these records from Day 1. Use a simple register or Google Sheet.

| Record | What to Log | Frequency |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Production log | Date, batch number, SKUs produced, quantities, ingredients used | Daily |
| Temperature log | Fridge temp (morning + evening), delivery bag spot-checks | Twice daily |
| Ingredient sourcing log | Supplier name, ingredient, quantity, date, cost, origin | Every purchase |
| Delivery log | Customer name, SKU, delivery time, any issues | Daily |
| Complaint log | Customer complaint, date, resolution, corrective action | As needed |
| Cleaning log | What was cleaned, when, by whom, sanitizer used | Daily |
| Pest control log | Date, service provider, areas treated, chemicals used | Monthly |
| Water test results | Lab name, date, parameters tested, results | Quarterly |
| Staff medical records | Employee name, medical certificate date, fitness status | Annual per employee |

These records serve three purposes: (1) FSSAI compliance, (2) quality control, (3) operational improvement. When something goes wrong — and it will — these logs help you trace the problem.

## Common Compliance Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's Dangerous | Prevention |
|---------|--------------------|------------|
| No FSSAI registration | Selling food without registration is a criminal offense under FSS Act. Fine: Rs 5 lakh. | Apply before your first sale. Takes 1 hour. |
| Missing label elements | Customer complaint or inspector visit = product seizure + fine | Use the checklist above. Print a test label, verify every element. |
| No batch numbers | Can't trace problems back to source if a customer gets sick | Simple format: YYYYMMDD-A (date + batch letter) |
| Ignoring temperature | Cold-pressed juice at room temp for 2+ hours = bacterial growth | Log temps twice daily. Insulated bags for delivery. Ice packs in summer. |
| Not testing water | Contaminated water = contaminated product = customer illness | RO water minimum. Lab test quarterly. |
| Using household kitchen items | Cross-contamination from household spices, oils, non-veg items | Dedicated equipment, separate storage, clearly labeled |
| No cleaning schedule | Gradual buildup of residue, mold, bacteria | Written cleaning SOP. Daily, weekly, monthly tasks. |
| Verbal-only record-keeping | "I remember" is not a food safety system | Written logs from Day 1. Non-negotiable. |
