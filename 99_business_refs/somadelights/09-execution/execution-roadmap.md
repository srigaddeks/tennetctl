# 24 — Execution Roadmap

> Staged execution plan for Soma Delights. Each phase has clear goals, deliverables, team, KPIs, systems, budget, and a decision gate. No phase starts without the previous gate being passed.

---

## Roadmap Overview

| Phase | Timeline | Customers | Team | Goal |
|-------|----------|-----------|------|------|
| 0 — Validation | Week 1–2 | 5–10 | Founder | Confirm people will pay |
| 1 — Pilot | Week 3 – Month 2 | 30–50 | Founder + helper | Prove subscription model |
| 2 — Neighborhood Model | Month 3–5 | 100–150 | Founder + kitchen + rider | Prove repeatable routes |
| 3 — Multi-Cluster Growth | Month 6–9 | 200–300 | Small ops team | Expand + introduce shots |
| 4 — Wellness Expansion | Month 10–15 | 300–500 | Ops team + support | Launch wellness plans |
| 5 — Meal Plans & Ecosystem | Month 15+ | 500+ | Full team | Meal plan pilot |

## Phase 0: Validation (Week 1–2)

### Goal
Confirm that real people will pay real money for daily cold-pressed juice delivery. Not "interested" — paying.

### Deliverables

| Deliverable | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Home kitchen operational | Stainless steel surface, cold-press juicer, dedicated fridge, sanitization setup |
| FSSAI Basic Registration applied | Receipt number = interim license |
| 3 core SKUs finalized | Green Morning, Citrus Charge, Beet Power (recipes tested, COGS validated) |
| Compliant labels printed | First batch of stickers with all mandatory elements |
| WhatsApp Business account | Catalog, saved replies, broadcast lists |
| 5–10 paying customers | From personal network — friends, family, apartment neighbors |
| Production workflow established | Prep → Press → Bottle → Label → Chill → Dispatch timing documented |
| Delivery process tested | Route timing, insulated bag performance, customer handoff protocol |

### Team
Founder only.

### Daily Operations

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 4:00 AM | Production (3 SKUs, 5–10 bottles) |
| 5:30 AM | Pack and load |
| 6:00–7:30 AM | Deliver |
| 8:00 AM | Clean, log data |
| 10:00 AM – 2:00 PM | Operations, customer conversations, prep for next day |
| 4:00 PM | Ingredient sourcing |

### Budget

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Cold-press juicer (if not owned) | Rs 15,000–25,000 |
| Stainless steel table | Rs 4,000 |
| Bottles, caps, labels (first 200) | Rs 4,000 |
| Ingredients (2 weeks) | Rs 3,000 |
| Insulated delivery bag | Rs 1,500 |
| Ice packs (10 reusable) | Rs 700 |
| Thermometer, gloves, caps, sanitizer | Rs 1,000 |
| FSSAI registration | Rs 100 |
| **Total Phase 0** | **Rs 29,300–39,300** |

### KPIs

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Paying customers by end of Week 2 | 5+ |
| Repeat orders (same person, 2nd order) | 5+ |
| Positive taste feedback | 80%+ |
| On-time delivery (within stated window) | 100% |
| COGS per bottle | Below Rs 45 |
| Customer willingness to subscribe weekly | 3+ |

### Systems
- WhatsApp Business for orders and communication
- Google Sheets for production log, delivery log, and finance tracking
- Physical registers for temperature and batch logs
- Razorpay or UPI for payments

### Decision Gate

**Pass:** 5+ people reorder in Week 2 AND at least 3 people express interest in a weekly subscription AND unit economics are positive (revenue per bottle > COGS + delivery cost).

**Fail:** Fewer than 3 reorders, or consistent taste/quality complaints, or COGS above Rs 50. If fail: reformulate products, adjust pricing, test for 1 more week. If still failing: reconsider product assortment entirely.

## Critical Path Summary

The entire roadmap hinges on these non-negotiable milestones:

| Milestone | Deadline | If Missed |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| 5 paying + reordering customers | End of Week 2 | Reformulate product, reprice, or reconsider |
| 40 subscribers with 60% retention | End of Month 2 | Fix product or subscription model before growing |
| Model works in 2nd cluster | End of Month 4 | First cluster may have been anomaly — investigate |
| Positive cash flow | End of Month 7 | Cost structure review — something is wrong |
| 200 customers | End of Month 9 | Growth engine not working — fix referral or density |
| Operations independent of founder | End of Month 12 | Hire production manager or burn out |

---

## Phase Transition Rules

1. **Never skip a phase.** Phase 0 learnings are essential for Phase 1 decisions. Phase 1 metrics determine Phase 2 approach.
2. **Decision gates are real.** If you don't pass the gate, you don't move forward. You fix what's broken or you pivot.
3. **Speed is less important than learning.** Completing Phase 1 in 4 weeks with weak retention is worse than taking 8 weeks with strong retention.
4. **Each phase has ONE primary metric.** Phase 0: reorders. Phase 1: retention. Phase 2: route profitability. Phase 3: cash flow. Phase 4: plan adoption. Phase 5: independence from founder.
5. **Document every phase transition.** What worked, what didn't, what you'd do differently. This becomes your playbook for geographic expansion.

---

*This roadmap is a compass, not a GPS. Real life will force detours. The decision gates are the fixed points — they tell you whether you're on the right road, even if you took a different path to get there.*
