# Soma Delights — Customer Personas

## How to Use This Document

These 8 personas represent distinct customer archetypes in the Kukatpally–Miyapur–Chandanagar corridor. Each persona includes a structured profile table and a narrative that explains how they discover, try, adopt, and retain (or churn from) Soma Delights.

The personas are ordered by priority — Persona 1 is the primary launch target, Persona 8 is a later-stage opportunity. **Do not try to serve all 8 from Day 1.** Focus on Personas 1–3 for launch, expand to 4–6 by Month 3, and reach 7–8 by Month 6.

### Rahul/Priya's Journey

**Discovery:** Rahul sees his colleague Sneha carrying a distinctive bottle at the office pantry. He asks about it. Sneha says "It's this wellness thing, cold-pressed juice delivered every morning. I've been doing it for 3 weeks, I feel way better." This is more powerful than any ad.

**Trial:** Rahul texts the WhatsApp number Sneha shares. Orders a 3-day trial pack (Rs 400). Gets his first delivery at 7 AM next morning with an ingredient card explaining why spinach + apple + ginger is a powerful combination.

**Conversion:** By Day 3, Rahul notices he feels less sluggish in the morning. The daily WhatsApp tip taught him something he didn't know about turmeric absorption. He subscribes to the daily plan.

**Retention risk:** Week 3 — Rahul goes on a work trip for 4 days. Pauses subscription. Will he restart? Yes, because: (a) he notices the difference when he stops, (b) he gets a "welcome back" message when he returns, (c) his morning routine feels incomplete without it.

### Sandeep & Divya's Journey

**Discovery:** Divya hears about Soma Delights at a parent meetup in the apartment complex. Another mother mentions that her picky 7-year-old actually drinks the fruit-forward blend. This is the trigger — if a picky kid drinks it, her kids might too.

**Conversion:** Family subscription: 5 days/week, 3 bottles/day. Mix of green (adults) and fruit-forward (kid). Monthly spend: Rs 8,000–9,000.

**Stickiness:** Family subscriptions have the lowest churn in the entire customer base. Once a family routine is established and kids are on board, the switching cost is enormous. Who wants to fight a "but I want my juice!" battle with a 6-year-old?

**Discovery:** Karthik's wife Smitha orders for herself. After a week, she adds a bottle for Karthik. He doesn't choose, doesn't order, doesn't manage — he just drinks whatever shows up at his spot in the fridge.

**Lesson for the business:** Sometimes the buyer is not the consumer. Design the subscription management for the family organizer (usually the spouse), not just the individual.

## Cross-Persona Dynamics

Understanding how personas interact and influence each other is critical for organic growth.

### Referral Chains

```
Tech Professional (Persona 1)
├── Refers colleagues at work → More Tech Professionals
├── Orders for parents → Senior Wellness Parents (Persona 8)
├── Spouse discovers it → Women's Wellness Consumer (Persona 4)
└── Mentions at gym → Gym Enthusiast (Persona 3)

Health-Conscious Young Adult (Persona 2)
├── Posts on Instagram → More Young Adults
├── Shares in fitness group → Gym Enthusiast (Persona 3)
└── Recommends to older family → Senior Wellness Parents (Persona 8)

Women's Wellness Consumer (Persona 4)
├── Shares in apartment women's group → More Women (Persona 4)
├── Orders for husband → Premium Convenience Buyer (Persona 6)
├── Orders for kids → Young Family (Persona 5)
└── Tells mother/mother-in-law → Senior Wellness (Persona 8)

Gym Enthusiast (Persona 3)
├── Gym buddy network → More Gym Enthusiasts
├── Trainer recommends to clients → Multiple personas
└── Mentions in sports/running group → Health-Conscious Young Adults (Persona 2)
```

### Household Clustering

The most powerful growth mechanism is **household expansion:**

1. One person subscribes (often Persona 1, 2, or 4)
2. Spouse/partner tries it → Second subscription
3. Kid-friendly option added → Family subscription
4. Parent visits, tries it → Orders for parents in another complex
5. Single household generates Rs 3,500 → Rs 8,000–12,000/month in 3–6 months

### Community Clustering

Within an apartment complex, growth follows this pattern:

```
Month 1: 2–3 subscribers in a 500-unit complex
Month 2: 5–8 (referrals from original 3)
Month 3: 12–15 (morning walk conversations, women's group mentions)
Month 4: 20–25 (critical mass — it's now "a thing" in the complex)
Month 6: 35–50 (5–10% penetration, community identity)
```

At 5–10% complex penetration, Soma Delights becomes part of the community identity. New residents discover it through the apartment WhatsApp group or during their first morning walk. Growth becomes self-sustaining within that complex.
