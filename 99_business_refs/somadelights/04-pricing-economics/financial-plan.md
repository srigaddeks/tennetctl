## Break-Even Analysis

### Daily Break-Even: How Many Subscribers Cover All Costs

**Monthly fixed costs (Month 3-6 average):** Rs 30,000-35,000
**Gross margin per subscriber per month:** Rs 2,200 (at Rs 150/day, 26 deliveries, Rs 65 variable cost)

**Break-even subscribers = Fixed Costs / Gross Margin per Subscriber**

| Phase | Fixed Costs/Month | Gross Margin/Sub | Break-Even Subs |
|-------|-------------------|------------------|----------------|
| Month 1-2 (founder delivers) | Rs 12,500 | Rs 2,200 | 6 subscribers |
| Month 3-4 (first rider) | Rs 30,300 | Rs 2,200 | 14 subscribers |
| Month 5-6 (growing ops) | Rs 37,500 | Rs 2,200 | 17 subscribers |
| Month 7-9 (scaling) | Rs 45,000 | Rs 2,200 | 21 subscribers |
| Month 10-12 (full ops) | Rs 60,000 | Rs 2,200 | 28 subscribers |

**Key insight:** Even with a rider and growing fixed costs, you only need 28 daily subscribers to cover everything. At Rs 150/day, 28 subscribers = Rs 1,09,200/month revenue, Rs 60,000 fixed costs, Rs 50,960 variable costs — a thin but real profit.

### Including Setup Cost Recovery

Setup cost: Rs 75,000-85,000
Monthly net profit at 30 subscribers: Rs 6,000-10,000
**Months to recover setup cost:** 8-14 months (depending on scenario)

At 50 subscribers: Monthly net profit ~Rs 25,000-35,000. Setup recovered in 2-3 months.

## Cash Flow Pressure Points

### Month 1-2: The Investment Phase

| Issue | Detail | Mitigation |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| Setup costs before revenue | Rs 75,000+ spent before first payment received | Collect monthly subscription payment upfront (start of month) |
| Ingredient front-loading | Buy 2 weeks of ingredients before knowing demand | Start with smaller batches, buy 3-4 days of stock at a time |
| Free samples cost | Pop-up sampling costs ingredients but generates zero immediate revenue | Budget Rs 1,500/month for sampling. Consider it acquisition cost. |

### Month 3: The Rider Hire

| Issue | Detail | Mitigation |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| Fixed cost jump | Rs 12,000/month for rider before revenue justifies it | Hire only when delivery time exceeds 2.5 hours (founder can't handle volume + production) |
| Rider reliability | New rider may be inconsistent, damaging delivery promise | Hire 2 weeks before needed. Train on route. Shadow for 3 days. |

### Month 4-6: Growth vs. Cash

| Issue | Detail | Mitigation |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| Ingredient costs scale before revenue stabilizes | More customers = more ingredient purchases, but payments come monthly | Collect payment on the 28th for the following month. This gives you cash BEFORE you buy ingredients. |
| Packaging reorder cycles | Labels and bottles ordered in bulk (500+) require upfront payment | Time reorders 2 weeks before running out. Negotiate credit terms with label printer if possible. |
| Booklet reprints | Rs 8,000-12,000 per batch of 100 | Print quarterly. Budget as a fixed quarterly expense. |

### Month 7-12: Scaling Operations

| Issue | Detail | Mitigation |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| Second rider hire | Another Rs 12,000-15,000/month fixed cost | Only when first rider's route exceeds 3 hours or 80+ deliveries |
| Equipment upgrade | Juicer may need replacement or upgrade at 100+ daily bottles | Budget Rs 20,000-30,000 for equipment upgrade around Month 8-10 |
| Working capital needs | Larger ingredient orders, more packaging inventory | Maintain 1 month of fixed costs as cash buffer (Rs 45,000-60,000) |

## Working Capital Requirements

**Rule of thumb:** Always have at least 6 weeks of fixed costs in cash reserve.

| Phase | Monthly Fixed Costs | Required Cash Buffer | Reasoning |
|-------|--------------------|--------------------|-----------|
| Month 1-2 | Rs 12,500 | Rs 25,000 | 2 months of costs — you're still figuring things out |
| Month 3-6 | Rs 30,000-37,500 | Rs 55,000-65,000 | 6 weeks of costs — rider salary must be guaranteed |
| Month 7-12 | Rs 45,000-60,000 | Rs 75,000-90,000 | 6 weeks of costs — two riders, growing ingredient bill |

**Total cash needed to start (including buffer):**
- Setup costs: Rs 75,000-85,000
- Working capital buffer: Rs 25,000
- **Total: Rs 1,00,000-1,10,000**

This is well within the Rs 2,00,000 budget, leaving Rs 90,000-1,00,000 as additional runway if things go slower than expected.

## What Numbers MUST Be True

These are the non-negotiable financial guardrails. If any of these fail consistently for 2+ months, something fundamental needs to change.

### 1. Gross Margin Per Bottle: > 45%

| Metric | Minimum | Target | Danger Zone |
|--------|---------|--------|-------------|
| Gross margin | 45% | 55% | < 40% |

If gross margin drops below 45%, either ingredient costs are too high (negotiate, change suppliers, adjust recipes) or pricing is too low (raise prices, reduce bottle size, or add value to justify premium).

### 2. Monthly Retention Rate: > 65%

| Metric | Minimum | Target | Danger Zone |
|--------|---------|--------|-------------|
| Month-to-month retention | 65% | 75% | < 60% |

Below 65% retention, you're on a treadmill — acquiring customers as fast as you're losing them. At 60% retention, you need to acquire 40 customers to grow by 10. At 75%, you need 25 to grow by 10. Fix retention before spending time on acquisition.

### 3. Trial-to-Subscription Conversion: > 35%

| Metric | Minimum | Target | Danger Zone |
|--------|---------|--------|-------------|
| 3-day trial → monthly subscription | 35% | 50% | < 25% |

If less than 35% of trial customers convert, either the product isn't good enough, the onboarding is weak, or you're attracting the wrong trial customers. Investigate: taste feedback, price sensitivity, delivery experience during trial.

### 4. Delivery Cost Per Bottle: < Rs 20

| Metric | Minimum | Target | Danger Zone |
|--------|---------|--------|-------------|
| Amortized delivery cost per bottle | — | Rs 12-15 | > Rs 25 |

Delivery cost is directly tied to route density. If delivery cost exceeds Rs 20/bottle, your customer clusters are too spread out. Focus on densifying existing clusters before expanding to new areas.

### 5. Monthly Revenue Per Subscriber: > Rs 3,500

| Metric | Minimum | Target | Danger Zone |
|--------|---------|--------|-------------|
| Monthly revenue per active subscriber | Rs 3,500 | Rs 4,500 | < Rs 3,000 |

If average revenue per subscriber is below Rs 3,500, too many customers are on low-frequency plans (3x/week) or pausing frequently. Either upsell to higher frequency plans or investigate why daily isn't working for them.

### 6. Customer Acquisition Cost: < Rs 500

| Metric | Minimum | Target | Danger Zone |
|--------|---------|--------|-------------|
| CAC (all-in: pop-ups, samples, referral credits) | — | Rs 150-300 | > Rs 500 |

With zero paid advertising, CAC should be naturally low. If it creeps above Rs 500, you're over-investing in events or giving too much away in sampling. The referral program's 10% is a variable cost, not CAC — it only costs you money when it works.

### 7. Referral Rate: > 20% of New Customers (by Month 3)

| Metric | Minimum | Target | Danger Zone |
|--------|---------|--------|-------------|
| % of new customers from referrals | 20% | 35-40% | < 15% |

If referrals aren't generating at least 20% of new customers by Month 3, either the referral program isn't being promoted enough, the incentive isn't motivating, or (most likely) the product/experience isn't good enough to recommend. The referral rate is the truest measure of product-market fit.

## Monthly Financial Review Checklist

Every 1st of the month, review these numbers:

- [ ] Active subscriber count (vs. last month, vs. target)
- [ ] New subscribers added (total + by channel)
- [ ] Subscribers lost (total + reasons from exit surveys)
- [ ] Monthly revenue collected (vs. billed — note payment failures)
- [ ] Ingredient costs this month (total + per-bottle average)
- [ ] Delivery costs (total + per-bottle average)
- [ ] Fixed costs (any unexpected expenses?)
- [ ] Gross margin per bottle (is it above 45%?)
- [ ] Net profit/loss for the month
- [ ] Cumulative cash position (do we have 6 weeks buffer?)
- [ ] Referral wallet balances (total obligation if everyone cashes out)
- [ ] Trial conversion rate
- [ ] Referral rate (% of new customers from referrals)

---

*Financial planning for a micro-wellness brand is not about projecting to impress investors. It's about knowing exactly how many bottles you need to make, how many customers you need to keep, and how much cash you need in the bank to sleep well at night. These numbers are your compass.*
