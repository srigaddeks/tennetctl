# Starter Kit & Free Week Acquisition Strategy

> Until 500 paying customers, every new potential customer gets a full free week — no payment required. The Premium Starter Kit arrives on Day 1 as a gift-worthy unboxing experience. Demo Packs at kiosks feed the top of funnel. This document covers the complete strategy, costs, logistics, conversion flow, and phase-out triggers.

---

## 1. Strategy Overview

### Why Free Week Until 500 Customers

The cold-pressed juice market in Hyderabad has a trust problem. Customers have been burned by Instagram brands that disappear after two weeks, street juice with hidden sugar, and overpriced national brands that taste like they were pressed last Thursday. Soma Delights solves all of this — but the customer does not know that yet.

A free week removes every barrier to trial:

| Barrier | How Free Week Removes It |
|---------|--------------------------|
| **Price risk** | Zero financial commitment. Nothing to lose. |
| **Taste uncertainty** | 7 days of variety — they find at least one SKU they love. |
| **Habit inertia** | 7 consecutive mornings rewires the routine. By Day 5, the juice IS the morning. |
| **Trust deficit** | Daily delivery proves reliability. They see you show up, every single day. |
| **Education gap** | Booklet + ingredient cards + WhatsApp tips teach them WHY this matters. |

**The psychology is deliberate:**

1. **Habit formation requires repetition.** Research consistently shows that daily behaviours need 7-21 days to become automatic. One sample at a kiosk creates interest. Seven mornings create a habit.
2. **Reciprocity principle.** When someone gives you something valuable for free — not a cheap sample, but a premium gift box followed by a week of daily service — you feel an obligation to reciprocate. That obligation converts to a paid subscription.
3. **Loss aversion.** After 7 days, stopping feels like losing something. The Day 8 "your morning routine misses you" message exploits this directly.
4. **Endowment effect.** Once the juice is part of their morning, they value it more than before they had it. Asking them to pay Rs 70/day to keep something they already "own" is easier than asking them to start paying Rs 70/day for something new.

### When to STOP the Free Week Offer

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Reached 500 paying customers | Phase out completely — switch to 3-day paid trial (Rs 249) |
| Conversion rate drops below 30% for two consecutive months | Reduce free week availability — require referral code to access |
| Referral program generating 20+ new customers/month organically | Reduce free weeks to 10/month (referral-only access) |
| Monthly free week budget exceeds Rs 40,000 with conversion below 35% | Hard stop. Re-evaluate unit economics. |
| Cash flow pressure (reserve drops below Rs 50,000) | Pause free weeks until reserve is rebuilt |

**Transition path:** Free week (0-500 customers) --> 3-day free trial (500-1,000) --> paid trial at Rs 249 (1,000+) --> referral discount only (2,000+).

### Freeloader Risk Assessment

Not everyone who takes a free week will convert. That is expected. The question is whether the conversion rate justifies the cost.

**Expected distribution of free week recipients:**

| Segment | % of Recipients | Behaviour | Value to Business |
|---------|----------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Converts** | 40-50% | Subscribe by Day 7-10 | Direct revenue. LTV Rs 2,500-7,700. |
| **Delayed converts** | 10-15% | Don't convert immediately but subscribe within 30 days | Revenue after nurture. Same LTV. |
| **Word-of-mouth spreaders** | 15-20% | Don't subscribe but tell 2-3 friends about the experience | Indirect acquisition. Worth Rs 200-400 in referral value. |
| **Freeloaders** | 20-30% | Take the free week, never engage, never convert | Pure cost. Rs 730-1,055 lost per person. |

**Freeloader mitigation is covered in Section 3 (Anti-Freeloader Measures).** The key insight: even at 30% freeloader rate, the blended CAC is attractive because converts have high LTV and the word-of-mouth segment generates free acquisition.

| Slot | SKU | Why This One |

### How It Works

```

    |
    v

    v

    |
    v
DAY 4: Single 250ml bottle + ingredient card
    |
    v
DAY 5: Single 250ml bottle + plan preview message
    |
    v
DAY 6: Single 250ml bottle + ingredient card
    |
    v

    |
    v
DAY 8: No delivery. Win-back message if not converted.
    |
    v

```

**Sign-up requirements:**
- Full name
- Delivery address (must be within Kukatpally/Miyapur/Chandanagar service area)
- Phone number (WhatsApp-enabled)
- NO payment information required upfront

**Day 2-7 delivery details:**
- One 250ml bottle per day, rotating through the SKU range
- Each bottle accompanied by a small ingredient card (A6, double-sided) explaining what is in that day's juice and why it matters
- Delivery in a simple branded paper sleeve (not the full starter kit box)

**Suggested daily rotation:**

| Day | SKU | Why |
|-----|-----|-----|
| Day 1 | All four in Starter Kit | Variety, excitement, unboxing |
| Day 2 | Green Morning | Start with the daily staple — the one they will drink most often |
| Day 3 | Beetroot Recharge | Switch to energy positioning — "How did you feel yesterday?" |
| Day 4 | ABC Classic | The crowd pleaser — sweet, easy, reinforces "I could drink this every day" |
| Day 5 | Citrus Immunity | Functional day — "Your immune system after 5 days of cold-pressed nutrition" |
| Day 6 | Hydration Cooler | New SKU not in the kit — surprise, delight, "there's even more to try" |
| Day 7 | Their Day 1 favourite (ask on Day 5) | Personal touch — shows you listened. Or Green Morning if no preference given. |

### Conversion Economics

| Metric | Conservative | Moderate | Optimistic |
|--------|-------------|----------|------------|
| **Conversion rate (Day 7)** | 35% | 45% | 55% |

**LTV calculation:** Average individual subscriber contributes Rs 499/month net (from unit economics doc). Over 6 months average lifetime: Rs 2,994. Family subscribers contribute Rs 924/month with 7-month average lifetime (Rs 6,468 LTV), which significantly improves the blended ratio.

**Comparison to other acquisition channels:**

| Channel | CAC | Conversion Quality | LTV:CAC |
|---------|-----|-------------------|---------|
| **Free week** | Rs 1,547 (at 45% conversion) | Very high — 7-day habit formed | 1.9:1 |
| **Pop-up kiosk sampling** | Rs 400-900 | Medium — single taste, no habit | 1.5:1 estimated |
| **Booklet distribution** | Rs 250 | Low — no product experience | 0.8:1 estimated |
| **Referral (existing program)** | Rs 0 upfront (10% ongoing) | High — trusted recommendation | 4.5:1 |

The free week customer is the second-highest quality after referrals. They have experienced the product daily, built a routine, received education, and felt the loss when it stops. This is not a casual trial customer — this is someone whose morning has been changed.

### Anti-Freeloader Measures

**Prevention (before sign-up):**

| Measure | Implementation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Address verification** | Must be a real residential address within Kukatpally/Miyapur/Chandanagar. Verify via Google Maps pin drop at sign-up. |
| **One per household** | Address-based deduplication. Same address = same household = one free week, ever. Track in master sheet. |
| **Phone verification** | Send OTP to WhatsApp number at sign-up. Must be a real, active number. |
| **Monthly cap** | Hard limit on starter kits per month (20 in Month 1, scaling to 40 by Month 3). First come, first served. Creates scarcity. |
| **Service area restriction** | Only addresses within delivery radius. No exceptions. This naturally limits freeloaders to your actual market. |

**Detection (during free week):**

| Day | Check | Action if Failed |
|-----|-------|-----------------|
| **Day 2** | Was Day 1 Starter Kit received? (Delivery confirmation) | If not received, investigate. Do not send Day 2. |
| **Day 3** | Did customer respond to check-in message? | If no response: send a follow-up. Still proceed with Day 4 delivery. |
| **Day 4** | Any WhatsApp engagement in 4 days? (Read receipts, replies, emoji reactions) | If zero engagement across all 4 days: flag as potential freeloader. |
| **Day 5** | Response to plan interest question? | If no response AND no Day 3 response: consider stopping Day 6-7 deliveries. Send "Are you still enjoying your Soma mornings?" message first. |
| **Day 6** | Cumulative engagement score | If customer has responded at least once: continue. If truly zero engagement: stop delivery, send "We noticed you haven't been in touch" message. |

**Engagement scoring (simple):**

| Signal | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Responded to any WhatsApp message | +3 |
| Asked a question about ingredients/health | +5 |
| Shared a photo of their morning routine | +5 |
| Mentioned to a friend (we learn via referral sign-up) | +10 |
| Responded to plan preview with interest | +8 |
| Read receipts on all messages but no reply | +1 |
| Zero engagement across all signals | 0 — freeloader flag |

**Customers scoring 0 after Day 5 get deliveries paused.** This saves Rs 100-150 in COGS + delivery for the remaining 2 days. At 20% freeloader rate across 30 kits/month, that saves Rs 600-900/month — modest but principled.

**Post free week:**

- Track freeloader addresses permanently. No second free week, ever.
- If a freeloader's household member signs up later: allow it (different person, fresh chance) but flag for monitoring.

```

    v

    |
    v
Person likes it --> receives Demo Pack to take home
    |
    v
Demo Pack card says: "Loved it? Get a full week FREE."
    |
    v
QR code --> WhatsApp signup --> address collection
    |
    v
Starter Kit delivery next morning
```

### Contents

| Item | Specification | Purpose |

Combined with free week cost: total CAC is Rs 323 + Rs 882 = Rs 1,205 per attempt, or Rs 2,678 per paying customer (at 45% free week conversion). Still within acceptable range given LTV.

**The free week program does not pay for itself in isolation during the first 6 months.** This is expected and acceptable because:

1. The Rs 2L total budget covers 10 months of operations, not just acquisition.
2. Subscribers generate revenue that covers their own COGS + delivery (contribution positive from Month 1).

**Cumulative acquisition budget through 500 customers:**

At 45% conversion, reaching 500 customers requires ~1,111 free weeks. At Rs 882 each: Rs 9.8L total. This exceeds the Rs 2L budget significantly, which is why:
- Free weeks start at 20-40/month, not 100/month
- Referral program must carry increasing share of acquisition after Month 3
- By Month 6+, target: 40% of new customers from free weeks, 40% from referrals, 20% from organic/kiosk
- Blended CAC drops as referral share increases

## 6. When to Phase Out Free Week

### Phase-Out Triggers

| Trigger | Threshold | Action |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| **Customer count** | 500 paying customers | Stop all free weeks. Switch to 3-day paid trial (Rs 249). |
| **Conversion decline** | Below 30% for 2 consecutive months | Restrict free weeks to referral-only (existing customer must refer). Reduces volume, improves quality. |
| **Referral dominance** | Referrals generate 20+ new customers/month | Reduce free weeks to 10/month. Referral is cheaper and higher quality. |
| **Cash pressure** | Cash reserve below Rs 50,000 | Immediate pause on free weeks. Resume when reserve exceeds Rs 75,000. |
| **Freeloader spike** | Freeloader rate exceeds 40% for any given month | Tighten eligibility (require referral code), add phone call verification before kit dispatch. |

### Transition Ladder

```
PHASE 1 (0-200 customers): Full free week for anyone in service area
    |
    v
PHASE 2 (200-350 customers): Free week requires referral code OR pop-up signup
    |
    v
PHASE 3 (350-500 customers): Free week for referrals only. Pop-ups get 3-day free trial.
    |
    v
PHASE 4 (500+ customers): No free week. 3-day paid trial at Rs 249. Referral gives 10% off first month.
    |
    v
PHASE 5 (1,000+ customers): Paid from Day 1. Referral discount only acquisition incentive.
```

### Post-Free-Week Acquisition Stack

Once free weeks are phased out, the acquisition stack becomes:

| Channel | Mechanism | CAC |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| **Referral program** | Existing customers share codes. 10% ongoing credit. | Rs 0 upfront, ~Rs 230/month ongoing |

| **Community ambassadors** | Top referrers distribute booklets in their networks | Rs 0 (covered by referral credits) |

## 8. Logistics & Operations

### Production Capacity for Starter Kits

**Current setup (home kitchen, 1 cold-press juicer):**

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Bottles per hour (300ml) | 12-15 |
| Bottles per hour (250ml, starter kit) | 15-18 |
| Daily production window | 2 hours (4:00 AM - 6:00 AM) |
| Daily capacity (250ml + 300ml combined) | 25-35 bottles |
| Starter kits per day (4 bottles each) | 3-4 kits (after regular subscriber production) |
| **Starter kits per week** | **15-20** |
| **Starter kits per month** | **60-80** (limited by production time, not demand) |

**Constraint:** Starter kit production competes with regular subscriber production. Subscriber bottles always take priority. Starter kits use remaining capacity.

**Solution for scaling beyond 20 kits/month:** Dedicate one production day per week to starter kits only (e.g., Sunday — no regular deliveries). This yields 15-18 kits in a single session.

### Assembly Workflow

**Kit assembly (batch operation — do once per delivery batch):**

| Step | Time | Who | When |
|------|------|-----|------|
| 1. Print welcome letters (batch) | 15 min for 20 | Founder | Weekend, once per batch |
| 2. Prepare habit tracker cards (batch) | 10 min for 20 | Founder | Weekend |
| 3. Label referral cards (if pre-printing codes) | 20 min for 20 | Founder | Weekend |
| 4. Assemble box interiors: insert dividers, place booklet + cards + pulp sample in accessory compartment | 3 min per kit | Founder/helper | Evening before delivery day |
| 5. Morning of delivery: press juice, bottle, label | 4 bottles x 4 min = 16 min per kit | Founder | 4:00-5:30 AM |
| 6. Place bottles in box, add tissue, seal with sticker | 2 min per kit | Founder/helper | 5:30-6:00 AM |
| 7. Load for delivery | 5 min total | Rider | 6:00 AM |

**Total per-kit assembly time: ~25 minutes** (including juice production). For 4 kits: ~90 minutes dedicated production + assembly.

### Delivery Schedule

**Recommendation: Starter Kits on Mon/Wed/Fri only.**

| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| **Monday** | Starter Kit deliveries (new sign-ups from Fri-Sun) + regular subscriber deliveries |
| **Tuesday** | Regular subscriber deliveries only. Free week Day 2 bottles for Monday kit recipients. |
| **Wednesday** | Starter Kit deliveries (new sign-ups from Mon-Tue) + regular deliveries + free week daily bottles |
| **Thursday** | Regular + free week daily bottles |
| **Friday** | Starter Kit deliveries (new sign-ups from Wed-Thu) + regular + free week daily bottles |
| **Saturday** | Regular + free week daily bottles. No new starter kits. |
| **Sunday** | No deliveries. Kit assembly and production prep for Monday. |

**Why Mon/Wed/Fri for kits:** Batching kit deliveries to 3 days/week means the rider handles 2-4 larger packages on those days instead of 1 kit randomly every day. This concentrates the logistics load and allows more efficient route planning. The customer signs up and waits a maximum of 2 days for their kit — acceptable when they are told "Your Starter Kit arrives on [next Mon/Wed/Fri]."

| **Freeloader rate** | < 25% | Zero-engagement recipients / total free weeks |
| **Engagement score average** | > 8 points | Average across all free week recipients |
| **Day 5 plan interest response rate** | > 60% | Responded to Day 5 message / total active free weeks |
| **Win-back conversion (Day 8-30)** | 10-15% of non-Day-7-converts | Late converts / (total free weeks - Day 7 converts) |
| **COMEBACK code redemption rate** | 5-10% | COMEBACK codes used / COMEBACK codes sent |

### Monthly Review Questions

At the end of each month, answer these:

1. **Is the conversion rate holding?** If below 35%, investigate: are we attracting the wrong people? Is the product not landing? Is the messaging off?
2. **Which SKU is the favourite?** Track Day 5 responses. If one SKU dominates, consider making it the Day 2 and Day 7 default.
3. **What day do people drop off?** If engagement drops after Day 3, the check-in message may be too pushy or too weak. If Day 5-6 drops, the plan preview timing may be wrong.
4. **Are freeloaders concentrated from one source?** If a specific pop-up location generates high freeloader rates, stop doing pop-ups there. Location quality matters.
5. **Is the referral multiplier growing?** Track: of free week converts, how many have referred at least one person within 90 days? Target: 30%+ of converts become referrers.
