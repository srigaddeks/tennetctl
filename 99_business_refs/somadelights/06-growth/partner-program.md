# Soma Partner Program

> The money other brands spend on Instagram ads? We give it directly to you.

## 1. The Partner Philosophy

### Every Customer Is a Partner

At Soma Delights, we do not have "customers" and "referrers." We have **partners**.
Every person who subscribes to Soma is invited to become a partner in building
something meaningful — a wellness movement that grows through trust, not through
advertising algorithms.

This is not a semantic game. The distinction between "partner" and "referrer" is
foundational to how we think about growth:

| Referral Program (industry standard) | Soma Partner Program |
|---------------------------------------|----------------------|
| "Share a code, get a discount" | "Invite someone into your wellness community" |
| Transactional — one-time reward | Relational — ongoing earnings for a year+ |
| Customer is a marketing channel | Customer is a co-builder |
| Brand controls the narrative | Partners carry the narrative |
| Money flows to platforms | Money flows to people |
| Loyalty is bought | Loyalty is earned and shared |

### The Math That Changes Everything

Most consumer brands allocate 15-20% of revenue to advertising. That money flows
to Meta, Google, YouTube, and influencers who may never use the product.

We take that same 15-20% and give it directly to the people who already love Soma.

The logic is simple:

- A stranger on Instagram does not know your morning routine.
- Your colleague who hands you a booklet and says "this changed my mornings" does.
- Trust converts. Ads interrupt.
- Partners have skin in the game. Ad platforms have your credit card.

When a partner invites someone into Soma, they are not "referring" — they are
**sharing something they believe in** and earning from that belief. Their wallet
grows as the brand grows. Their success and Soma's success are the same thing.

### Why "Partner" and Never "Referral"

The word "referral" reduces a relationship to a transaction. It implies:
"I mentioned you to get something."

The word "partner" implies: "I am building this with you."

In every customer-facing context — WhatsApp messages, booklets, the website, kiosk
signage, wallet screens — we use **Partner**, **Soma Partner**, and **Partner
Program**. The word "referral" does not exist in the customer's vocabulary.

Internally, the mechanics may be described using referral terminology for clarity
in engineering and operations documents. But the customer never sees it.

Every Soma subscriber starts as a **Soma Friend**. As you invite more people
into the community and they become active subscribers, you climb through the
partner tiers.

| **Soma Friend** | 1-2 | 10% | 12 months | Store credit | Welcome to the family |

Every partner starts here. You receive your unique partner code at sign-up.

- **Duration:** 12 months from each invitee's first paid order

- Invite 1 friend who spends Rs 2,299/month
- Monthly earning: Rs 230
- After 3 months: Rs 690 in wallet (covers roughly 3 free bottles per month)

The sweet spot. Advocates are genuinely enthusiastic and naturally share Soma

- **Earn rate:** 10%

  - 5 extra starter kits per quarter to gift to friends

- **Communication:** Monthly wallet update + quarterly thank-you note

**Example earnings:**
- 4 active invitees, each spending Rs 2,299/month

**Example earnings:**
- 25 active invitees, each spending Rs 2,299/month

- Over 24 months per invitee: extraordinary lifetime value

### Tier Progression Rules

- Tiers are based on **active** invitees (currently subscribing or subscribed
  for at least 1 paid month)
- Tier upgrades happen automatically when a new invitee completes activation
- Tier downgrades: if active invitee count drops below tier threshold for 3
  consecutive months, tier adjusts down (but earned credits are never clawed back)
- Earn rate upgrades apply to ALL active invitees, not just new ones (reward
  loyalty generously)

## 4. The Economics: Ads vs Partners

### What a Typical Brand Spends (Per 100 Customers Acquired)

| Channel | Cost per Customer | Total for 100 | Where the Money Goes |
|---------|------------------|---------------|---------------------|
| Instagram Ads | Rs 500 - 1,500 | Rs 50,000 - 1,50,000 | Meta / Facebook |
| Google Ads | Rs 300 - 1,000 | Rs 30,000 - 1,00,000 | Google |
| YouTube Ads | Rs 200 - 800 | Rs 20,000 - 80,000 | Google |
| Influencer Marketing | Rs 1,000 - 5,000 | Rs 1,00,000 - 5,00,000 | Influencers (strangers) |
| **TOTAL** | | **Rs 2,00,000 - 8,30,000** | **Platforms and strangers** |

These numbers are well-documented industry averages for D2C brands in India.
The money leaves your ecosystem entirely. It buys impressions, not relationships.
A customer acquired through Instagram ads has no loyalty to your brand — they
were interrupted while scrolling, and they will forget you by tomorrow.

### What Soma Spends (Per 100 Customers Acquired via Partners)

| Channel | Cost per Customer | Total for 100 | Where the Money Goes |
|---------|------------------|---------------|---------------------|
| Partner Program (10-15% earnings) | Rs 800 - 1,200 | Rs 80,000 - 1,20,000 | YOUR CUSTOMERS |
| Starter Kits (free week) | Rs 400 - 500 | Rs 40,000 - 50,000 | Product (customer gets real value) |
| **TOTAL** | | **Rs 1,20,000 - 1,70,000** | **Your community** |

### The Result

**40-80% cheaper than traditional advertising.** And fundamentally better in
every dimension:

| Dimension | Ad-Acquired Customer | Partner-Acquired Customer |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------------------|
| **Trust at sign-up** | Zero — they saw an ad | High — a friend invited them |
| **First-month retention** | 30-50% | 70-85% (industry data on trust-based acquisition) |
| **Lifetime value** | Lower — no emotional connection | Higher — part of a community |
| **Acquisition cost trend** | Increases every year (ad inflation) | Stays flat or decreases (network effects) |
| **Where money goes** | Silicon Valley | Your neighbourhood |
| **Brand building** | You rent attention | You earn advocacy |

### The Flywheel

```
Partner invites friend
       |
       v
Friend starts Soma routine -----> Friend becomes a partner
       |                                    |
       v                                    v
Partner earns from friend          Friend invites THEIR friends
       |                                    |

       v                                    v
```

Every partner-acquired customer becomes a potential partner themselves. The

## 5. Partner Wallet Design

### Naming

- Customer-facing name: **Soma Wallet** (or **Partner Wallet** in tier contexts)
- The wallet is a core part of the partner experience, not an afterthought

### Balance Visibility

**Phase 1 (0-50 partners):** WhatsApp manual response
- Partner sends "WALLET" to Soma WhatsApp number
- Founder replies with balance within 2 hours

**Phase 2 (50-150 partners):** WhatsApp semi-automated
- Partner sends "WALLET" — auto-reply from Google Sheet lookup via WhatsApp
  Business API or Twilio integration

**Phase 3 (150+ partners):** Simple web page
- somadelights.in/wallet?code=SOMA-PRIYA
- Shows balance, transaction history, tier status, invitee activity

### Credit Mechanics

- Credits accumulate monthly, processed on the **1st of each month**
- Credits cover the previous calendar month's invitee spend
- **Pending credits** are visible before release:
  "Rs 150 earning — releases April 1"
- Credits are calculated per invitee, then summed to the partner's wallet

### Credit Lifecycle

```
Invitee makes purchases throughout March
              |
              v
March 31: Month closes, earnings calculated
              |
              v
March 31 - April 1: Credits appear as "Pending"
              |
              v
April 1: Credits release to "Available" balance
              |
              v
Partner notified via WhatsApp with breakdown
```

### Usage Options

1. **Apply to Soma orders:** Mention wallet credit at next delivery or via
   WhatsApp. Credit deducted from wallet, applied to order.
2. **Cash withdrawal:** Request via WhatsApp ("WITHDRAW 500"). UPI transfer
   within 5 business days. Minimum withdrawal depends on tier.
3. **Accumulate:** No action needed. Credits never expire while account is active.

### Wallet Rules

- **Credits never expire** while the partner's account is active (even if they
  pause their own subscription)
- If partner pauses subscription: wallet remains active, credits still accumulate
  from their invitees' purchases
- If partner cancels entirely: wallet frozen for 6 months, then credits expire
  (with 30-day advance notice)
- Wallet balance is not transferable to another person
- All wallet transactions are visible in monthly WhatsApp updates

## 7. Gating and Anti-Abuse (Internal)

> This section is internal. It describes the mechanics that protect the Partner
> Program from gaming and abuse.

### Activation Gating

An invitee must meet the following criteria before the partner starts earning:

| Gate | Requirement | Rationale |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| **Paid delivery gate** | Invitee completes 1 full month of paid deliveries (minimum 20 deliveries for daily; 8 for alternate-day) | Ensures real, committed customers — not sign-up gaming |
| **Household gate** | Maximum 1 credit per household address | Prevents family members signing up under different names at the same address |
| **Self-invitation detection** | Same address or same payment method as partner triggers manual review | Prevents partners from inviting themselves |
| **Cooling period** | First month's credits are held for 7 extra days | Allows time to detect early churn or fraud |

### Monthly Earning Caps

| Tier | Monthly Cap |
|------|------------|
| Soma Friend | Rs 3,000 |
| Soma Advocate | Rs 3,000 |
| Soma Champion | Rs 5,000 |
| Soma Builder | Rs 10,000 |
| Soma Founding Partner | Rs 10,000 |

Caps exist to prevent scaled gaming and keep the program financially sustainable.
At normal earn rates, hitting the cap requires a large active community — which
is legitimate. The cap protects against edge cases and manipulation.

### Self-Invitation Detection

| Signal | Detection Method | Action |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| Same delivery address as partner | Address matching in order records | Auto-flag, manual review |
| Same phone number pattern | Phone number similarity check | Auto-flag, manual review |
| Same UPI account for withdrawal | UPI ID matching | Block withdrawal, manual review |
| Partner and invitee sign up within 1 hour | Timestamp matching | Auto-flag |
| Same device fingerprint (Phase 3) | Cookie/fingerprint check | Auto-flag |

### Volume Flags

| Signal | Threshold | Action |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| More than 3 invitations activated in 24 hours | 3 | Auto-pause credits, founder review |
| More than 10 invitations in 7 days | 10 | Account flagged for investigation |
| Invitation from a non-subscriber | N/A | Partner code disabled — must be active subscriber |
| Invitee churns within first 2 weeks of paid period | N/A | No credits earned; partner notified |

### Fraud Review Process

For the first 6 months of the program (feasible at small scale), all fraud
detection is manual:

1. **Flag identified:** Founder reviews within 24 hours
2. **Investigation:** Check delivery addresses, payment methods, order patterns,
   timing correlations
3. **False positive:** Unflag, apologize, offer Rs 50 goodwill credit
4. **Confirmed fraud:** Revoke all pending credits, disable partner code, send
   a neutral message (no accusation — just "unusual activity detected")
5. **Repeat offender:** Permanent ban from Partner Program (can still subscribe)

### Right to Revoke

Soma reserves the right to revoke partner credits, downgrade tiers, or remove
a partner from the program for:

- Confirmed fraud or self-invitation
- Misleading claims about Soma products
- Harassment or pressure tactics in inviting others
- Any behaviour that damages the Soma brand

## 10. How Partners Invite (Not "Refer")

Partners have multiple natural ways to invite people into Soma. None of them
should feel like "selling" or "marketing." They should feel like sharing
something good.

### Invitation Methods

#### 1. Personal WhatsApp Message

The most common and most effective method. Partner sends a personal message
to a friend with their partner code.

**Pre-written message (customizable):**

> Hey! I have been starting my mornings with Soma Delights — fresh cold-pressed
> juice delivered to my door at 7 AM. No preservatives, made daily in Hyderabad.
>
> They have a free starter week if you want to try it. Use my code **SOMA-[CODE]**
> when you sign up.
>
> Trust me, your mornings will thank you.

#### 2. Hand Someone a Booklet

Partners at Advocate tier and above receive 5 extra premium booklets per quarter.
These are the same beautiful, educational booklets that every new subscriber gets.
The partner writes their code inside and hands it to a colleague, neighbour, or
gym buddy.

Physical booklets convert at 2-3x the rate of digital messages because they carry
weight, intention, and personal touch.

#### 3. Share Digital Booklet PDF via WhatsApp

For partners who prefer digital: a PDF version of the booklet that can be
forwarded via WhatsApp. The partner's code is embedded in the PDF or added
as a message alongside it.

#### 4. Bring a Friend to a Kiosk Pop-Up

When Soma runs kiosk events (apartment complexes, offices, weekend markets),
partners can bring friends directly. The friend tastes the product, meets the
team, and signs up on the spot with the partner's code.

#### 5. Gift a Starter Kit

Partners can purchase a starter kit as a gift. They pay for the kit, the
invitee receives a free week of deliveries. The partner's code is automatically
attached. This is particularly effective for:

- Birthday gifts
- Housewarming presents
- "Get well soon" gestures
- Corporate wellness gifts

#### 6. Word of Mouth

The best method. No code needed upfront — the partner simply talks about their
Soma routine in conversation. When someone expresses interest, the partner
shares their code.

This is why the product experience must be exceptional. Word of mouth cannot
be manufactured. It can only be earned.

## 11. Partner Program as Brand Story

The Partner Program is not a feature of Soma Delights. It IS the growth strategy.
It is how Soma scales without a marketing budget. Every piece of brand
communication should reflect this.

### Core Messaging

These lines should appear across all brand touchpoints — website, booklet,
WhatsApp, kiosk signage, packaging:

**Primary:**
> "We do not advertise. We partner."

**Supporting:**
> "The money other brands give to Instagram, we give to you."

> "Every partner is a co-builder of the Soma wellness movement."

> "Soma grows through trust, not through ads."

> "Your mornings. Your community. Your earnings."

### Where This Messaging Lives

| Touchpoint | How the Partner Story Appears |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| **Website homepage** | Hero section or prominent banner: "We do not advertise. We partner." |
| **Premium booklet** | Dedicated full page on the Partner Program |
| **Starter kit** | Partner card with code and one-line explanation |
| **WhatsApp welcome** | Partner Program introduced on Day 7 of free week |
| **Monthly wallet update** | Reinforces partnership identity every month |
| **Kiosk signage** | "Ask any Soma Partner about their experience" |
| **Social media bio** | "Zero ad spend. 100% partner-powered." |
| **Packaging/bottles** | Small text: "Partner-powered wellness" (subtle) |

### Why This Matters for Brand Positioning

In a market saturated with D2C brands spending crores on performance marketing,
Soma's positioning as a partner-powered brand is a genuine differentiator:

1. **It is honest.** There is no manipulation, no dark patterns, no algorithmic
   targeting. Just people sharing something they love.
2. **It is equitable.** The money stays in the community instead of flowing to
   tech platforms.
3. **It is sustainable.** Partner acquisition costs are predictable and do not
   inflate like ad costs.
4. **It is a story people want to tell.** "My juice brand pays me instead of
   paying Instagram" is inherently shareable.
5. **It builds a moat.** A network of invested partners is nearly impossible
   for competitors to replicate.

## 12. Metrics

### Primary Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target (Year 1) |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Partner activation rate** | % of subscribers who have invited at least 1 person | 30-40% |
| **Invitations per active partner per month** | Average number of new invitations sent | 0.5-1.0 |
| **Invitation-to-signup conversion rate** | % of invited people who become paid subscribers | 25-35% |
| **Partner-acquired customer retention (Month 3)** | % of partner-acquired customers still active at 3 months | 75-85% |
| **Organic customer retention (Month 3)** | % of non-partner-acquired customers still active at 3 months | 55-65% |
| **Cost per partner-acquired customer** | Total partner payouts / number of customers acquired | Rs 800-1,200 |
| **Industry CAC benchmark** | Average D2C customer acquisition cost in India | Rs 1,500-3,000 |

### Financial Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target (Year 1) |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Total wallet credits outstanding** | Sum of all unredeemed partner wallet balances | Track monthly |
| **Monthly wallet cashouts** | Total cash withdrawn by partners in a month | Track monthly |
| **Partner payout as % of revenue** | Total partner earnings / total revenue | 6-10% |
| **Gross margin after partner payouts** | Gross margin minus partner earnings as % of revenue | 45-50% |

### Community Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target (Year 1) |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Tier distribution** | % of partners at each tier | 60% Friend, 25% Advocate, 10% Champion, 4% Builder, 1% Founding |
| **Average partner lifetime earnings** | Total earnings / number of partners (lifetime) | Rs 2,000-5,000 |
| **Partner satisfaction score** | Quarterly survey (1-10 scale) | 8+ |
| **Booklet distribution rate** | Booklets given to Advocates+ vs booklets actually shared | 70%+ shared |

### Tracking Cadence

| Frequency | What to Review |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Weekly** | New invitations, new activations, fraud flags |
| **Monthly** | Wallet reconciliation, tier movements, earnings distribution, retention comparison |
| **Quarterly** | Partner satisfaction survey, tier distribution analysis, CAC comparison vs industry, program economics review |
| **Annually** | Full program audit, tier structure review, earn rate sustainability check, Founding Partner recognition |

### Partner-Acquired vs Organic: The Key Comparison

The single most important metric to track is the **retention and LTV difference
between partner-acquired customers and organic customers**. If partner-acquired
customers retain better and spend more (which trust-based acquisition research
strongly suggests), the Partner Program pays for itself many times over.

Track this from day one, even if manually in a spreadsheet. This data becomes
the proof point for doubling down on the Partner Program as the primary growth
engine.

## Appendix: Program Launch Checklist

### Before Launch

- [ ] Partner codes generated for all existing subscribers
- [ ] WhatsApp templates written and tested
- [ ] Google Sheet set up for wallet tracking
- [ ] Booklet updated with Partner Program page
- [ ] Partner card designed for starter kits
- [ ] Founder trained on manual wallet reconciliation process
- [ ] Anti-abuse rules documented and understood

- [ ] Tier thresholds finalized

- [ ] Send Partner Program welcome message to all existing subscribers
- [ ] Distribute partner codes via WhatsApp
- [ ] Begin tracking invitations in spreadsheet
- [ ] Monitor for early fraud signals
- [ ] Collect qualitative feedback from first partners

- [ ] How many partners have invited at least 1 person?
- [ ] What is the invitation-to-signup conversion rate?
- [ ] Are the WhatsApp templates working (engagement, clarity)?
- [ ] Any fraud flags triggered?
