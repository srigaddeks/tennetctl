# B2B / Office Wellness Playbook

> Offices will come knocking. Be ready — but not too early.

---

## 1. Why B2B Matters

B2B is not your launch strategy. It is your density strategy.

**The math is simple:**
- One office = 10-50 subscriptions from a single client
- Same delivery route, multiple bottles at one stop
- HR/admin handles internal distribution — you don't touch last-mile inside the building
- Lower delivery cost per bottle (one stop vs ten stops)
- Predictable daily volume = easier production planning
- Corporate gifting opens seasonal revenue (Diwali, New Year, Women's Day)

**But here's the trap:**
- B2B clients expect reliability you haven't proven yet at month 1
- One bad delivery to 30 people = 30 angry people, not 1
- Corporate clients negotiate harder — margins compress
- If B2B becomes 50%+ of revenue, one client leaving is catastrophic
- You lose the personal connection that makes Soma special

**The rule:** B2B is secondary. Only after B2C operations are stable and you have 50+ individual subscribers proving the model works.

## 2. Target Segments

### Primary targets

**IT Offices (50-500 employees)**
Ideal first B2B customers. HR and Admin managers are the decision-makers. They have budget lines for employee benefits and are familiar with vendor relationships. Target companies in HITEC City, Gachibowli, Madhapur, Kondapur.

**Co-working Spaces**
WeWork, 91springboard, T-Hub ecosystem, local independent co-working operators in Kondapur and Banjara Hills. Sell to the space operator, not individual members. The operator becomes a Soma Partner and earns credit on volume.

**Gyms and Yoga Studios**
High-intent wellness audience already spending money on health. They want branded products in their fridges that align with their positioning. Minimum 20 bottles/week. They earn 10% margin on what they sell. They serve as both distribution point and endorsement. Target fitness studios in HITEC City, Jubilee Hills, Banjara Hills, Kondapur.

**Clinics and Diagnostic Centers**
Physiotherapy clinics, naturopathy centers, women's health clinics, preventive health check-up centers. Patients waiting for appointments are a captive audience. Practitioners who endorse Soma add credibility. Focus on integrative health and women's health clinics in Gachibowli, Kondapur, Kukatpally.

**Apartment Common Areas**
Premium apartment complexes in Kondapur, Manikonda, Gachibowli, Narsingi. Society management buys a fixed daily quantity for the clubhouse or lobby. Works well for apartments with active community management.

### Segments to avoid

**Restaurants and Cafes**
Wrong positioning. Cafes negotiate hard on price, ask for exclusivity, want delivery at inconvenient times, and place Soma next to sugary drinks. Cafe customers are not wellness-first buyers. Price pressure will erode margin and dilute brand.

**Retail (Supermarkets, Kirana stores)**
Retail demands 30-40% margins, return policies, and extended payment terms. For a home kitchen producing short shelf-life product, retail is structurally incompatible. Not viable until Soma operates at a fundamentally different scale.

**Hospitals**
Complex procurement processes, long sales cycles, regulatory hurdles around food products in medical settings. Not worth it at this stage.

## 3. The B2B Pitch

**"Soma for Teams — your office wellness subscription."**

Fresh cold-pressed juices delivered to your pantry every morning. No preservatives. No vending machines. No artificial sweeteners. Real ingredients. Real food.

For less than what most companies spend on office snacks, your team gets a daily wellness ritual that's visible, tangible, and appreciated.

Frame it as an employee benefit, not a food order. HR managers think in terms of benefits. Admin managers think in terms of vendors. Go through HR first.

**Key proof points:**
- Cost per employee per day: 30 bottles across 50 employees = Rs 45/person/day at the large office rate. Less than a chai from a vending machine.
- Wellness benefits consistently rank among the top 5 non-salary retention factors in IT sector surveys.
- Visible, daily benefit — employees notice it every morning. Unlike gym reimbursements nobody uses.

## 4. Pricing Model

Pricing rewards volume without destroying margins. The B2B discount comes from delivery efficiency savings — one stop vs ten — not from cutting product quality or core margin.

| Segment | Per-Bottle Price | Minimum Order | Commitment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Individual bulk (same address, 20+ bottles) | Rs 79 | 20 bottles/day | None |
| Small office (20-50 employees) | Rs 75 | 15 bottles/day | 3-month contract |
| Large office (50+ employees) | Rs 70 | 30 bottles/day | 6-month contract |
| Co-working operator | Rs 72 | 20 bottles/day | 3-month contract |
| Gym / clinic distribution | Rs 65 (they sell at Rs 80+) | 20 bottles/week | Monthly rolling |

**Minimum order: 15 bottles/day.** Below this threshold, a separate delivery stop is not economically justified. Smaller offices can aggregate orders individually through the standard subscriber channel.

**Custom blends** are available for large offices (50+ employees) on 6-month contracts. Minimum 3 months lead time for recipe development and testing. Premium of Rs 5/bottle over standard pricing.

**Large format (400ml)** — to be evaluated once the B2B channel is established. Offices may prefer larger bottles for desk use vs. standard 250ml. Do not develop until there is demonstrated demand from at least 3 office clients.

## 5. Office Wellness Day Program

A low-cost lead generation and conversion mechanism.

**The offer:** Soma comes to your office for a free Office Wellness Day. Sample juices for the entire team (or 20-30 people). A 15-minute talk on micro-wellness habits and how cold-pressed nutrition fits a busy workday. Zero cost to the office.

**Cost to Soma:**
- 25-30 sample bottles × Rs 40 COGS = Rs 1,000-1,200
- 1 hour of founder time
- Printed handouts or WhatsApp invite to community
- **Total: Rs 1,000-1,500**

**Expected conversion outcomes:**
- Office wellness subscription: Rs 50,000-2,00,000/year
- Individual subscribers from attendees: 3-8 employees who subscribe personally = Rs 3,000-8,000/month recurring

Run 2-4 Wellness Days per month once the B2B channel is active. Prioritize offices where at least one individual subscriber already exists — warm entry, trusted context.

**The warm intro path:**
1. Identify which offices current subscribers work at (ask during onboarding)
2. When 2+ customers are at the same office: "Would your office be interested in a free wellness session?"
3. Customer introduces to HR/admin over WhatsApp
4. Pitch the Wellness Day or starter kit
5. The existing customer earns partner credit for every colleague who subscribes

## 7. Gym / Clinic Distribution Play

Gyms and clinics are endorsement channels as much as distribution channels.

**Gym model:**
- Soma supplies 20-30 bottles per week to the gym's front desk or fridge
- Gym sells at Rs 80-90/bottle (Soma supplies at Rs 65)
- Gym earns Rs 15-25/bottle margin
- Soma gets daily visibility with an active wellness audience
- Gym promotes Soma organically because they earn on every sale

**Clinic model:**
- Bottles available at reception for waiting patients
- Doctor/practitioner endorsement (even informal) carries significant weight
- Same pricing as gym model
- Offer 2 free bottles per week to the practitioner personally — their experience is the endorsement

## 8. B2B Onboarding Process

**Step 1 — Pilot month**
Company signs a 1-month paid pilot. Minimum 15 bottles/day. No long-term commitment. Priced at Rs 79/bottle. Delivered daily, Monday-Saturday.

**Step 2 — Mid-pilot check-in (Week 2)**
WhatsApp to contact person: "How's the team responding? Any feedback so far?" Relationship touchpoint, not a survey.

**Step 3 — Pilot evaluation (End of Month 1)**
Assessment: what percentage of employees are regularly taking a bottle? If 60%+ are regular users, the pilot is a success. Present the 3-month contract at Rs 75/bottle.

**Step 4 — Contract conversion**
Simple 1-page agreement: daily quantity, price per bottle, delivery time, payment terms, renewal notice period (30 days). Clear mutual understanding, not a complex legal document.

**Step 5 — Quarterly review**
For 6-month contracts, a quarterly check-in to assess satisfaction, adjust quantities if needed, and discuss custom blend options.

## 9. Delivery Mechanics

B2B deliveries fold into the existing morning route. No separate delivery infrastructure needed.

- Drop time: pantry or reception by 8:00am, before most employees arrive
- Single contact person per office: one WhatsApp number (Admin/HR/Office Manager)
- Delivery in Soma-branded insulated bags or boxes (returnable). Bottles stay cold.
- No refrigerator required if delivered by 8am and consumed by 11am. If office has a pantry fridge, bottles go directly in.
- Delivery confirmation: contact person acknowledges receipt on WhatsApp

**Route planning:** B2B drops in HITEC City / Gachibowli add 20-40 minutes depending on traffic and number of stops. Plan route density before signing more than 3-4 B2B clients to avoid route bloat degrading the B2C delivery window.

## 10. Invoicing and Payment

**GST invoice required.** All B2B clients need a proper GST invoice. Soma must be GST-registered before first B2B invoice.

**Payment terms:**
- New clients (first 3 months): advance payment weekly or bi-weekly. No credit to unproven clients.
- Established clients (3+ months): Net-7 payment terms. Invoice every Monday for the previous week.
- Large companies (MNC/listed): they will ask for Net-30 or Net-45. Negotiate to Net-15. If they insist on Net-30, build this into cash flow planning — but do not allow it to become the default.

**Non-payment protocol:** reminder at Day 3, firm reminder at Day 7, pause delivery at Day 10. No delivery resumes until outstanding invoice is cleared. No exceptions.

## 11. B2B Operations

### Daily delivery

- Single drop point: reception desk, front desk, or office pantry. Never individual desks.
- Branded insulated box per office (cost: Rs 300-500, one-time). Keeps bottles cold until employees collect.
- Consistent delivery time daily — corporate clients plan around it.
- WhatsApp photo of drop to office contact as delivery confirmation.
- Backup plan: if delivery cannot be made (illness, equipment failure), WhatsApp the office contact by 7am. No surprises.

### Order management

- Fixed daily order. Changes require 2-day advance notice.
- Weekly menu rotation through 3-4 blends so employees don't face the same juice daily.
- Send next week's menu to office contact every Friday.
- Each bottle labeled with blend name + date + "Best before 6 PM today" (no individual names for B2B).

### Sample weekly rotation

| Day | Blend |
|---|---|
| Monday | Energy Blend (citrus, ginger, turmeric) |
| Tuesday | Immunity Blend (amla, beetroot, carrot) |
| Wednesday | Digest Blend (mint, cucumber, aloe) |
| Thursday | Seasonal Special |
| Friday | Employee's Choice (WhatsApp poll on Monday, deliver the winner) |

The Friday poll creates engagement. Office contact runs a quick poll on Monday: "What do you want Friday?" Employees feel involved.

### Contact structure per account

1. Primary contact — person who arranged the subscription
2. Backup contact — one more person who can receive deliveries
3. Finance contact — person or email for invoice-related communication

## 12. Risk Factors and Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Seasonal order drops (festivals, WFH periods) | Build 20% buffer into financial planning. Never model B2B at 100% utilization. |
| Payment delays | Net-7 terms for new clients. Pause delivery at Day 10 overdue. No exceptions. |
| Contract non-renewals | Never let any single B2B client exceed 25% of total Soma revenue. |
| Operational overload from early B2B | Respect the 50-subscriber threshold. Do not activate B2B before it is met. |
| Taste/format mismatch | Track B2B feedback separately from individual subscriber feedback. Larger bottles and milder flavors may perform better in office settings. |
| Route bloat degrading B2C delivery | Cap B2B at 4-5 office stops. Route density before route count. |

## 13. Activation Checklist

Before pursuing the first B2B client, all of these must be true:

- [ ] 50+ individual subscribers with stable daily delivery
- [ ] GST registration complete
- [ ] B2B pricing sheet finalized
- [ ] 1-page pilot agreement template ready
- [ ] Invoicing process set up (Zoho Books or similar)
- [ ] WhatsApp Business account with B2B contact labels
- [ ] At least one warm introduction to an office contact from an existing subscriber
- [ ] Delivery route has capacity for 2-3 additional stops without impacting B2C window
- [ ] Product liability insurance in place

When all boxes are checked, activate. Not before.

---

## Summary

B2B is a powerful density multiplier — but only at the right time, with the right safeguards.

**Do:**
- Activate at 50+ individual subscribers, stable operations
- Grow through warm intros from existing customers
- Cap any single B2B client at 25% of revenue, B2B channel at 30-35% of total
- Keep operations tight: single drop point, fixed orders, monthly invoicing
- Track individual conversions — every B2B employee is a potential personal subscriber

**Don't:**
- Chase B2B before B2C is proven and stable
- Accept a client so large that losing them would cause a revenue crisis
- Negotiate away your margin — the discount comes from delivery efficiency, not product quality
- Let B2B operations degrade B2C experience
- Agree to exclusivity under any circumstances
