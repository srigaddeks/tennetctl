# Go-to-Market Strategy

## The Rule: Zero Paid Advertising. Ever.

No Instagram ads. No Google ads. No performance marketing. No influencer payments. No sponsored posts. No boosted content. Not now, not at 100 customers, not at 1,000.

This is not a cost-saving tactic. This is a brand architecture decision. Soma Delights grows because the product works, the habit sticks, and customers tell people about it. If we cannot grow organically, we have a product problem — not a marketing budget problem.

**Three growth channels. That's it:**

1. **Word of mouth** — driven by booklets, product quality, habit results, and genuine delight
2. **Kiosks** — pop-up sampling at apartment gates, gyms, community events
3. **Referral program** — 10% wallet credit of referred person's spend for 12 months

Everything else is noise until we have 500+ subscribers.

## Growth Milestones

### Milestone 1: First 10 Customers (Week 1-2)

**Source:** Personal network. Friends, family, colleagues in Kukatpally/Miyapur/Chandanagar.

**Tactics:**
- Make a list of 30 people you know in the area. Not people who might buy — people you can hand-deliver to and have a real conversation with.
- WhatsApp each one personally. Not a broadcast. A personal message: "I'm starting something I'm genuinely excited about. Can I bring you a sample tomorrow morning?"
- Hand-deliver every order yourself. Stay for 2 minutes. Explain the ingredients. Leave a premium booklet.
- Ask each person to fill in their wellness goals on page 3 of the booklet. This is not data collection — it's commitment. When they write "I want more energy in the afternoon," they've started the relationship.
- Follow up on Day 3 with a WhatsApp voice note: "Hey, how did the turmeric-ginger feel? Most people notice the afternoon energy shift around day 3-4."
- Do NOT ask for referrals yet. Just deliver consistently and check in.

**Success metric:** 10 people receiving daily deliveries by end of Week 2.

**What you learn:** Which products resonate, what delivery window works, what questions people ask, which objections come up.

---

### Milestone 2: First 50 Customers (Week 3 - Month 2)

**Source:** Apartment kiosk pop-ups (60%), referrals from first 10 (30%), organic WhatsApp spread (10%).

**Tactics:**
- Run your first pop-up kiosk at 2-3 large apartment complexes (My Home Vihanga, Aparna Sarovar, Ramky One Galaxia — these have 500-2,000 units each)
- NOW ask your first 10 customers for referrals. They've been receiving for 2+ weeks. They know the product. Say: "If anyone in your apartment or office would enjoy this, I'd love to send them a trial. And you'll earn 10% of whatever they spend for the next 12 months."
- Start a WhatsApp broadcast list for subscribers. Daily wellness tip at 7 AM (arrives with or just before their delivery).
- Introduce the 3-day trial pack (Rs 350-450) at kiosks — low commitment, easy yes.

**Kiosk execution (see detailed playbook below):**
- Saturday/Sunday mornings, 7-10 AM at apartment gates
- Small table, samples in cups, booklets, sign-up sheet
- Target: 20-30 samples per event, 5-8 trial sign-ups

**Referral program live:**
- Track manually in Google Sheets: who referred whom, monthly spend of referred customer, 10% credit to referrer's wallet
- WhatsApp the referrer monthly: "Your wellness wallet has Rs XXX. Cashable at Rs 500."

**Success metric:** 50 active subscribers by end of Month 2.

---

### Milestone 3: First 100 Customers (Month 2-3)

**Source:** Referral flywheel (30-40%), kiosk pop-ups (30%), gym partnerships (15%), organic word of mouth (15%).

**Tactics:**
- 2 pop-ups per week at different locations (rotate through 6-8 apartment complexes)
- Sign partnership with 2-3 gyms: Snap Fitness Miyapur, Gold's Gym Kukatpally, any local CrossFit box
- Gym deal: free daily supply for the trainer (1 bottle/day) in exchange for display space and trainer recommendations. No revenue share needed — the trainer endorsement is worth more.
- WhatsApp group for subscribers (not broadcast — actual group). Let them talk to each other. This generates organic word of mouth you cannot manufacture.
- Monthly wellness tip booklet — collect ingredient cards into a mini-magazine. Subscribers share these.
- Start Saturday morning "wellness walk" in KP/Miyapur — free, open to anyone, Soma Delights provides post-walk drinks. 10-15 people attending = 3-5 new trials.

**Referral check:** If referrals are generating less than 25% of new customers, the product or onboarding experience needs work. Do NOT compensate by increasing kiosk frequency — fix the root cause.

**Success metric:** 100 active subscribers. 70%+ Month 1 to Month 2 retention. 25%+ customers from referrals.

---

### Milestone 4: First 200 Customers — Fermented Shots Trigger (Month 4-6)

**Source:** Referral flywheel (40%), word of mouth (25%), kiosks (20%), gym/wellness partnerships (15%).

**Tactics:**
- Community events: monthly Saturday morning wellness meetup. Invite a local nutritionist or yoga instructor to speak. Free Soma Delights for attendees. 30-50 people per event.
- Ambassador program: identify 5-10 people who are natural advocates — gym trainers, yoga instructors, apartment complex fitness group leaders. Give them unlimited free product + referral wallet. They become the local face of Soma Delights in their community.
- Multiple apartment clusters now dense: 3-4 complexes with 15+ subscribers each. These clusters generate their own word of mouth.
- Launch fermented shots as an add-on for existing subscribers. Do NOT sell to new customers — reward loyalty.
- Start B2B pilot: approach 2-3 small IT offices (< 50 people) for office wellness supply. 10-20 bottles/day per office at a slight bulk discount (Rs 130/bottle vs Rs 150).

**Success metric:** 200 active subscribers. Referral generating 35-40% of new customers. 3+ dense apartment clusters. Fermented shots launched.

---

### Milestone 5: First 500 Customers — Meal Plan Trigger (Month 8-12)

**Source:** Referral + organic word of mouth generating 60%+ of new customers.

**Tactics:**
- Multi-cluster model proven: 8-10 apartment complexes with 20+ subscribers each
- Brand recognized across the Kukatpally-Miyapur-Chandanagar corridor. People know "the wellness delivery" even if they are not subscribers yet.
- Wellness plans launched: bundled product + education for specific goals (gut health, energy, immunity)
- Permanent kiosk in 1-2 high-traffic locations (gym lobby, apartment clubhouse) — not a pop-up, a small branded display with QR code ordering
- Meal plan pilot with top 20 most engaged subscribers
- Consider Gachibowli-Kondapur-Madhapur as second corridor

**Success metric:** 500 active subscribers. 60%+ acquisition from organic channels. Profitable operations. Ready to expand geography.

## Detailed Playbooks

### Apartment Complex Launch Playbook

This is your primary growth engine for the first 6 months. Get this right.

**Step 1: Permission (1-2 weeks before)**
- Visit the apartment management office or security desk
- Ask for the RWA (Resident Welfare Association) president or secretary's contact
- WhatsApp them: "Hi, I run Soma Delights — a daily wellness habit service based in Kukatpally. We'd love to set up a free wellness tasting at your apartment gate on a Saturday morning. Completely free for residents, we just need a small table space near the gate. Would this be possible?"
- If they hesitate, offer to donate Rs 500-1,000 to the apartment maintenance fund
- Get written/WhatsApp confirmation with date and time
- Ask: "Is there a residents' WhatsApp group where you could share this?" — if yes, draft a message for them to forward

**Step 2: Preparation (3 days before)**
- Prepare 40-50 small sample cups (60ml each) of 2-3 different drinks
- Print 30 booklets (have extras)
- Prepare sign-up sheet: Name, Flat No., WhatsApp number, "Which wellness goal matters most to you?" (checkbox: energy, immunity, digestion, skin, general wellness)
- Prepare 10 trial pack vouchers (3-day trial at Rs 350) — limited creates urgency
- Ice box / insulated bag to keep samples cold
- Small folding table, clean tablecloth, small banner or standee (Rs 500 from a local printer)

**Step 3: Event Day (Saturday/Sunday, 7-10 AM)**
- Arrive 30 minutes early. Set up at the apartment gate or near the walking track.
- Table layout: samples in the center, booklets on one side, sign-up sheet on the other
- Your pitch (30 seconds max): "Good morning! We're Soma Delights — we make fresh cold-pressed wellness drinks and deliver them to your door every morning. Would you like to try today's turmeric-ginger? It's great for inflammation and energy."
- Let the drink do the talking. While they taste, hand them the booklet: "This has some really interesting stuff about how these ingredients work in your body."
- If interested: "We have a 3-day trial — Rs 350, delivered to your flat every morning at 6:30 AM. Want to try it?" Fill in sign-up sheet.
- Collect WhatsApp numbers from EVERYONE who tastes, even if they don't sign up: "Can I add you to our weekly wellness tip list? Just one tip per week, no spam."

**Step 4: Same Day Follow-Up (by 6 PM)**
- WhatsApp every person who signed up for the trial: "Hey [Name]! Great meeting you at [Apartment Name] this morning. Your first delivery arrives tomorrow at [time]. I've included [product] based on your wellness goal. Looking forward to starting this journey with you!"
- WhatsApp everyone who tasted but didn't sign up: "Hey [Name]! Thanks for stopping by our tasting at [Apartment Name] today. Here's a quick read about why daily turmeric makes such a difference: [link to booklet PDF]. If you'd like to try a 3-day trial anytime, just reply here."

**Step 5: Trial Conversion (Day 3-4 of trial)**
- After the 3rd delivery, WhatsApp: "How's the trial going? Most people start noticing the difference around Day 3-4. Would you like to continue with a daily subscription? Rs 150/day, same time every morning. And you'll earn referral credits — 10% of anything a friend you refer spends for a whole year."
- Trial to subscription conversion target: 40-50%

**Step 6: Cluster Building (Ongoing)**
- Once you have 5+ subscribers in an apartment complex, that complex is a "cluster"
- Clusters are self-reinforcing: residents see neighbors getting deliveries, ask about it
- Return for a second pop-up at the same complex 4-6 weeks later. Now existing subscribers are your advocates — some will come down to vouch for you.
- Target: 15-25 subscribers per complex before moving to new ones

### Gym Partnership Playbook

**Identifying the Right Gyms:**
- Local, independently owned gyms or small chains (Snap Fitness, Cult.fit, local CrossFit boxes)
- NOT large corporate gyms (too much bureaucracy, too slow)
- Gyms with 100-300 members, active trainer community, WhatsApp groups for members
- In Kukatpally/Miyapur: Snap Fitness, Gold's Gym, Xtreme Fitness, local yoga studios

**The Approach:**
- Visit the gym during a quiet time (2-4 PM). Ask to speak with the owner or head trainer.
- Bring a sample and a booklet.
- Opening line: "I run a daily wellness drink delivery in the area. I'd love to supply your trainers with a free daily bottle — and set up a small display for your members. No cost to you."

**The Deal:**
- Free daily supply for 2-3 trainers (cost to you: Rs 300-450/day = Rs 9,000-13,500/month)
- Small display/poster in the gym with QR code for ordering
- Trainer mentions Soma Delights to clients who ask about nutrition
- No formal revenue share — keep it simple. The trainer endorsement IS the value.
- Optional: give the gym a referral code. 10% credit on every member who subscribes.

**What You Get:**
- Credibility: "my gym trainer drinks this every day" is powerful social proof
- Captive audience of health-conscious people
- Trainer as an organic ambassador
- Gym WhatsApp group exposure (if the trainer shares)

**What to Avoid:**
- Don't pay gyms for shelf space — destroys your margin and creates a transactional relationship
- Don't do exclusive deals — you want multiple gyms
- Don't sell through the gym (they take a cut, you lose the customer relationship) — always direct WhatsApp ordering

### WhatsApp-First GTM Playbook

WhatsApp is your CRM, your marketing channel, your ordering system, and your feedback loop. All in one app that every customer already uses.

**WhatsApp Business Setup:**
- Business profile: Soma Delights logo, description, catalog with products
- Quick replies for common messages: "How to order," "Today's menu," "Pause my subscription"
- Labels: Trial, Active Subscriber, Paused, Churned, Referrer
- Catalog: all products with photos and prices

**Broadcast Lists (not groups for marketing):**
- "Daily Wellness Tip" — all subscribers, 1 message/day at 7 AM
- "Weekly Update" — all contacts (including non-subscribers), 1 message/week
- "Referral Updates" — active referrers, monthly wallet balance update

**Subscriber WhatsApp Group:**
- Create once you have 20+ subscribers
- Rules: no spam, no promotions, wellness discussion welcome
- Share: customer wins ("I've been on my turmeric routine for 30 days!"), recipe tips, wellness articles
- Founder participates actively — this is not a broadcast, it's a community
- This group generates organic word of mouth as members talk about it with non-member friends

**WhatsApp Status Updates:**
- Post 2-3 status updates daily: 4 AM kitchen prep, fresh bottles ready, delivery route, ingredient close-ups
- Subscribers see these and feel connected to the process
- Non-subscribers see these and get curious

**Voice Notes > Text:**
- For important messages (wellness tips, personal check-ins), use voice notes
- A 30-second voice note from the founder feels 10x more personal than a text message
- "Hey Priya, just wanted to share — the amla we sourced this week is from Anantapur, and it's incredibly fresh. You'll notice a slightly sweeter taste in your ABC juice this week."

### Micro-Influencer Approach (Organic Only)

**The Rule:** No payment. No sponsored posts. No "collaboration" DMs. Ever.

**How It Works:**
- Identify 10-15 fitness/wellness people in the Kukatpally-Miyapur area on Instagram (1,000-10,000 followers). Yoga instructors, gym trainers, runners, people who post about healthy eating.
- Send them product. No strings attached. WhatsApp: "Hey [Name], I follow your fitness journey and I think you'd genuinely enjoy what we make. Can I send you a 3-day trial? No obligations, no post required — just want your honest feedback."
- If they like it, they'll post about it naturally. If they don't, you got feedback.
- If they do post: thank them, offer continued free supply as a subscriber (not payment — they get the product because they genuinely use it)
- NEVER ask them to post. NEVER send a brief. NEVER offer money for content. The moment it becomes transactional, it becomes advertising.

**Why This Works Better Than Paid Influencers:**
- Authentic recommendation vs. paid endorsement — their followers can tell the difference
- Zero cost beyond the product (Rs 450 for a 3-day trial vs. Rs 5,000-50,000 for a paid post)
- Long-term relationship vs. one-time transaction
- They become genuine customers who happen to have an audience

## Top 10 Acquisition Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Don't Do Paid Advertising
Not now. Not ever. The moment you start buying customers, you build dependency on ad spend. Every rupee spent on ads is a rupee not spent on product quality and customer experience. Organic growth is slower but infinitely more durable.

### 2. Don't Go City-Wide
Hyderabad is massive. If you try to serve Kukatpally AND Gachibowli AND Banjara Hills in Month 1, you'll have thin coverage everywhere, terrible delivery economics, and no word-of-mouth density. Own one corridor first. Completely.

### 3. Don't Discount Heavily
No "50% off first month." No "Buy 1 Get 1." No coupon codes. Discounting trains customers to wait for deals and destroys your pricing power. The 3-day trial IS your entry offer. After that, full price. If someone won't pay Rs 150/day for daily wellness, they're not your customer.

### 4. Don't List on Swiggy/Zomato Early
They take 25-35% commission. Your margins can't handle it. More importantly, you lose the customer relationship — Swiggy owns the data, the experience, and the reorder. Use them ONLY for discovery at 200+ subscribers, and always push customers to direct ordering.

### 5. Don't Build an App
You do not need an app at 50 customers. You do not need an app at 200 customers. WhatsApp + a simple website handles everything. An app costs Rs 2-5 lakhs to build, takes months, and nobody will download it until you have serious brand recognition. Build an app at 500+ subscribers, maybe.

### 6. Don't Chase Corporate Accounts Too Early
Corporate wellness pilots sound exciting (volume!) but they're slow to close (2-3 months), require proposals and approvals, want bulk discounts, and can cancel without notice. Focus on individual subscribers first. B2B is a bonus at Month 3+, not a strategy at Month 1.

### 7. Don't Over-Expand Your Menu
4-6 SKUs at launch. Not 12. Not 20. Every new SKU adds ingredient complexity, production time, inventory risk, and decision fatigue for customers. Add new products based on customer requests and operational readiness, not "variety."

### 8. Don't Ignore Delivery Consistency
Acquiring a customer and then delivering late, delivering the wrong product, or missing a delivery is worse than not acquiring them. One bad delivery experience undoes a week of trust-building. Fix delivery ops BEFORE scaling acquisition.

### 9. Don't Copy Juice Bar Marketing
You are not a juice bar. Juice bars sell impulse purchases to walk-in traffic. You sell daily wellness habits to subscribers. Different customer, different psychology, different marketing. Stop looking at RAW Pressery's Instagram for inspiration — look at how milkmen built routes.

### 10. Don't Spend Money Before Spending Time
Every channel above that costs money (pop-ups, gym partnerships, events) should only be activated AFTER you've exhausted the free channels (personal network, WhatsApp, referrals). If you can't get 10 customers from people you know, spending money on pop-ups won't save you.
