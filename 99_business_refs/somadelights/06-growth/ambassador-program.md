# 36 — Ambassador Program

> Ambassadors are not salespeople. They are wellness professionals who genuinely use Soma and recommend it as part of their expertise. The difference between a referral and an ambassador: a customer says "I like this." An ambassador says "I recommend this as part of your wellness routine."

---

## 1. Ambassador vs Customer Referral — Clear Separation

The customer referral program (10% for 12 months) is available to everyone. The ambassador program is invitation-only, reserved for wellness professionals who can credibly recommend Soma as part of their professional advice.

| Aspect | Customer Referral | Ambassador Program |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Who** | Any subscriber | Wellness professionals only |
| **Earn rate** | 10% of referee's purchases | 15% of referee's purchases |
| **Duration** | 12 months per referral | Ongoing (while ambassador is active) |
| **Cash withdrawal minimum** | Rs 500 | Rs 300 |
| **Free product** | No | Yes — monthly supply of all SKUs |
| **Co-branding** | No | Yes — personalized referral page |
| **Booklet distribution** | Get 1 extra to share | Get 20/month to distribute |
| **Minimum referrals** | None | Must bring 5 subscribers in first month |
| **Support** | Self-serve via WhatsApp | Dedicated WhatsApp contact with founder |
| **Reporting** | Basic referral count | Monthly report: referrals, earnings, feedback |
| **Agreement** | Terms acceptance on sign-up | Formal written agreement |
| **Exclusivity** | No | Cannot promote competing juice brands |
| **Reviews required** | No | Monthly check-in, quarterly review |

## 2. Who Are Ambassadors

### 2.1 Primary Ambassador Profiles

**Gym Trainers and Fitness Coaches**
- Why: Their clients trust them on nutrition. A trainer saying "try this cold-pressed juice as your post-workout recovery" carries massive weight.
- Reach: Average gym trainer in Hyderabad works with 30-80 active clients.
- Natural integration: nutrition advice is already part of their service.
- Distribution point: gym reception desk for booklets.

**Yoga Instructors**
- Why: Yoga practitioners are already wellness-oriented. Soma fits naturally into a yogic daily routine.
- Reach: Average yoga class has 10-25 regular students. Instructors with multiple classes reach 50-100.
- Natural integration: "What you consume matters as much as what you practice."
- Distribution point: yoga studio or class gathering area.

**Nutritionists and Dietitians**
- Why: Professional credibility. When a nutritionist includes Soma in a dietary recommendation, it's not a sales pitch — it's medical-adjacent advice.
- Reach: Active nutritionists in Hyderabad see 20-50 clients per month.
- Natural integration: cold-pressed juice as part of a prescribed nutrition plan.
- Distribution point: clinic or consultation room.
- Caution: must not make medical claims. "Supports daily nutrition" not "cures anything."

**Fitness Influencers (Micro, Local Only)**
- Why: Organic reach in Hyderabad fitness community.
- Criteria: Must be local to Hyderabad, genuine fitness practitioner (not just content creator), 1,000-10,000 followers.
- Reach: 1,000-10,000 followers, but high engagement from a local, relevant audience.
- Natural integration: morning routine content, workout nutrition content.
- Caution: no paid promotion feel. Must genuinely use and love the product.

### 2.2 Secondary Ambassador Profiles

**Apartment Wellness Committee Members**
- Why: Direct access to 200-500 families in a single apartment complex.
- Reach: Their recommendation carries community trust.
- Natural integration: wellness events, community notice boards, apartment WhatsApp groups.
- Distribution point: community center, apartment office.

**Corporate HR Wellness Leads**
- Why: Companies with wellness programs can introduce Soma as an employee benefit.
- Reach: 50-500 employees in a single company.
- Natural integration: wellness week events, employee health initiatives.
- Distribution point: office pantry, HR communications.
- Upside: potential for corporate subscription orders (10-50 daily deliveries to one office).

**Ayurvedic Practitioners**
- Why: Soma's ingredient philosophy (turmeric, amla, ashwagandha) aligns with Ayurvedic principles.
- Reach: Established practitioners see 10-30 patients weekly.
- Natural integration: recommending cold-pressed juice as part of Ayurvedic dietary guidance.
- Caution: no Ayurvedic medical claims on Soma packaging or materials. The practitioner can contextualize, but Soma's materials remain simple and non-medical.

## 3. Ambassador Onboarding — Step by Step

### 3.1 Identification (Week 0)

**How we find ambassadors:**
1. **Existing customers first.** The best ambassadors are people who are already Soma subscribers and happen to be wellness professionals. They already love the product.
2. **Community observation.** When doing pop-ups at gyms or apartment complexes, note who shows genuine interest and has professional wellness credentials.
3. **Inbound requests.** Wellness professionals who reach out after hearing about Soma from their clients.
4. **Never cold outreach.** Soma does not approach strangers with ambassador pitches. The relationship must be organic.

**Qualification checklist:**
- [ ] Has been a Soma customer for at least 30 days
- [ ] Has a genuine wellness/fitness professional role
- [ ] Has a relevant audience (clients, students, patients, community members)
- [ ] Aligns with Soma values: habit-focused, education-first, no fads
- [ ] Located in Kukatpally / Miyapur / Chandanagar delivery zone (or their audience is)
- [ ] Is not currently promoting a competing cold-pressed juice brand

### 3.2 First Meeting (Week 1)

**Format:** In-person meeting, 30-45 minutes. At their gym/studio/clinic or at a coffee shop.

**Agenda:**
1. **Listen first** (10 min) — Understand their wellness philosophy, what they recommend to clients, how they approach nutrition.
2. **Explain Soma philosophy** (10 min) — Habit building over quick fixes. Education over selling. Daily wellness over weekend cleanses. No ads, no pushy marketing.
3. **Show the materials** (5 min) — Bottle, booklet, ingredient cards. Let them see the quality.
4. **Explain the program** (10 min) — 15% commission, free monthly supply, booklets for distribution, co-branded page.
5. **Set expectations** (5 min) — This is not MLM. Single-level only. No pressure to sell. Recommend only when genuinely relevant to a client's needs.
6. **Next step** (5 min) — 2-week free trial of full product range. No commitment yet.

**Red flags to watch for:**
- More interested in the commission than the product → decline
- Wants to "sell" Soma rather than recommend it → misaligned
- Promotes fad diets, detox cleanses, or questionable supplements → brand risk
- Asks about multi-level, downline, or team building → not our model

### 3.3 Product Trial (Weeks 2-3)

- Send full SKU range for 2 weeks — all 5-6 flavors, daily delivery.
- No strings attached. Just try it.
- Check in at Day 3 and Day 10: "How's it going? What do you think?"
- If they don't love it: "No worries, it's not for everyone. Stay as a regular customer if you want."
- If they love it: proceed to formal agreement.

### 3.4 Formal Agreement (Week 4)

**Ambassador Agreement covers:**
1. Commission structure: 15% of referred customers' purchases, paid monthly.
2. Free product: monthly supply of all active SKUs (retail value Rs 1,500-2,500).
3. Exclusivity: cannot actively promote competing cold-pressed juice brands during the term.
4. Brand representation: must represent Soma accurately. No health claims, no exaggeration, no medical promises.
5. Materials: 20 booklets/month provided free. Additional booklets available at cost (Rs 40/each).
6. Duration: 6-month initial term, renewable.
7. Termination: either party can end with 30 days notice. Commission stops for new referrals but continues for existing referred customers for 90 days.
8. Review: monthly check-in, quarterly formal review.

**Agreement is a simple 2-page document.** Not a legal fortress — a clear, honest understanding between two people.

### 3.5 Launch Kit (Week 4)

Ambassador receives:

| Item | Quantity | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| Full SKU product supply | 1 month | Personal use + tasting for clients |
| Premium booklets | 20 | Distribution to clients/students |
| Ingredient card full set | 1 complete set | Show to clients |
| Business cards with unique code | 50 | Hand out with referral code |
| Welcome letter from founder | 1 | Personal commitment letter |
| Co-branded referral page URL | 1 | Share digitally |
| WhatsApp broadcast add | Immediate | Receive all wellness tips to reshare |

## 4. Ambassador Economics

### 4.1 Cost to Soma Per Ambassador

| Item | Monthly Cost (INR) | Notes |
|------|-------------------|-------|
| Free product supply (all SKUs) | Rs 1,500-2,000 | At cost, not retail |
| Commission (15% on referred revenue) | Variable | Depends on referral success |
| Booklets (20/month) | Rs 800-1,200 | Print cost |
| Business cards (50/quarter) | Rs 100-150 | Quarterly, amortized monthly = Rs 35-50 |
| Founder time (monthly check-in) | 30 min | Opportunity cost |
| **Fixed monthly cost** | **Rs 2,335-3,250** | Before commission |

### 4.2 Revenue from Ambassador (Conservative Scenario)

**Assumption:** Ambassador brings 5 subscribers in month 1, then 2-3 new subscribers per month ongoing.

| Month | Total Referred Subscribers | Monthly Revenue from Referrals | Commission (15%) | Net Revenue |
|-------|--------------------------|-------------------------------|-----------------|-------------|
| 1 | 5 | Rs 22,500 | Rs 3,375 | Rs 19,125 |
| 2 | 7 | Rs 31,500 | Rs 4,725 | Rs 26,775 |
| 3 | 10 | Rs 45,000 | Rs 6,750 | Rs 38,250 |
| 6 | 18 | Rs 81,000 | Rs 12,150 | Rs 68,850 |
| 12 | 30 | Rs 135,000 | Rs 20,250 | Rs 114,750 |

**Assuming:** Average subscriber spends Rs 4,500/month. 85% retention month-over-month.

### 4.3 Break-Even Analysis

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Fixed monthly cost per ambassador | Rs 2,800 (midpoint) |
| Revenue needed to cover fixed cost | Rs 2,800 / 0.85 (margin) = Rs 3,294 |
| Subscribers needed to cover fixed cost | Rs 3,294 / Rs 4,500 = 0.73 subscribers |
| **Break-even: 1 referred subscriber covers the ambassador cost** | |

Even a minimally effective ambassador who brings just 1-2 subscribers per month is profitable.

### 4.4 Commission Payment Process

1. Referral tracking: unique code per ambassador (e.g., COACH-RAVI, YOGA-PRIYA).
2. Monthly tally: count all active subscribers using that code.
3. Commission calculation: 15% of total purchases by referred subscribers that month.
4. Payment: bank transfer by 10th of following month.
5. Minimum payout: Rs 300 (lower than customer referral minimum).
6. Statement: simple WhatsApp message with breakdown.

**Example monthly statement:**

## 5. Ambassador Playbook — Role-Specific Strategies

### 5.1 Gym Trainer Playbook

**Context:** Gym trainers in Hyderabad charge Rs 2,000-8,000/month for personal training. Their clients already pay for health guidance and trust their nutrition advice.

**How to integrate Soma:**
1. When discussing nutrition plans with clients: "For your morning nutrition, I recommend starting with a cold-pressed green juice. I use Soma Delights myself — spinach, ginger, lemon, fresh every morning."
2. Keep booklets at the gym reception desk with a small sign: "Ask your trainer about morning nutrition."
3. After client completes a workout: "Recovery starts with what you consume. Try Soma's Beet Revival — it's designed for post-workout recovery."
4. Don't push. Mention once. If the client is interested, hand them a booklet and your referral card.

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't make it a sales pitch. "You MUST try this" is wrong. "I use this and recommend it" is right.
- Don't promise fitness results from juice. "This will help you lose weight" is wrong. "This supports your daily nutrition" is right.
- Don't display Soma bottles on the gym floor. Keep it natural and conversational.

**Expected results:** A trainer with 50 active clients can realistically convert 5-10 to Soma subscribers within 2-3 months.

### 5.2 Yoga Instructor Playbook

**Context:** Yoga practitioners value holistic wellness. Nutrition is part of the practice. Soma's philosophy of daily habits aligns perfectly with yoga's emphasis on daily practice (sadhana).

**How to integrate Soma:**
1. Before or after class, when students are relaxed and receptive: "I've been starting my mornings with a cold-pressed juice before practice. It's light enough to not interfere with asanas but nourishing enough to fuel the session."
2. In nutrition discussions: "Ayurveda emphasizes fresh, sattvic food. Cold-pressed juice with turmeric and amla fits that principle perfectly."
3. Place booklets near the yoga mat storage area or sign-in sheet.
4. Host a "Yoga + Wellness" morning session — 45 min yoga followed by Soma tasting.

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't position Soma as an Ayurvedic medicine or treatment.
- Don't claim spiritual benefits. Keep it practical: nutrition, energy, daily habit.
- Don't interrupt the meditative quality of yoga with sales talk. Before or after class only.

**Expected results:** A yoga instructor with 2-3 classes of 15-20 students can convert 5-8 subscribers within 2-3 months.

### 5.3 Nutritionist/Dietitian Playbook

**Context:** Nutritionists prescribe dietary plans. Including a specific product recommendation carries significant weight because clients follow dietary advice precisely.

**How to integrate Soma:**
1. When creating a nutrition plan: "For your morning micro-nutrients, I recommend a cold-pressed juice. Soma Delights in Hyderabad makes them fresh daily — no preservatives, no added sugar."
2. Provide a booklet as part of the consultation materials.
3. For clients with specific needs: "The Golden Balance has turmeric and ginger — both anti-inflammatory, which aligns with what we're trying to achieve in your diet."

**What NOT to do:**
- Never claim Soma treats, cures, or prevents any medical condition.
- Do not position Soma as a meal replacement. It supplements a balanced diet.
- Do not recommend Soma if it conflicts with a client's medical dietary restrictions (e.g., certain ingredients may interact with medications — the nutritionist should use professional judgment).

**Expected results:** A nutritionist seeing 30 clients/month can convert 3-5 to Soma subscribers per month through dietary recommendations.

### 5.4 Apartment Wellness Committee Playbook

**Context:** Large apartment complexes in Kukatpally/Miyapur (Aparna, Rajapushpa, My Home, etc.) have 500-2,000 families. Wellness committees organize health events, yoga sessions, and health talks.

**How to integrate Soma:**
1. Propose a "Wellness Morning" event: 30-min yoga/walk + Soma tasting + health talk.
2. Place booklets in the apartment clubhouse or community library.
3. Include Soma in apartment newsletter or WhatsApp group (with committee approval).
4. Monthly sampling: small table near the apartment gate on a weekend morning.

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't spam the apartment WhatsApp group. One introduction post, then only when relevant.
- Don't set up shop in common areas without management approval.
- Don't position as a sales event. It's a wellness event that happens to include Soma tasting.

**Expected results:** A well-connected committee member can bring 10-20 subscribers from a single large apartment complex within 2-3 months.

### 5.5 Corporate HR Wellness Lead Playbook

**Context:** IT companies and corporate offices in HITEC City / Gachibowli / Madhapur run employee wellness programs. HR wellness leads are always looking for new wellness initiatives.

**How to integrate Soma:**
1. Propose a "Wellness Week" pilot: deliver Soma to 10-20 interested employees for one week.
2. Set up a small tasting station in the office pantry during a wellness event.
3. Offer corporate subscription: company subsidizes or fully covers Soma for employees.
4. Monthly wellness talk: 15-minute session on nutrition (by founder or ambassador) + tasting.

**Corporate pricing:**
| Employees | Per-person/month | Discount from retail |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 10-20 | Rs 4,000 | ~10% discount |
| 20-50 | Rs 3,750 | ~15% discount |
| 50+ | Rs 3,500 | ~20% discount |

**Expected results:** One corporate deal can add 10-50 subscribers in a single conversion.

## 6. Ambassador Limits and Quality Control

### 6.1 Capacity Limits

| Period | Maximum Ambassadors | Reason |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| Month 1-3 | 3-5 | Personal onboarding, learning what works |
| Month 4-6 | 5-10 | Scaling with proven playbook |
| Month 7-12 | 10-20 | Sustainable founder attention |
| Year 2 | 20-30 | May need ambassador manager |

**Why limit:** Each ambassador requires personal attention — monthly check-ins, material supply, commission management, brand alignment monitoring. 20 ambassadors is the maximum one founder can manage while also making juice every morning.

### 6.2 Monthly Check-In Template

Every month, founder has a 15-minute WhatsApp call or in-person meeting with each ambassador:

1. **How are you enjoying the product?** (Genuine — not "are you selling enough?")
2. **Any feedback from your clients/students?** (Flavor, timing, pricing)
3. **How's the booklet distribution going?** (Do they need more? Fewer?)
4. **Any concerns about the brand or product?** (Quality, consistency, delivery)
5. **Referral update:** share numbers, celebrate wins.
6. **Anything you need from us?** (More materials, different flavors, event support)

### 6.3 Quarterly Review

More formal assessment:

| Criterion | Minimum Standard | Action if Below |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Active referred subscribers | 3+ (after first quarter) | Coaching conversation |
| Brand representation | No complaints, no misrepresentation | Warning, then removal |
| Engagement | Responds to check-ins, provides feedback | Check if they want to continue |
| Product usage | Actually consumes Soma regularly | If they stopped drinking it, they can't authentically recommend it |

### 6.4 Grounds for Removal

An ambassador is removed (with 30 days notice) if:
- They make medical/health claims about Soma (e.g., "this cures diabetes")
- They use aggressive sales tactics (pushy WhatsApp messages, spamming)
- They promote a competing cold-pressed juice brand simultaneously
- They stop using the product themselves
- They have zero referrals for 3 consecutive months (after initial quarter)
- Any behavior that damages the Soma brand reputation

**Removal process:**
1. Conversation with founder explaining the concern.
2. 30-day improvement period (if fixable).
3. If not resolved: formal notice, ambassador code deactivated, remaining commission paid out.
4. They remain welcome as a regular customer.

## 7. Ambassador Application Process

### 7.1 How to Apply

Ambassadors don't "apply" in the traditional sense. The process is:

1. **Be a customer first.** Must have been a Soma subscriber for at least 30 days.
2. **Express interest.** Either in conversation with the founder, or by filling a simple Google Form linked in the monthly newsletter.
3. **Screening conversation.** Founder reviews their background and has a personal chat.
4. **Trial period.** 2 weeks of enhanced product supply, see how they naturally recommend.
5. **Formal onboarding.** If it's a fit, sign the agreement and launch.

### 7.2 Application Questions

Simple Google Form:

1. Name and contact
2. How long have you been a Soma subscriber?
3. What is your wellness/fitness profession?
4. How many clients/students do you work with monthly?
5. Where are you located? (Must be in or near delivery zone)
6. Why do you want to be a Soma ambassador? (Open-ended)
7. Do you currently promote any other food/beverage brands? (Transparency check)

### 7.3 Decision Criteria

**Founder approves all ambassadors personally.** This is not delegated. At Soma's scale (first 1-2 years), every ambassador is a direct reflection of the brand.

| Factor | Weight | What we look for |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| Genuine product love | 30% | Do they actually drink Soma daily? Do they talk about it unprompted? |
| Professional credibility | 25% | Are they a legitimate wellness professional? Do clients trust them? |
| Audience relevance | 20% | Are their clients/students in our delivery zone? Would they benefit from Soma? |
| Brand alignment | 15% | Do they share our philosophy? Habits over fads? Education over selling? |
| Communication style | 10% | Are they articulate? Do they represent well? |

## 10. What This Program is NOT

**It is NOT multi-level marketing (MLM).**
- Single level only. Ambassadors earn from their direct referrals. Period.
- No "downline," no "team building," no "levels."
- No recruitment incentives — ambassadors are not rewarded for recruiting other ambassadors.

**It is NOT a sales channel.**
- Ambassadors recommend. They do not sell, invoice, collect payments, or handle delivery.
- All transactions go through Soma directly. The ambassador just provides the referral code.

**It is NOT influencer marketing.**
- No sponsored posts. No paid content. No "post 3 stories per month" requirements.
- If an ambassador wants to post about Soma on social media, great — but it's never required.
- Authentic recommendation only.

**It is NOT a franchise.**
- Ambassadors do not represent Soma at events independently (unless coordinated with founder).
- They do not set prices, offer discounts, or make brand promises.
- Soma's brand is controlled centrally by the founder.
