## 2-Minute Founder Pitch

*Delivered verbally — at an investor meetup, a demo day, or a casual coffee conversation. Memorize the structure, not the words.*

---

Here's a question. How many of us know someone — maybe ourselves — who buys a gym membership in January, goes for two weeks, and then has an expensive key tag for the rest of the year?

The problem isn't motivation. The problem isn't access. The problem is that wellness today requires too much effort, too much time, and too much willpower. The entire industry is designed around dramatic transformations — 30-day challenges, juice cleanses, extreme diets. And they all fail because they fight human nature instead of working with it.

Soma Delights takes the opposite approach. We're a micro-wellness company. Not a juice company — a habit company. We deliver one small, cold-pressed wellness drink to your door every morning, at the same time, like the milkman. Rs 120 a day. No effort required. Just open the door, drink it, and go about your day.

But the drink is the vehicle, not the product. The product is a daily wellness habit. Every bottle comes with education — an ingredient card, a QR code linking to a 60-second wellness tip, a monthly booklet. Over time, our customers don't just consume a drink — they build an identity. "I'm someone who takes care of my health every day."

We launched in Kukatpally, Hyderabad — dense apartment communities, tech professionals, health-curious but time-poor. We have [X] subscribers with [Y]% monthly retention, and [Z]% of our new customers come from word of mouth alone. We've spent exactly zero rupees on advertising.

Our growth engine is density and referral. We concentrate in apartment clusters — 10, 15, 20 customers per complex. Each new customer in the same building makes delivery cheaper. And our referral program gives 10% back for 12 months, creating a network effect where our happiest customers become our sales team.

But drinks are just the start. Our roadmap goes from cold-pressed juices to fruit pulp products, to fermented gut-health shots, to structured 21-day wellness plans, and eventually curated meal plans. Each layer deepens the relationship. Today's juice customer becomes tomorrow's wellness plan subscriber spending Rs 4,000 a month.

We're raising [amount] to transition to a commercial kitchen, expand to 3 neighborhoods, and launch our fermented shots line. With this funding, we'll reach 500 subscribers within 12 months, at which point the business is profitable and self-sustaining.

The India wellness market is growing 15–20% annually. There's no dominant daily wellness brand in Hyderabad — or in India. We're building the category from scratch, one habit at a time.

## Why Micro-Wellness Is a Strong Category

### The Thesis for Investors

**1. Habit-based = high retention = high LTV**

Subscription businesses are only as good as their retention. Most D2C subscriptions (beauty boxes, snack boxes, supplement packs) see 40–50% annual churn. Soma Delights targets < 50% annual churn because:
- The product is consumed daily (not occasionally)
- Delivery is effortless (not a trip to the store)
- Education creates understanding (not just consumption)
- Identity formation makes switching emotionally costly ("I'm someone who does this")

A subscriber who stays 24 months at Rs 2,400/month = Rs 57,600 LTV. At Rs 300 CAC, that's 192x return on acquisition cost.

**2. Zero ad dependency = efficient growth**

Every D2C brand in India is caught in the Meta/Google ads trap — spending Rs 500–2,000 per customer acquisition, with rising costs every year. Soma Delights' growth model has zero ad spend:
- Referral: success-based, only costs money when it works
- Pop-ups: Rs 500–1,000 per event, 3–5 customers per event
- Density: existing customers in a building attract more customers in the same building
- This means growth costs DECREASE over time, not increase

**3. Density model = improving unit economics over time**

Most delivery businesses face a flat or worsening cost curve as they scale. Soma Delights has improving economics:
- 5 customers in one building: Rs 15 delivery cost per customer
- 15 customers in same building: Rs 5 delivery cost per customer
- Each new customer in an existing cluster reduces the per-unit delivery cost for all customers in that cluster
- This creates a natural moat: a competitor trying to enter the same building starts with worse economics

**4. Product expansion roadmap is natural, not forced**

Many startups expand into adjacencies that don't make sense (a mattress company selling dog beds). Soma Delights' expansion path follows the customer relationship:
- Trust the brand with a daily drink → trust it with gut-health shots → trust it with a 21-day wellness plan → trust it with daily meals
- Each expansion uses the same delivery infrastructure, same customer relationship, same brand promise
- ARPU grows from Rs 2,400 to Rs 7,000+ without acquiring a new customer

**5. Subscription + wallet = predictable revenue**

- Weekly/monthly subscriptions create base recurring revenue
- Referral wallet creates lock-in (credits only cashable after Rs 500)
- Wellness plans create commitment and advance payment
- Revenue predictability enables inventory planning and cash flow management

| Dimension | Juice Company | Soma Delights |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|

| **Unit economics at scale** | Flat (distribution costs stay constant) | Improving (density reduces delivery cost) |

## Founder Talking Points for Investor Meetings

When an investor asks "Why should I fund a juice delivery business?", here's how to reframe:

1. **"It's not juice delivery — it's a daily wellness habit platform."** The juice is the vehicle. The habit is the product. The ecosystem is the business.

2. **"Our CAC is Rs 300 and our LTV is Rs 20,000+. Show me another D2C brand with that ratio."** Zero ad spend means every unit of growth is efficient.

3. **"We don't need to convince people to be healthy. We need to make being healthy effortless."** The market demand exists. The supply of easy, daily, affordable wellness does not.

4. **"Our best marketing channel is our existing customers."** 70% of growth from referrals means our customers do the selling. We just need to make them happy.

5. **"Density creates a moat."** Once we have 15 customers in one building, a competitor can't profitably enter that building. We own the cluster.

6. **"The roadmap from Rs 2,400 to Rs 7,000 ARPU doesn't require acquiring new customers."** Product expansion deepens existing relationships. Revenue grows faster than customer count.
